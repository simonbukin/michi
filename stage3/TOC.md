# 日本語の道 — Stage 3 (N3): Table of Contents

## Scope and Targets

**Prerequisite:** Stages 1-2 complete (~1,500 vocab, ~300 kanji, full verb paradigm, basic keigo, conditionals, passive/causative)
**Duration:** ~200 hours | **Kanji:** ~650 cumulative | **Vocabulary:** ~3,500 cumulative
**New in this stage:** は/が full treatment, compound particles, sentence-ending modality (わけ/もの/こと), aspect as a system, nominalization, formal written style (である), colloquial grammar for recognition, aizuchi deep dive, discourse connectors, first exposure to vertical text

This is the stage where the learner transitions from "textbook Japanese" to "real-world Japanese." Reading materials shift from constructed passages to lightly adapted authentic texts. Listening targets shift from clear textbook audio to near-natural speech.

---

## Front Matter

- **Stage 3 Introduction** — The N3 plateau: what changes, why it's hard, what to expect. Reading passages are now longer (500-800 characters). Two registers (formal written, casual spoken) are taught in parallel. Introduction of extensive reading/listening as a parallel track.
- **What You Should Already Know** — Checklist of Stage 2 prerequisites (full verb conjugation paradigm, four conditional forms, keigo recognition, て-form giving/receiving, aspect basics)

## Part One: The は/が Distinction — Full Treatment

**Chapter 1 — は: The Topic Marker in Depth**
1.1 Review: は as "as for X..." — frame-setting for the comment that follows
1.2 Contrastive は (対比のは): コーヒーは飲むが、紅茶は飲まない — は signals "this one, as opposed to others"
1.3 は for known/old information — the topic is what speaker assumes listener already knows
1.4 は in negative sentences — tendency for negated elements to take は (水は飲まなかった vs 水を飲まなかった — the は version implies "water specifically, I didn't drink")
1.5 Multiple は in one sentence — each sets a different frame: 日本では夏は暑い (in Japan, as for summer, it's hot)
1.6 は with other particles: には, では, からは, までは — は layers on top
1.7 Reading passage — contrastive text comparing two cities/seasons

**Chapter 2 — が: The Subject Marker in Depth**
2.1 Review: が for new information — announcing something the listener doesn't know
2.2 Exhaustive-listing が: 田中さんが来ました (Tanaka is the one who came — not someone else)
2.3 が with specific predicates: 好き, 嫌い, 欲しい, 分かる, できる, ある, いる, 見える, 聞こえる, 要る, 上手, 下手
2.4 が inside subordinate clauses — relative clauses, 時/前に/後で clauses typically use が, not は (the subordinate clause has its own subject)
2.5 が as "but" at clause boundary — review and clarification that this is a different が

**Chapter 3 — は vs が: Contrastive Analysis**
3.1 The information-structure framework: は = old/known, が = new/unknown (with caveats)
3.2 The same sentence with は vs が — how meaning shifts: 猫はいる vs 猫がいる
3.3 Question-answer pairs: が in questions maps to が in answers for the new-info element (誰が来た？→田中さんが来た)
3.4 Thematic は across paragraphs — how は chains to build coherent discourse
3.5 The places where the rules break or blur — acknowledging that native speakers disagree on some cases
3.6 Extended reading passage — analyze は/が choices in a real text
3.7 Vocabulary list

## Part Three: Sentence-Ending Modality

**Chapter 7 — わけ: Logical Conclusion and Denial**
7.1 わけだ — "it means that, so that's why" (logical conclusion from known facts): つまり、彼は来ないわけだ
7.2 わけがない — "there's no way that": そんなことができるわけがない
7.3 わけにはいかない — "can't very well do, it's not acceptable to": 約束したから、行かないわけにはいかない
7.4 わけではない — "it's not that" (partial denial): 嫌いなわけではない "it's not that I dislike it"
7.5 というわけだ — "that's the reason why"
7.6 Reading passage illustrating all わけ forms in context
7.7 Vocabulary list

**Chapter 8 — もの: General Truths, Nostalgia, Obligation**
8.1 ものだ (general truth / common sense): 人間は誰でも間違えるものだ "everyone makes mistakes"
8.2 ものだ (nostalgia): 子どもの頃、よくこの川で遊んだものだ "I used to play in this river as a child"
8.3 ものだ (mild obligation / "ought to"): 約束は守るものだ "one ought to keep promises"
8.4 ものか — emphatic denial: そんなこと信じるものか "as if I'd believe that"
8.5 ものの — "although" (concessive, literary): 努力したものの結果は出なかった
8.6 ものだから / もので — "because" (with nuance of excuse): 急いでいたものだから "because I was in a hurry..."
8.7 Vocabulary list

**Chapter 9 — こと: Advice, Emphasis, and Nominalization Review**
9.1 ことだ (advice): もっと早く寝ることだ "you should go to bed earlier"
9.2 ことはない — "there's no need to": 心配することはない
9.3 ことに(は) — "to my [emotion]": 驚いたことに "to my surprise"
9.4 Nominalization review: こと vs の — functional distinction after specific predicates; の for direct perception, こと for abstract reference
9.5 Vocabulary list

**Chapter 10 — Additional Modality: べき, はず, つもり (Expanded)**
10.1 べきだ — "ought to, should" (stronger than ほうがいい): 学生は勉強すべきだ; negative: べきではない
10.2 べき — prenominal: 注目すべき問題 "a problem worth noting"
10.3 はずだ — review and expansion: はずがない "there's no way" (≈ わけがない), はずだった "was supposed to" (expectation failed)
10.4 つもり — review and expansion: つもりだった "I had intended to" (but didn't); そんなつもりはなかった "I didn't mean to"
10.5 Vocabulary list

**Chapter 12 — Advanced Predicate Endings**
12.1 ～ざるを得ない — "have no choice but to" (classical ざる = ない): 認めざるを得ない
12.2 ～かねる — "find it difficult to, cannot bring oneself to": お答えしかねます (polite refusal)
12.3 ～かねない — "might well, there's a risk of": 事故になりかねない

12.6 ～がち — "tend to, prone to" (negative connotation): 忘れがち, 遅れがち
12.7 ～気味 — "slight tendency, touch of": 風邪気味, 太り気味
12.8 ～得る (うる/える) — "possible, can": あり得る "possible," あり得ない "impossible"
