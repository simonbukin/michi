# Appendix E — Sentence-Ending Modality Quick Reference

This appendix consolidates all sentence-ending modality expressions taught in Stage 3: わけ, もの, こと, べき, and はず. Each form is listed with its meaning, formation, an example, and notes on usage.

---

## わけ — Logical Reasoning and Denial

わけ encodes the speaker's logical reasoning about a situation. It connects facts to conclusions, or denies that a particular conclusion follows.

| Form | Formation | Meaning | Example | Notes |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| わけだ | Plain form + わけだ | It means that; so that's why | 三年も住んでいるのだから、詳しいわけだ。 | Draws a logical conclusion from known facts. Often preceded by だから or つまり. |
| わけがない | Plain form + わけがない | There's no way that | そんなことができるわけがない。 | Strong denial of possibility. Stronger than はずがない. |
| わけにはいかない | 辞書形 + わけにはいかない | Can't very well do; it's not acceptable to | 約束したから、行かないわけにはいかない。 | Social/moral constraint prevents the action. Not about ability but about acceptability. |
| わけではない | Plain form + わけではない | It's not that; it doesn't mean that | 嫌いなわけではない。 | Partial denial. "It's not that I dislike it" — leaves room for other explanations. |
| というわけだ | Clause + というわけだ | That's the reason why | そういうわけで、今日は来られなかった。 | Wraps up an explanation. Often used after presenting evidence. |
| というわけではない | Clause + というわけではない | It doesn't mean that | 安いからといって品質が悪いというわけではない。 | Denies that a stated reason leads to a stated conclusion. |

### Common Mistakes

- Using わけだ for personal decisions or intentions. わけ is about logical deduction, not personal will. ("I decided to go" is NOT わけだ territory.)
- Confusing わけがない (no logical possibility) with わけにはいかない (social/moral impossibility). わけがない = "impossible"; わけにはいかない = "I mustn't."

## もの — General Truths, Memory, and Justification

もの anchors statements in shared human experience, past habits, or social expectations.

| Form | Formation | Meaning | Example | Notes |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| ものだ (general truth) | Plain nonpast + ものだ | It's the way things are; it's natural that | 人間は誰でも間違えるものだ。 | States a universal or widely accepted truth. |
| ものだ (nostalgia) | た form + ものだ | Used to; would often (nostalgic) | 子どもの頃、よくこの公園で遊んだものだ。 | Always past tense. Conveys warm recollection. |
| ものだ (obligation) | 辞書形 + ものだ | One ought to; you should | 約束は守るものだ。 | Mild obligation based on social norm, not personal opinion. Compare べきだ (stronger). |
| ものか | Plain form + ものか | As if; no way (emphatic denial) | そんなこと信じるものか。 | Strong emotional denial. Masculine/rough register. ものですか is slightly softer. |
| ものの | Plain form + ものの | Although (literary concessive) | 努力したものの、結果は出なかった。 | Written/literary. Close to けれども but more literary. |
| ものだから | Plain form + ものだから | Because (with excuse nuance) | 急いでいたものだから、つい忘れてしまった。 | The speaker presents a reason as an excuse or justification. Often implies "it's not really my fault." |
| もので | Plain form + もので | Because (with excuse nuance, slightly softer) | 電車が遅れたもので、遅刻しました。 | Softer variant of ものだから. Common in polite speech. |

### Common Mistakes

- Confusing nostalgic ものだ (past tense) with general-truth ものだ (present tense). The tense of the verb before ものだ determines the meaning.
- Using ものだから when there is no excuse nuance. For neutral "because," use ので or から.

## こと — Advice, Necessity, and Emotional Commentary

こと in sentence-ending position gives advice, denies necessity, or frames an emotional reaction.

| Form | Formation | Meaning | Example | Notes |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| ことだ (advice) | 辞書形 + ことだ | You should; the thing to do is | もっと早く寝ることだ。 | General advice, often from someone experienced. Less forceful than べきだ. |
| ことだ (negative advice) | ない形 + ことだ | You shouldn't; the thing to do is not | あまり気にしないことだ。 | Same structure, negative verb. "You shouldn't worry about it." |
| ことはない | 辞書形 + ことはない | There's no need to | 心配することはない。 | Reassuring. "There's no need to worry." |
| ことに(は) | た form / い-adj + ことに | To my [emotion]; [emotion]-ly | 驚いたことに、彼はもう辞めていた。 | The emotion word comes first: 嬉しいことに, 残念なことに, 困ったことに. |
| ことになる | 辞書形 + ことになる | It has been decided that; it turns out that | 来月、転勤することになった。 | Review from Stage 2. The decision is external or presented as such. |
| ことにする | 辞書形 + ことにする | Decide to | 今日は早く帰ることにした。 | Review from Stage 2. Personal decision. |
| ことになっている | 辞書形 + ことになっている | It is the rule/custom that | ここでは靴を脱ぐことになっている。 | Review from Stage 2. Established rule or custom. |

### こと vs の (Nominalization Review)

| Use こと when... | Use の when... |
|-----------------|---------------|
| The clause is abstract or general: 泳ぐことが好きだ | The clause describes a directly perceived action: 子どもが泳ぐのを見た |
| After certain predicates: ことがある, ことにする, ことができる | After perception verbs: 見る, 聞く, 感じる |
| In formal/written contexts | In casual/spoken contexts (の is often preferred) |

## べき — Strong Obligation and Moral Judgment

べき expresses what ought to be done based on moral judgment, social expectation, or logical necessity. It is stronger than ほうがいい and ものだ.

| Form | Formation | Meaning | Example | Notes |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| べきだ | 辞書形 + べきだ | Ought to, should (strong) | 学生は勉強すべきだ。 | する → すべきだ (classical contraction). Strong moral/logical obligation. |
| べきではない | 辞書形 + べきではない | Ought not to | そんなことを言うべきではない。 | Strong prohibition based on principle. |
| べき + Noun | 辞書形 + べき + Noun | Worth doing; should be [verb]ed | 注目すべき問題が見つかった。 | Prenominal use. Very common in written Japanese. |
| べきだった | 辞書形 + べきだった | Should have (regret) | もっと早く相談すべきだった。 | Expresses regret about past inaction. |

### Strength Comparison

| Expression | Strength | Nuance |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| ほうがいい | Mild | Friendly advice, personal suggestion |
| ことだ | Moderate | General wisdom, experienced advice |
| ものだ (obligation) | Moderate | Social norm, common sense |
| べきだ | Strong | Moral/logical imperative |
| なければならない | Strong | Objective necessity/rule |

## はず — Expectation and Assumed Certainty

はず expresses what the speaker believes should be true based on evidence, logic, or prior information.

| Form | Formation | Meaning | Example | Notes |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| はずだ | Plain form + はずだ | Should be; expected to | 届くはずだ。 | The speaker has reason to believe this. Not a guess — based on evidence. |
| はずがない | Plain form + はずがない | There's no way; can't be | 彼がそんなことをするはずがない。 | The speaker's evidence/knowledge rules it out. Slightly softer than わけがない. |
| はずだった | Plain form + はずだった | Was supposed to (but wasn't) | 届くはずだったが、届かなかった。 | Expectation was not met. Implies disappointment or confusion. |
| はずなのに | Plain form + はずなのに | It should be X, and yet... | 届くはずなのに、まだ届いていない。 | Expectation contradicted by reality. のに adds frustration. |

### はず vs わけ

| はず | わけ |
|------|------|
| Based on the speaker's belief/evidence | Based on logical deduction from stated facts |
| "It should be the case" | "It follows logically that" |
| Can be wrong (はずだった = "was supposed to") | Presents the conclusion as airtight |
| はずがない = "shouldn't be possible (I believe)" | わけがない = "logically impossible" |

### はず vs だろう

| はず | だろう |
|------|--------|
| Evidence-based expectation | Probability/guess |
| "It should be" (I have reason to think so) | "It probably is" (I think so) |
| Stronger certainty | Weaker certainty |
