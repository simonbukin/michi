# Chapter 21 — Technology and Daily Life

Japan's relationship with technology is a study in contrasts. The country that pioneered the bullet train, the Walkman, and the QR code is also the country where fax machines remain in active use and where some government offices still require 判子（はんこ）-- personal seals -- instead of digital signatures. Understanding technology vocabulary in Japanese is not merely about naming gadgets; it is about understanding how technology shapes social behavior, creates new social problems, and generates public debate.

This chapter introduces the vocabulary of modern technology in Japanese daily life and then turns to technology as a social issue -- a topic that appears frequently in N3-level reading materials. Grammar from earlier chapters, particularly formal written style, opinion-expressing patterns, and discourse connectors, appears throughout.

## 21.1 Technology Vocabulary

### Devices and Services

Many technology terms in Japanese are borrowed from English and written in katakana. However, some have been abbreviated or modified in ways that are not immediately transparent to English speakers.

| Japanese | Origin / Full form | Meaning |
|----------|--------------------|---------|
| スマホ | スマートフォン | smartphone |
| パソコン | パーソナルコンピューター | personal computer |
| タブレット | tablet | tablet device |
| ノートパソコン | note + personal computer | laptop |
| ガラケー | ガラパゴス携帯 | feature phone (non-smart) |
| アプリ | アプリケーション | app / application |
| ネット | インターネット | internet |
| SNS | ソーシャル・ネットワーキング・サービス | social media |
| Wi-Fi | Wi-Fi | Wi-Fi |
| AI | 人工知能（じんこうちのう） | artificial intelligence |
| IoT | アイオーティー | Internet of Things |

The term ガラケー deserves explanation. ガラパゴス携帯（がらぱごすけいたい）refers to older Japanese feature phones that evolved with many advanced features (mobile payments, TV tuners, email) but were incompatible with global standards -- like the unique species of the Galapagos Islands. The term is now used nostalgically or to distinguish non-smartphones.

> スマホが普及する以前は、ガラケーが日本の携帯電話市場を支配していた。

> "Before smartphones became widespread, feature phones dominated the Japanese mobile phone market."

### Online Activities

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 検索（する） | けんさく | searching (online) |
| ダウンロード（する） | ダウンロード | downloading |
| アップロード（する） | アップロード | uploading |
| ログイン（する） | ログイン | logging in |
| ログアウト（する） | ログアウト | logging out |
| 課金（する） | かきん | in-app purchase; charging money |
| 投稿（する） | とうこう | posting (on social media) |
| フォロー（する） | フォロー | following (on social media) |
| 炎上（する） | えんじょう | going viral in a negative way; online firestorm |
| バズる | バズる | going viral (positive or neutral) |
| 拡散（する） | かくさん | spreading / sharing (information) |

The distinction between 炎上 and バズる is culturally important. 炎上 literally means "going up in flames" and refers specifically to a backlash -- when a post or action provokes mass criticism online. バズる (from English "buzz") can be positive or neutral.

> 有名人の不用意な発言がSNSで炎上し、謝罪に追い込まれるケースが増えている。

> "Cases where careless remarks by celebrities cause online firestorms on social media, forcing them into apologies, are increasing."

### Digital Payments and Convenience

Japan's adoption of cashless payment (キャッシュレス決済, キャッシュレスけっさい) has accelerated in recent years. Common terms:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 電子マネー | でんしマネー | electronic money |
| QRコード決済 | キューアールコードけっさい | QR code payment |
| キャッシュレス | キャッシュレス | cashless |
| ポイント | ポイント | reward points |
| チャージ（する） | チャージ | charging / adding money |
| 交通系ICカード | こうつうけいアイシーカード | transit IC card (Suica, PASMO, etc.) |

> 交通系ICカードは電車やバスだけでなく、コンビニでの支払いにも使える。

> "Transit IC cards can be used not only for trains and buses, but also for payments at convenience stores."

The pattern だけでなく...にも (review from Chapter 6, のみならず) expands the scope from one use to another.

## 21.2 Technology as a Social Issue

### スマホ依存 -- Smartphone Dependence

The term スマホ依存（スマホいぞん）refers to excessive reliance on smartphones -- a topic of increasing concern in Japanese media, particularly regarding young people.

> スマホ依存とは、スマホなしでは不安を感じ、日常生活に支障をきたす状態のことを指す。

> "Smartphone dependence refers to a state in which one feels anxious without a smartphone and it interferes with daily life."

Key related vocabulary:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 依存 | いぞん | dependence / addiction |
| 依存症 | いぞんしょう | dependency disorder |
| 画面 | がめん | screen |
| 使用時間 | しようじかん | usage time |
| 睡眠不足 | すいみんぶそく | lack of sleep |
| 視力低下 | しりょくていか | declining eyesight |
| 歩きスマホ | あるきスマホ | walking while using smartphone |

歩きスマホ is a distinctly Japanese compound. Warning signs against it appear in train stations and on sidewalks across Japan.

> 歩きスマホは事故の原因になり得るため、多くの自治体が注意を呼びかけている。

> "Because walking while using a smartphone can become a cause of accidents, many local governments are calling for caution."

The pattern ～得る（うる） (Chapter 12) expresses possibility -- "can become."

### ネットいじめ -- Cyberbullying

As students spend more time online, ネットいじめ (cyberbullying) has become a serious issue in Japanese schools. Unlike traditional bullying, it can happen anonymously and around the clock.

> ネットいじめの特徴として、加害者が匿名であること、そして被害が24時間続き得ることが挙げられる。

> "As characteristics of cyberbullying, the fact that perpetrators are anonymous and that harm can continue 24 hours a day can be cited."

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| ネットいじめ | ネットいじめ | cyberbullying |
| 匿名 | とくめい | anonymity |
| 加害者 | かがいしゃ | perpetrator |
| 被害者 | ひがいしゃ | victim |
| 誹謗中傷 | ひぼうちゅうしょう | slander and defamation |
| 書き込み | かきこみ | online post / comment |
| 削除（する） | さくじょ | deletion |
| 通報（する） | つうほう | reporting (to authorities / platform) |

> 誹謗中傷に対しては、法的措置が取られるケースも増えてきている。

> "Cases where legal measures are taken against slander and defamation have also been increasing."

### 個人情報 -- Personal Information

Privacy and personal information protection (個人情報保護, こじんじょうほうほご) is a growing concern. Japan enacted the 個人情報保護法（こじんじょうほうほごほう）(Act on the Protection of Personal Information), which regulates how businesses collect and use personal data.

> 個人情報の取り扱いについては、十分な注意が必要である。

> "Regarding the handling of personal information, sufficient caution is necessary."

The compound particle について (Chapter 4) marks the topic of concern.

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 個人情報 | こじんじょうほう | personal information |
| 情報漏洩 | じょうほうろうえい | information leak |
| パスワード | パスワード | password |
| 二段階認証 | にだんかいにんしょう | two-factor authentication |
| フィッシング詐欺 | フィッシングさぎ | phishing scam |
| 不正アクセス | ふせいアクセス | unauthorized access |

> パスワードを定期的に変更することはもちろん、二段階認証を設定すべきである。

> "Not only should you change your password regularly, you should also set up two-factor authentication."

### AI and Society

The rapid development of AI (人工知能) has generated discussion in Japan, as elsewhere, about its impact on employment and daily life.

> AIの発展に伴い、なくなる仕事もあれば、新しく生まれる仕事もあるだろう。

> "Along with the development of AI, while some jobs will disappear, new jobs will likely be born as well."

The pattern に伴い（にともない）means "accompanying, along with" and is used in formal writing to express simultaneous change. The ～ば...も pattern presents balanced alternatives.

> AIに仕事を奪われるのではないかという不安を抱える人も多いが、AIを道具として活用する能力が今後ますます求められるわけだ。

> "Many people harbor anxiety about whether AI might take their jobs, but the ability to utilize AI as a tool will be increasingly demanded going forward -- that's the logical conclusion."

The ending わけだ (Chapter 7) presents this as a logical conclusion drawn from the preceding discussion.

## 21.3 Reading Passage -- Technology's Impact

### テクノロジーと私たちの生活

スマートフォンが普及して以来、私たちの生活は大きく変わった。電車の中を見渡せば、ほとんどの乗客がスマホの画面を見ていることに気づくだろう。ニュースを読む人、SNSに投稿する人、動画を見る人、ゲームをする人。スマホは今や生活に欠かせないものとなっている。

しかし、その便利さの裏側には問題もある。スマホ依存が社会問題として取り上げられるようになり、特に若者の長時間使用が懸念されている。また、SNSにおける誹謗中傷やネットいじめも深刻化している。匿名で簡単に書き込みができるため、言葉の暴力が広がりやすいのである。

一方で、テクノロジーは社会課題の解決にも貢献し得る。AIを活用した医療診断や、IoTによる高齢者の見守りサービスなど、技術の恩恵を受けている分野も多い。大切なのは、テクノロジーに振り回されるのではなく、上手に付き合っていくことではないだろうか。

### English Translation

**Technology and Our Lives**

Since smartphones became widespread, our lives have changed greatly. If you look around inside a train, you will likely notice that almost all passengers are looking at smartphone screens. People reading news, people posting on social media, people watching videos, people playing games. Smartphones have now become indispensable to daily life.

However, behind that convenience there are also problems. Smartphone dependence has come to be taken up as a social issue, and particularly the long usage hours of young people are a source of concern. Additionally, slander and cyberbullying on social media are also becoming more serious. Because anyone can easily post anonymously, verbal violence tends to spread easily.

On the other hand, technology can also contribute to solving social problems. There are many fields benefiting from technological advances, such as AI-powered medical diagnosis and IoT-based elderly monitoring services. What is important is not being pushed around by technology, but rather getting along with it skillfully -- is it not?
