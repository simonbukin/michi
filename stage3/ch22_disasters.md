# Chapter 22 — Environment and Natural Disasters

Japan sits at the intersection of four tectonic plates, in the path of Pacific typhoons, and along the volcanic Ring of Fire. Natural disasters are not abstract possibilities -- they are recurring realities that shape architecture, policy, education, and daily habits. Every resident of Japan participates in disaster drills, keeps emergency supplies, and knows the sound of the 緊急地震速報. Understanding disaster-related vocabulary is not just academically useful; it is a matter of safety.

This chapter covers three interconnected domains: natural disaster vocabulary, disaster preparedness (防災), and environmental issues. These topics appear frequently in N3-level reading materials -- newspaper articles, public notices, and opinion essays -- and they naturally employ the formal written style, compound particles, and modality patterns studied earlier in Stage 3.

### Earthquake-Specific Terms

Because earthquakes are the most frequent and most feared disaster in Japan, the vocabulary around them is especially rich:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 地震が起きる | じしんがおきる | an earthquake occurs |
| 地震が発生する | じしんがはっせいする | an earthquake occurs (formal) |
| 揺れ | ゆれ | shaking |
| 揺れる | ゆれる | to shake (一段) |
| 震度〇 | しんど〇 | seismic intensity ○ |
| 直下型地震 | ちょっかがたじしん | inland/direct-hit earthquake |
| 海溝型地震 | かいこうがたじしん | trench-type earthquake |
| 液状化 | えきじょうか | liquefaction |
| 倒壊（する） | とうかい | collapse (of a building) |
| 耐震 | たいしん | earthquake-resistant |

Japan uses its own seismic intensity scale (震度), which measures the shaking felt at a specific location, distinct from magnitude (マグニチュード), which measures the energy released at the source. The scale runs from 震度0 (not felt) to 震度7 (catastrophic). Levels 5 and 6 are each subdivided into 弱（じゃく, lower）and 強（きょう, upper）.

> 震度とマグニチュードは異なる指標である。震度は観測地点での揺れの強さを、マグニチュードは地震そのもののエネルギーの大きさを示す。

> "Seismic intensity and magnitude are different indicators. Intensity shows the strength of shaking at the observation point, while magnitude indicates the size of the earthquake's energy itself."

## 22.2 防災 and 避難 -- Preparedness and Evacuation

### 防災 -- Disaster Preparedness

防災（ぼうさい）literally means "preventing disaster," but in practice it refers to the full range of preparedness activities: drills, supplies, planning, and public education.

> 日本では、防災に関する意識が他国と比べて高い傾向がある。

> "In Japan, awareness regarding disaster preparedness tends to be higher compared to other countries."

The compound particle に関する (Chapter 4) modifies the noun 意識 directly, and 傾向がある (Chapter 14) marks an observed tendency.

### Key 防災 Terms

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 防災 | ぼうさい | disaster preparedness |
| 防災訓練 | ぼうさいくんれん | disaster drill |
| 防災グッズ | ぼうさいグッズ | emergency supplies |
| 避難 | ひなん | evacuation |
| 避難所 | ひなんじょ | evacuation shelter |
| 避難場所 | ひなんばしょ | evacuation site (open area) |
| 避難経路 | ひなんけいろ | evacuation route |
| 非常口 | ひじょうぐち | emergency exit |
| 非常食 | ひじょうしょく | emergency food |
| 非常持ち出し袋 | ひじょうもちだしぶくろ | emergency grab bag |
| 緊急地震速報 | きんきゅうじしんそくほう | earthquake early warning |
| 安否確認 | あんぴかくにん | safety confirmation |
| 災害伝言ダイヤル | さいがいでんごんダイヤル | disaster message dial (171) |

Note the distinction between 避難所 and 避難場所. An 避難場所 is typically an open space (park, schoolyard) where people gather immediately after a disaster to escape danger. An 避難所 is an indoor shelter (school gymnasium, community center) where people stay for extended periods if they cannot return home.

> 自宅から最も近い避難所がどこにあるか、日頃から確認しておくべきである。

> "You should check in advance where the nearest evacuation shelter from your home is located, on a regular basis."

The pattern ておく (Chapter 11, "do in preparation") combines with べきである (Chapter 10 + Chapter 14) for a strong formal recommendation.

### 緊急地震速報 -- Earthquake Early Warning

The 緊急地震速報（きんきゅうじしんそくほう）is a system that sends alerts to mobile phones, televisions, and public speakers seconds before strong shaking arrives. The distinctive alarm sound is immediately recognizable to anyone who has lived in Japan.

> 緊急地震速報が鳴ったら、すぐにテーブルの下に隠れるなど、身を守る行動を取るべきだ。

> "When the earthquake early warning sounds, you should take protective action, such as immediately hiding under a table."

### Disaster Drills

Disaster drills (防災訓練) are conducted in schools, workplaces, and communities, often on September 1st -- 防災の日（ぼうさいのひ）-- which commemorates the 1923 Great Kanto Earthquake.

> 防災の日に合わせて、全国各地で防災訓練が行われる。

> "In line with Disaster Prevention Day, disaster drills are conducted throughout the country."

The pattern ～に合わせて means "in time with, to match."

### Energy After 3/11

The 2011 earthquake, tsunami, and nuclear accident at Fukushima changed Japan's energy debate fundamentally.

> 東日本大震災以降、原子力発電の安全性をめぐる議論が活発になった。

> "Since the Great East Japan Earthquake, debate surrounding the safety of nuclear power generation has become active."

The compound particle をめぐる (Chapter 4) marks a topic of controversy or debate.

> 再生可能エネルギーの導入が進む一方で、コストや安定供給の課題も残っている。

> "While the adoption of renewable energy is advancing, challenges of cost and stable supply also remain."

The connector 一方で (Chapter 13) presents a simultaneous contrasting reality.

## 22.4 Reading Passage -- Disaster Preparedness and Environmental Awareness

### 災害に備える

日本は地震、台風、洪水など、さまざまな自然災害が発生する国である。特に地震は予測が難しく、いつどこで大きな地震が起きてもおかしくない。そのため、日頃からの備えが不可欠である。

まず、自宅の近くにある避難所を確認しておくことが重要だ。家族と連絡が取れなくなった場合に備えて、集合場所を決めておくべきである。また、水や非常食、懐中電灯などの防災グッズを準備し、定期的に中身を確認することも欠かせない。

近年では、自然災害に加えて環境問題への関心も高まっている。地球温暖化の影響で、台風の大型化や猛暑日の増加が指摘されている。つまり、防災と環境問題は切り離せない関係にあるわけだ。一人ひとりが日常生活の中でエネルギーの使い方を見直し、同時に災害への備えを怠らないことが、これからの社会において求められている。

### English Translation

**Preparing for Disasters**

Japan is a country where various natural disasters occur, including earthquakes, typhoons, and floods. Earthquakes in particular are difficult to predict, and it would not be strange for a large earthquake to occur anywhere at any time. For that reason, everyday preparedness is indispensable.

First, it is important to check where the evacuation shelter near your home is. In preparation for the possibility of losing contact with family, you should decide on a meeting place. Additionally, preparing emergency supplies such as water, emergency food, and flashlights, and regularly checking their contents, is also essential.

In recent years, in addition to natural disasters, interest in environmental problems has also been growing. Due to the effects of global warming, the increasing size of typhoons and the increase in extreme-heat days have been pointed out. In other words, disaster preparedness and environmental issues are in a relationship that cannot be separated -- that is the logical conclusion. What is being called for in society going forward is that each individual reconsider their energy usage in daily life and at the same time not neglect preparedness for disasters.
