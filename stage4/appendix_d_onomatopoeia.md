# Appendix D — Onomatopoeia Reference

This appendix lists onomatopoeia introduced in Stage 4 of 日本語の道, organized by domain. Use it as a quick-reference when reading, writing, or studying.

Japanese onomatopoeia (オノマトペ) fall into three categories:

- **擬音語 (ぎおんご)** — Words that imitate actual sounds (rain falling, doors slamming, dogs barking).
- **擬態語 (ぎたいご)** — Words that describe states, textures, manners of action, or visual appearances without representing sound (smoothness, sparkle, sluggishness).
- **擬情語 (ぎじょうご)** — Words that describe internal emotional or psychological states (excitement, irritation, relief).

Onomatopoeia combine with grammar in four main ways:

| Pattern | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ～と + verb | Adverb (modifies action) | にこにこと笑う |
| ～する | Verb (describes state/action) | どきどきする |
| ～な / ～の + noun | Adjectival modifier | ふわふわな毛布 / ぴかぴかの靴 |
| Standalone | Independent expression | ばたん。しーん。 |

In the tables below, the **Grammar** column indicates which patterns each word commonly takes: **と** (adverbial), **する** (verbal), **な/の** (adjectival). Most onomatopoeia accept と; the column notes the most natural patterns.

## Sound Symbolism Quick Reference

The following patterns help predict meaning from form. They are tendencies, not absolute rules.

### Voicing (濁点) adds weight, roughness, or intensity

| Voiceless | Voiced | Shift |
|---|---|---|
| さらさら (smooth) | ざらざら (rough) | Roughness |
| とんとん (light knocking) | どんどん (heavy pounding) | Force |
| ぱたぱた (light flapping) | ばたばた (frantic commotion) | Intensity |
| ころころ (light rolling) | ごろごろ (heavy rumbling) | Weight |

### Structural patterns

| Feature | Effect | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Reduplication (ABAB) | Continuation or repetition | どきどき, きらきら, ぐるぐる |
| っ (small tsu) | Sudden, sharp action | ぱっと, ぐっと, はっと |
| ん | Weight or resonance | どんどん, がんがん, ぽんぽん |
| り ending | Softer, more gradual | ぐったり, のんびり, うっかり |
