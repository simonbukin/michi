# Appendix F — Classical Grammar Residues Reference

Classical Japanese (文語, *bungo*) was the standard written language of Japan until the late nineteenth century. When Japan modernized its written language to match spoken Japanese (口語, *kougo*), many classical forms did not vanish entirely. They survive today in signs, proverbs, legal documents, formal writing, and everyday set phrases. This appendix collects every classical residue form introduced in Stage 4 Chapters 1-2 into a single quick-reference.

You do not need to produce these forms in conversation. What you need is **recognition** — the ability to see a classical form in context and understand what it means.

---

## Classical Negative Forms

### ～ざる (= ～ない) — Classical Negative Attributive

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 書かざる | 書かない | not writing | Literary, formal writing | 許されざる行為だ。 ("An unforgivable act.") |
| 食べざる | 食べない | not eating | Proverbs | 食わざる者、働くべからず。 ("Those who do not eat shall not work.") |
| せざる | しない | not doing | Journalism, formal prose | 予期せざる事態が発生した。 ("An unforeseen situation occurred.") |
| 来（こ）ざる | 来ない | not coming | Literary | — |

**Formation:** Classical 未然形 (irrealis stem) + ざる. For most verbs, the irrealis stem matches the あ-stem used before ない. For する, the classical irrealis stem is せ, giving せざる.

### ～ざるを得ない — "Cannot Help But Do"

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 認めざるを得ない | 認めないわけにはいかない | cannot help but acknowledge | Formal speech, writing | 認めざるを得ない事実だ。 ("A fact that must be acknowledged.") |
| せざるを得ない | しないわけにはいかない | cannot help but do | Formal speech, business | あらゆる手段を用いざるを得なかった。 ("Had no choice but to use every means.") |

**Structure:** ざる (classical negative) + を得ない ("cannot obtain/avoid") = double negative meaning "cannot not-do."

---

### ～ず / ～ずに (= ～ないで) — Classical Negative Continuative

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 書かず | 書かないで / 書かなくて | without writing; did not write and... | Writing, formal connective | 雨が降らず、作物が枯れた。 ("It did not rain, and the crops withered.") |
| 書かずに | 書かないで | without writing | General writing, speech | 何も食べずに学校に行った。 ("Went to school without eating.") |
| せず | しないで | without doing | Writing, set phrases | 何の準備もせずに試験を受けた。 ("Took the exam without any preparation.") |
| 来（こ）ず | 来ないで | without coming | Literary, formal | — |

**Formation:** Classical 未然形 (irrealis stem) + ず/ずに. For する, the form is せず (not しず).

**Usage note:** ずに is broadly interchangeable with ないで in the meaning "without doing," but sounds slightly more formal. Bare ず (without に) is more restricted, appearing in set phrases and formal connective writing.

---

### ～べからず (= ～てはいけない) — Classical Prohibitive

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 入るべからず | 入ってはいけない | must not enter | Signs, rules | 芝生に入るべからず。 ("Keep off the grass.") |
| 捨てるべからず | 捨ててはいけない | must not discard | Signs | ここにゴミを捨てるべからず。 ("Do not dump garbage here.") |
| 忘るべからず | 忘れてはいけない | must not forget | Proverbs, maxims | 初心忘るべからず。 ("One must not forget the beginner's mind.") |

**Formation:** Verb dictionary form + べからず. The attributive form (before nouns) is べからざる: 許すべからざる行為 ("an act that must not be forgiven").

**Tone:** Absolute authority — a commandment, not a suggestion. You will see it on public signs and in classical quotations, not in conversation.

---

### ～ごとく / ～ごとき (= ～のように / ～のような) — Classical Comparison

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 嵐のごとく (adverbial) | 嵐のように | like a storm | Literary writing, speeches | 嵐のごとく彼は去っていった。 ("He departed like a storm.") |
| 夢のごとき (attributive) | 夢のような | dream-like | Literary writing | 夢のごとき日々が過ぎ去った。 ("Dream-like days passed away.") |
| 矢のごとし (terminal) | 矢のようだ | like an arrow | Proverbs | 光陰矢のごとし。 ("Time flies like an arrow.") |
| 飛ぶがごとく | 飛ぶように | as if flying | Literary, formal | 飛ぶがごとく走っていった。 ("Ran as if flying.") |

**Formation:** Noun + の + ごとく/ごとき. Verb + が + ごとく/ごとき. The sentence-final (terminal) form is ごとし.

**Three forms:**

| Form | Function | Modern parallel |
|---|---|---|
| ごとく | Adverbial (modifies verbs) | ～のように |
| ごとき | Attributive (modifies nouns) | ～のような |
| ごとし | Terminal (ends a sentence) | ～のようだ |

## Classical Temporal Forms

### ～なり — "Immediately Upon"

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Verb (dict.) + なり | ～するとすぐに | the instant someone does A, they immediately do B | Written narrative, formal speech | 彼は私の顔を見るなり、泣き出した。 ("The moment he saw my face, he burst into tears.") |

**Formation:** Verb dictionary form + なり.

**Constraints:** The subject of both clauses is normally the same. The second action is observed and past (not habitual or future). The second action is typically volitional and rapid.

---

### ～や否や (やいなや) — "The Instant That"

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Verb (dict.) + や否や | ～するとすぐに | before you can even tell whether A happened, B already begins | Literary, formal writing | ベルが鳴るや否や、学生たちは教室を飛び出した。 ("The instant the bell rang, the students rushed out.") |

**Formation:** Verb dictionary form + や否や.

**Nuance vs ～なり:** Both express extreme immediacy, but ～や否や allows different subjects in each clause and carries a slightly more dramatic tone.

---

### ～そばから — "As Soon As... Undone"

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Verb (dict./た form) + そばから | ～してもすぐに（元に戻る） | as fast as you do something, the result is undone | General writing, speech | 教えたそばから忘れてしまう。 ("She forgets the moment I teach her.") |

**Formation:** Verb dictionary form or た form + そばから.

**Defining feature:** Repetition and futility. The pattern implies something frustrating happens again and again. It would be unnatural to use ～そばから for something pleasant.

---

### ～が早いか — "No Sooner Than"

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Verb (dict.) + が早いか | ～するとほぼ同時に | so fast the two events seem simultaneous | Literary narrative | ドアを開けるが早いか、彼は走り出した。 ("No sooner had he opened the door than he broke into a run.") |

**Formation:** Verb dictionary form + が早いか.

**Tone:** Literary and dramatic. Describes a single observed event, not a habitual pattern.

---

### ～たとたん(に) — "The Moment (Something Unexpected)"

| Classical Form | Modern Equivalent | Meaning | Common Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Verb (た form) + とたん(に) | ～した瞬間に（予想外のことが起きた） | at the exact moment A was completed, something unexpected happened | Everyday conversation and writing | 立ち上がったとたんに、めまいがした。 ("The moment I stood up, I felt dizzy.") |

**Formation:** Verb た form + とたん(に).

**Defining feature:** Surprise. The second event must be unintentional or unexpected. You cannot use とたん if the second action was planned.

---

## Temporal Forms Comparison

| Expression | Immediacy | Surprise Required? | Repetition/Futility? | Formality | Same Subject Required? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ～なり | Very high | No | No | Literary | Usually yes |
| ～や否や | Very high | No | No | Literary | No |
| ～そばから | Moderate | No | Yes (defining) | Neutral | Usually yes |
| ～が早いか | Extreme | No | No | Literary | Usually yes |
| ～たとたん(に) | High | Yes (defining) | No | Conversational | Often different |

## Common Set Phrases Using Classical Forms

Many everyday expressions contain fossilized classical grammar. These are used by native speakers without conscious awareness of their classical origins.

### Set Phrases with ～ず

| Phrase | Reading | Meaning | Classical Element |
|---|---|---|---|
| 知らず知らず | しらずしらず | unknowingly, before one realizes | ず = ない |
| 絶えず | たえず | constantly, incessantly | ず = ない ("without ceasing") |
| 少なからず | すくなからず | not a little, considerably | からず = classical negative of ある |
| やむを得ず | やむをえず | unavoidably, having no choice | ず = ない |
| 限らず | かぎらず | not limited to | ず = ない |
| 問わず | とわず | regardless of | ず = ない ("without asking") |
| 尽きず | つきず | inexhaustibly | ず = ない ("without running out") |

### Set Phrases with ～ざる

| Phrase | Reading | Meaning | Classical Element |
|---|---|---|---|
| ～ざるを得ない | ざるをえない | cannot help but do | ざる = ない (attributive) |
| 予期せざる | よきせざる | unforeseen | せざる = しない |
| 許されざる | ゆるされざる | unforgivable | ざる = ない (attributive) |

### Set Phrases with ～べからず / ～べし

| Phrase | Reading | Meaning | Classical Element |
|---|---|---|---|
| 初心忘るべからず | しょしんわするべからず | never forget the beginner's mind | べからず = てはいけない |
| 侮るべからず | あなどるべからず | must not be underestimated | べからず = てはいけない |

### Set Phrases with ～ごとく / ～ごとし

| Phrase | Reading | Meaning | Classical Element |
|---|---|---|---|
| 光陰矢のごとし | こういんやのごとし | time flies like an arrow | ごとし = のようだ |
| 人生は旅のごとし | じんせいはたびのごとし | life is like a journey | ごとし = のようだ |

| ～ごとき | Noun + の / Verb + が | ～のような | Comparison (attributive) |

## Irregular Stems to Remember

The classical irrealis stem of する is せ (not し). This produces the irregular forms that appear across multiple patterns:

| Pattern | する form | Note |
|---|---|---|
| ～ざる | せざる | Not しざる |
| ～ざるを得ない | せざるを得ない | Not しざるを得ない |
| ～ず | せず | Not しず |
| ～ずに | せずに | Not しずに |
