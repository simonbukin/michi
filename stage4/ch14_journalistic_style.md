# Chapter 14 — Journalistic Style

Japanese newspaper articles have a distinctive style that is immediately recognizable and quite different from both spoken Japanese and academic writing. Headlines drop copulas and compress information into nominal phrases. Attribution follows rigid conventions for indicating the reliability of sources. The passive voice serves a special function — not just grammatical passivization, but a way of marking claims as reported rather than verified. Quotation conventions distinguish between direct speech and attributed viewpoints with a precision that English lacks.

If you want to read Japanese news — whether online, in print, or in broadcast transcripts — you need to understand these conventions. A reader who approaches a Japanese newspaper article with only conversational grammar will find the headlines incomprehensible, the attribution markers invisible, and the passive constructions confusing.

This chapter covers the five core features of Japanese journalistic prose: headline conventions, attribution patterns, the journalistic passive, quotation styles, and the language of numbers and statistics. The reading passage at the end is an unsimplified newspaper article that puts all of these features together.

## 14.1 Headline Conventions

Japanese news headlines follow conventions that are radically different from the body text. Understanding these conventions is essential for scanning news quickly.

### Noun止め (Nominalization)

The most characteristic feature of Japanese headlines is noun止め — ending with a noun rather than a verb. This creates a compressed, telegraphic style.

### Formation

| Full sentence | Headline form |
|---|---|
| 首相が辞任を表明した | 首相、辞任を表明 |
| 新幹線が全線で運転を再開した | 新幹線、全線で運転再開 |
| 日本企業の海外投資が過去最高を記録した | 日本企業の海外投資、過去最高 |
| 与党が法案を可決した | 与党、法案を可決 |

### Examples

> **首相、来月訪米へ**
> "Prime Minister to visit the US next month"

> **東京五輪、延期決定**
> "Tokyo Olympics, postponement decided"

> **円安、一時一五〇円台に**
> "Weak yen, temporarily reaches 150-yen range"

> **大雨で土砂崩れ　二人死亡**
> "Landslide from heavy rain; two dead"

### Dropped copula and particles

Headlines routinely drop だ/である, を, が, and は. The reader must infer the grammatical relationships from context and word order.

> **来年度予算、過去最大規模**
> "Next fiscal year's budget, largest scale ever" (は and だ both dropped)

> **政府、新対策発表**
> "Government announces new measures" (が and を both dropped)

### Abbreviated forms

Headlines use abbreviated vocabulary that would not appear in the body text:

| Abbreviation | Full form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 米 | アメリカ | America |
| 日 | 日本 | Japan |
| 中 | 中国 | China |
| 韓 | 韓国 | South Korea |
| 北 | 北朝鮮 | North Korea |
| 仏 | フランス | France |
| 英 | イギリス | UK |
| 独 | ドイツ | Germany |
| 露 | ロシア | Russia |
| 豪 | オーストラリア | Australia |

These abbreviations combine freely in compound forms:

> **日米首脳会談**
> "Japan-US summit meeting"

> **日中関係、改善の兆し**
> "Japan-China relations, signs of improvement"

### へ for future direction

Headlines frequently use へ to indicate future direction or upcoming action:

> **政府、消費税引き上げへ**
> "Government to raise consumption tax"

> **新制度、来年四月導入へ**
> "New system to be introduced in April next year"

## 14.2 Attribution

Japanese journalism uses a precise set of expressions to indicate the source and reliability of information. These attribution markers tell the reader whether a claim is an official statement, a leak, a rumor, or an inference.

### Formation

| Pattern | Source type | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| ～によると / ～によれば | Named source | High — official or reliable |
| 関係者によると / によれば | Unnamed insider | Medium-high — informed but anonymous |
| ～の話では | Specific person's account | Medium — one person's version |
| ～が明らかにした | Source revealed information | High — active disclosure |
| ～が明らかになった | Information became clear | High — passive/natural emergence |
| ～ことが分かった | It became known that | Medium-high — investigation result |
| ～と見られている | It is seen/believed that | Medium — inference, not confirmed |
| ～とされている | It is said/considered that | Medium — general understanding |
| ～という | Reported that | Neutral — simply passing along |
| ～模様だ | It appears that | Low — uncertain |

### Examples

> 政府**関係者によると**、首相は　来月中に　訪米する　方向で　調整しているという。
> "According to a government source, the prime minister is coordinating toward visiting the US within next month."

> 警察**の話では**、容疑者は　事件当日、現場近くに　いた　ことを　認めている。
> "According to police, the suspect admits to having been near the scene on the day of the incident."

> 同社**が明らかにした**ところに　よると、不正アクセスに　より　約一〇万件の　個人情報が　流出した。
> "According to what the company revealed, approximately 100,000 pieces of personal information were leaked due to unauthorized access."

> 新たな　証拠が　見つかった**ことが分かった**。
> "It has become known that new evidence was found."

### ～によると vs ～によれば

Both mean "according to," but によれば (conditional form) can carry a slightly more cautious tone — "if we go by what X says." In practice, the difference is minimal in news writing, and both are used interchangeably.

> 気象庁**によると**、台風は　今夜半に　関東地方に　最接近する　見通しだ。
> "According to the Meteorological Agency, the typhoon is expected to make its closest approach to the Kanto region around midnight tonight."

> 専門家**によれば**、この　傾向は　今後も　続く　可能性が　高いという。
> "According to experts, this trend is likely to continue going forward."

## 14.3 Journalistic Passive

The passive voice in journalism serves a different function than in everyday Japanese. Rather than expressing suffering or inconvenience (the so-called "adversative passive"), the journalistic passive marks information as reported, observed, or generally understood — without the writer taking personal responsibility for the claim.

### Formation

| Pattern | Nuance | English |
|---|---|---|
| ～とされている | "It is said/held that" — general consensus | established understanding |
| ～と見られている | "It is believed/seen that" — informed inference | expert or insider judgment |
| ～と報じられた | "It was reported that" — media attribution | reported by another outlet |
| ～と伝えられている | "It is conveyed/reported that" — ongoing report | continuing coverage |
| ～と指摘されている | "It is pointed out that" — critical assessment | identified by experts |
| ～と見られる | "It is viewed as" — current assessment | real-time inference |

### Examples

> 容疑者は　金銭目的で　犯行に　及んだ　**と見られている**。
> "It is believed that the suspect committed the crime for financial reasons."

> 被害額は　数十億円に　上る　**とされている**。
> "The damage is said to amount to tens of billions of yen."

> 現地メディアは、大統領が　辞任の　意向を　示した　**と報じた**。
> "Local media reported that the president indicated an intention to resign."

> 同制度には　いくつかの　課題が　残されている　**と指摘されている**。
> "It has been pointed out that several issues remain with the system."

### と見られている vs とされている

These two are the most commonly confused pair. The difference is:

| Pattern | Basis | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| と見られている | Observable evidence, expert inference | "Based on what we can see, it seems..." |
| とされている | General agreement, established view | "It is generally held that..." |

> 犯人は　建物の　裏口から　侵入した　**と見られている**。
> "It is believed that the criminal entered through the back door of the building." (Based on evidence at the scene.)

> この　遺跡は　約二千年前のものだ　**とされている**。
> "This ruin is said to date back approximately 2,000 years." (Based on established scholarship.)

## 14.4 Quotation in Journalism

Japanese news articles use two distinct quotation styles: direct quotation with 「」 brackets, and indirect quotation through attributed viewpoints. The choice between them signals how precisely the words represent what the source actually said.

### Direct quotation

Direct quotation uses Japanese quotation marks 「」 and is followed by と述べた, と語った, と話した, or similar verbs.

### Formation

| Verb | Nuance |
|---|---|
| と述べた | "stated" — formal, official |
| と語った | "spoke of, recounted" — narrative, detailed |
| と話した | "said" — neutral, general |
| と明言した | "stated clearly" — emphatic |
| と強調した | "emphasized" — with force |
| と説明した | "explained" — providing detail |

### Examples

> 首相は　記者会見で　「経済再生を　最優先課題として　取り組む」**と述べた**。
> "The prime minister stated at the press conference, 'We will tackle economic revitalization as our top priority.'"

> 被災者の　男性は　「一瞬の　出来事で、何が　起きたのか　分からなかった」**と語った**。
> "A male disaster victim recounted, 'It happened in an instant, and I did not know what was going on.'"

> 同社の　広報担当者は　「事実関係を　確認中です」**と話した**。
> "The company's public relations representative said, 'We are currently confirming the facts.'"

### Indirect quotation

Indirect quotation does not use 「」 brackets. Instead, the source's position is paraphrased, often using noun phrases with の or との.

### Formation

| Pattern | Nuance |
|---|---|
| ～との見方を示した | "indicated the view that" — analytical |
| ～との考えを示した | "indicated the thinking that" — position statement |
| ～との認識を示した | "indicated the understanding that" — assessment |
| ～との方針を明らかにした | "made clear the policy of" — decision |
| ～と見方が広がっている | "the view is spreading that" — growing consensus |

### Examples

> 日銀総裁は、当面　金利を　据え置く　**との方針を　明らかにした**。
> "The Bank of Japan governor made clear the policy of maintaining interest rates for the time being."

> 与党内では、年内の　解散は　ない　**との見方が　広がっている**。
> "Within the ruling party, the view is spreading that there will be no dissolution of parliament within the year."

> 専門家は、今回の　地震が　さらに　大きな　地震の　前触れである　可能性を　否定できない　**との認識を　示した**。
> "Experts indicated the understanding that the possibility of this earthquake being a precursor to a larger one cannot be denied."

### Direct vs indirect — when each is used

Direct quotation is used for:
- Key phrases that the source specifically chose
- Emotional or dramatic statements
- Official positions that must be quoted exactly

Indirect quotation is used for:
- Summarizing a longer statement
- Describing a general position or policy
- When the exact wording is less important than the substance

## 14.5 Numbers and Statistics

Japanese news is full of numerical data, and it uses specific expressions to present statistics, growth rates, and quantities.

### Formation

| Pattern | Meaning | Example context |
|---|---|---|
| ～に上る | "amounts to, reaches" | Damage, costs, numbers |
| ～に達する | "reaches, attains" | Milestones, records |
| ～を超える / 上回る | "exceeds, surpasses" | Comparisons |
| ～を下回る | "falls below" | Below expectations |
| 前年比～％増 | "~% increase year-on-year" | Annual comparisons |
| 前年比～％減 | "~% decrease year-on-year" | Annual comparisons |
| 前年同期比 | "compared to same period previous year" | Quarterly comparisons |
| ～倍 | "~times, ~fold" | Multiples |
| 約～ / およそ～ | "approximately ~" | Rough figures |
| ～台 | "~range" (for round numbers) | Price, temperature ranges |

### Examples

> 被害総額は　**約五百億円に　上る**　と見られている。
> "The total damage is believed to amount to approximately 50 billion yen."

> 訪日外国人旅行者数が　初めて　年間三千万人に　**達した**。
> "The number of foreign visitors to Japan reached 30 million per year for the first time."

> 売上高は　**前年比　一二・五パーセント増**の　一兆二千億円だった。
> "Sales were 1.2 trillion yen, an increase of 12.5% year-on-year."

> 失業率は　**前月を　〇・一ポイント　下回り**、二・四パーセントとなった。
> "The unemployment rate fell 0.1 percentage points below the previous month, reaching 2.4%."

> 感染者数は　前週の　**約二倍**に　増加した。
> "The number of infections increased to approximately double the previous week."

> 東京の　最高気温は　**三五度台**に　達し、猛暑日となった。
> "The high temperature in Tokyo reached the 35-degree range, making it an extremely hot day."

### Large numbers in Japanese

Japanese groups numbers by ten-thousands (万), not thousands:

| Number | Japanese | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| 10,000 | 一万 | いちまん |
| 100,000 | 十万 | じゅうまん |
| 1,000,000 | 百万 | ひゃくまん |
| 10,000,000 | 千万 | せんまん |
| 100,000,000 | 一億 | いちおく |
| 1,000,000,000,000 | 一兆 | いっちょう |

News articles frequently use these units. Being comfortable reading them quickly is essential for following economic and political news.

## 14.6 Reading Passage

次の文章は、新聞記事です。

---

**高齢者の就業率、過去最高を更新　七〇歳以上も二割超**

総務省が十五日発表した労働力調査によると、六五歳以上の高齢者の就業率が二五・一パーセントとなり、過去最高を更新した。七〇歳以上に限っても就業率は二〇・三パーセントに達し、初めて二割を超えた。人手不足を背景に企業が高齢者の雇用を拡大していることに加え、年金だけでは生活が厳しいとして働き続ける高齢者が増えていることが要因と見られている。

就業者数は六五歳以上で約九三〇万人に上り、前年同期比で約三五万人増加した。産業別では、卸売・小売業が最も多く、次いで医療・福祉、製造業の順だった。雇用形態別では、非正規雇用が約七割を占めている。

政府は二〇二一年に施行した改正高年齢者雇用安定法で、企業に対し七〇歳までの就業機会の確保を努力義務とした。厚生労働省の調査によると、七〇歳までの就業機会を確保している企業の割合は前年比三・二ポイント増の二九・七パーセントだった。

一方、高齢者の就業増加には課題もあるとの指摘がある。労働問題に詳しい東京大学の山本教授は「高齢者の就業率が上がっていること自体は望ましいが、その背景には年金制度への不安がある。やむを得ず働いている高齢者も少なくないのではないか」との見方を示した。

また、高齢者の労働災害が増加していることも問題とされている。厚労省のまとめでは、六五歳以上の労働災害による死傷者数は前年比八パーセント増の約三万九千人に上った。転倒や腰痛など、加齢に伴う身体機能の低下が原因と見られるケースが多いという。

政府関係者は「高齢者が安心して働ける環境の整備が急務だ」と述べた。今後、職場の安全対策や柔軟な勤務形態の導入を促進する方針だとしている。

---

**Translation:**

**Employment Rate Among Elderly Reaches Record High; Over 20% Among Those 70 and Above**

According to the Labor Force Survey released by the Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications on the 15th, the employment rate among people aged 65 and above reached 25.1%, setting a new record high. Even when limited to those aged 70 and above, the employment rate reached 20.3%, exceeding 20% for the first time. The factors are believed to be companies expanding employment of the elderly against a backdrop of labor shortages, as well as an increasing number of elderly people who continue working because they find it difficult to live on pensions alone.

The number of employed persons aged 65 and above amounted to approximately 9.3 million, an increase of about 350,000 compared to the same period of the previous year. By industry, wholesale and retail trade was the most common, followed by medical and welfare services, then manufacturing. By employment type, non-regular employment accounted for approximately 70%.

In the revised Act on Stabilization of Employment of Elderly Persons, enacted in 2021, the government made it an "effort obligation" for companies to ensure employment opportunities up to age 70. According to a Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare survey, the proportion of companies ensuring employment opportunities up to age 70 was 29.7%, an increase of 3.2 percentage points year-on-year.

On the other hand, there are those who point out that the increase in elderly employment also has challenges. Professor Yamamoto of the University of Tokyo, an expert on labor issues, indicated the view that "While the rising employment rate among the elderly is itself desirable, behind it lies anxiety about the pension system. It may be that no small number of elderly people are working because they have no choice."

Additionally, the increase in workplace accidents among the elderly is also considered a problem. According to the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare's compilation, the number of deaths and injuries from workplace accidents among those 65 and above amounted to approximately 39,000, an 8% increase year-on-year. Many cases are believed to be caused by declines in physical function associated with aging, such as falls and lower back pain.

A government official stated, "Creating an environment where the elderly can work with peace of mind is an urgent task." The government's policy going forward is said to be to promote workplace safety measures and the introduction of flexible work arrangements.
