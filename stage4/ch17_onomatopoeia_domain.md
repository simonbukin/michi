# Chapter 17 — Onomatopoeia by Domain

The previous chapter covered the system — the categories, script conventions, sound symbolism, and grammatical patterns. This chapter is a practical reference. It organizes over 100 onomatopoeia by the domains where they appear: weather, body sensations, emotions, movement, texture, and sound. For each word, you will find its meaning, a natural example sentence, and notes on usage.

This is a chapter to study actively and return to as a reference. You do not need to memorize every item at once. Focus first on the words marked with a star in the tables — these are the most frequent and most useful. Then expand your range gradually as you encounter them in reading, conversation, and media.

The reading passage at the end of this chapter demonstrates how onomatopoeia appear in literary Japanese. Notice how densely they are used compared to the news and academic texts you have been reading in previous chapters.

## 17.1 Weather and Nature

These onomatopoeia describe rain, wind, light, and natural phenomena. Many of them appear in weather forecasts, literature, and everyday conversation about weather.

### Rain

| オノマトペ | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ざあざあ | heavy rain (loud, forceful) | 雨がざあざあと降っている。 |
| しとしと | gentle, quiet rain | 朝からしとしとと雨が降り続いている。 |
| ぽつぽつ | scattered drops, beginning to rain | ぽつぽつと雨が降り始めた。 |
| ぱらぱら | light, scattered rain | ぱらぱらと小雨が降った。 |
| びしょびしょ | soaking wet (result of rain) | 傘を忘れてびしょびしょになった。 |
| じとじと | persistently damp, drizzly | じとじとした梅雨の季節が続く。 |

| ひゅうひゅう | whistling wind | 隙間からひゅうひゅうと風が入ってくる。 |

### Light and sky

## 17.2 Body and Physical Sensation

## 17.7 Reading Passage

次の文章は、雨の日の帰り道を描いた短い散文です。オノマトペの使い方に注目してください。

---

梅雨が明けないまま七月になった。

朝からしとしとと降り続いていた雨が、夕方になってざあざあという本降りに変わった。駅を出ると、アスファルトの上をぱしゃぱしゃと水が跳ねている。傘をさしても足元はびしょびしょだった。

商店街を抜けると、人通りがぱたりと途絶えた。しーんとした住宅街に、自分の足音だけがぺたぺたと響く。遠くでごろごろと雷が鳴り、ぴかっと空が光った。思わずぞくっとして、足を速めた。

家の前に着くと、ほっとため息をついた。鍵をがちゃりと開け、玄関に入ると、飼い猫がにゃあと鳴きながら足元にすりすりと寄ってきた。濡れた靴をぬるぬるする手で脱ぎ、ぐったりとソファに倒れ込んだ。

しばらくぼうっとしていると、台所からことことと鍋の音が聞こえてきた。母が夕飯を作っているらしい。温かいスープの匂いがふわっと漂ってきて、やっとすっきりした気持ちになった。外ではまだ雨がざあざあと降り続いていたが、家の中はほんわかと温かかった。

---

**Translation:**

July arrived without the rainy season ending.

The rain that had been falling gently since morning changed to a real downpour in the evening. Stepping out of the station, water splashed across the asphalt. Even with an umbrella, my feet were soaked.

Once past the shopping street, foot traffic abruptly disappeared. In the hushed residential area, only my own footsteps echoed with a wet slapping sound. In the distance, thunder rumbled, and the sky flashed. I shuddered involuntarily and quickened my pace.

Arriving in front of my house, I breathed a sigh of relief. I turned the key with a click and entered the entryway, where my pet cat came rubbing up against my legs with a meow. I peeled off my wet shoes with slippery hands and collapsed limply onto the sofa.

After spacing out for a while, I heard the sound of a pot simmering from the kitchen. My mother seemed to be making dinner. The smell of warm soup drifted over softly, and I finally felt refreshed. Outside, the rain was still pouring, but inside the house it was warm and cozy.
