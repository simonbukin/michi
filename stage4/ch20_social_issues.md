# Chapter 20 — Social Issues

Japan faces a set of deeply interconnected social challenges that dominate public discourse, shape government policy, and appear constantly in newspapers, television debates, and online commentary. Understanding the vocabulary and framing of these issues is essential for anyone who wants to read Japanese media at an adult level. This chapter covers five major domains: the demographic crisis of declining birthrates and an aging population, economic inequality and precarious employment, gender inequality in the workplace and society, the growing presence of foreign workers and the debate over immigration, and the phenomenon of exploitative companies and death from overwork.

These topics appear regularly on the JLPT N2 reading section and are common subjects in university entrance essays, opinion columns, and policy documents. The vocabulary is largely Sino-Japanese compounds, and the arguments follow patterns you have already studied in Chapters 13-15 on academic and journalistic style. What is new here is the thematic depth: learning to recognize how these issues are framed, debated, and connected to each other in Japanese public discourse.

## 20.1 少子高齢化 — Declining Birthrate and Aging Society

Japan's most frequently discussed demographic challenge is 少子高齢化, a compound term combining 少子化 (declining birthrate) and 高齢化 (aging of the population). These two trends reinforce each other: fewer children are born while the elderly population grows, creating a shrinking workforce and an expanding burden on social services.

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 少子化 | declining birthrate |
| 高齢化 | aging (of society) |
| 少子高齢化 | declining birthrate + aging society (combined) |
| 出生率 | birthrate |
| 合計特殊出生率 | total fertility rate |
| 人口減少 | population decline |
| 高齢者 | elderly person |
| 介護 | nursing care, eldercare |
| 年金 | pension |
| 社会保障 | social security |
| 労働力不足 | labor shortage |
| 生産年齢人口 | working-age population |

### How it is discussed

The standard framing presents 少子高齢化 as a national crisis. News articles and opinion pieces typically cite statistics and then discuss consequences.

> 日本の合計特殊出生率は長年にわたって低下し続けており、現在は約1.2である。このまま人口減少が進めば、社会保障制度の維持が困難になると指摘されている。
> "Japan's total fertility rate has continued to decline over many years and currently stands at approximately 1.2. It has been pointed out that if population decline continues at this rate, maintaining the social security system will become difficult."

> 高齢者の割合が増加する一方で、介護を担う若い世代は減少している。誰が高齢者を支えるのかという問題は、もはや避けて通れない課題である。
> "While the proportion of elderly people increases, the younger generation that bears the burden of caregiving is decreasing. The question of who will support the elderly is a challenge that can no longer be avoided."

### Causes commonly cited

Japanese media typically discusses several interconnected causes:

> 晩婚化や未婚率の上昇が少子化の主な原因として挙げられている。経済的な不安から結婚や出産をためらう若者が増えている。
> "Late marriage and rising rates of remaining unmarried are cited as the main causes of the declining birthrate. The number of young people who hesitate to marry or have children due to economic anxiety is increasing."

> 保育所の不足も深刻な問題である。「保育園落ちた日本死ね」というブログ記事が話題になったことは、待機児童問題の深刻さを象徴している。
> "The shortage of daycare facilities is also a serious problem. The fact that a blog post saying 'I didn't get into daycare, die Japan' became a topic of public discussion symbolizes the severity of the waitlisted children problem."

## 20.2 格差社会 — Inequality

格差社会 (a society of disparities) refers to the widening gap between rich and poor in Japan. The term became a media buzzword in the mid-2000s and remains central to discussions of economic and social policy.

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 格差 | gap, disparity |
| 格差社会 | society of inequality |
| 貧困 | poverty |
| 相対的貧困 | relative poverty |
| 非正規雇用 | non-regular employment |
| 正社員 | regular (full-time, permanent) employee |
| 派遣社員 | dispatched/temporary worker |
| パート | part-time worker |
| ワーキングプア | working poor |
| 所得格差 | income disparity |
| 生活保護 | welfare, public assistance |
| 最低賃金 | minimum wage |

### The regular vs non-regular divide

The most important structural concept is the distinction between 正規雇用 (regular employment) and 非正規雇用 (non-regular employment). Regular employees enjoy job security, benefits, bonuses, and career advancement. Non-regular workers lack most of these protections.

> 日本の労働者の約四割が非正規雇用であると言われている。正社員と非正規社員の間には、賃金だけでなく、福利厚生や昇進の機会にも大きな格差がある。
> "It is said that approximately forty percent of Japanese workers are in non-regular employment. Between regular and non-regular employees, there is a large gap not only in wages but also in benefits and opportunities for promotion."

> ワーキングプアとは、働いているにもかかわらず、生活保護水準以下の収入しか得られない人々のことである。非正規雇用の拡大とともに、この問題は深刻化している。
> "Working poor refers to people who, despite working, can only earn an income below the level of public assistance. Along with the expansion of non-regular employment, this problem has become more severe."

### Poverty and children

> 子どもの貧困率が上昇していることも大きな問題である。経済的に困難な家庭の子どもは、教育の機会が限られ、将来の所得にも影響が出るという悪循環に陥りやすい。
> "The rising child poverty rate is also a major problem. Children from economically disadvantaged families are prone to falling into a vicious cycle where educational opportunities are limited and future income is also affected."

## 20.3 ジェンダー — Gender Equality

Japan consistently ranks low in international gender equality indices. Discussions of ジェンダー (gender) in Japanese media focus on workplace discrimination, the unequal burden of childcare and housework, and the slow progress of policy reforms.

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| ジェンダー | gender |
| 男女平等 | gender equality |
| 男女格差 | gender gap |
| 女性活躍 | women's active participation |
| ガラスの天井 | glass ceiling |
| セクハラ | sexual harassment |
| マタハラ | maternity harassment |
| 育児休暇 | childcare leave |
| 育休 | childcare leave (abbreviation) |
| 共働き | dual-income (both spouses working) |
| 家事分担 | division of housework |
| M字カーブ | M-shaped curve (women's labor participation) |

### The M-shaped curve

A concept frequently referenced in gender discussions is the M字カーブ: the pattern where women's labor force participation drops sharply during childbearing years and partially recovers later.

> 日本の女性の労働力率は、出産・育児の時期に大きく低下するM字カーブを描くことが知られている。多くの女性が出産を機に退職し、子育てが一段落してからパートタイムで復帰するというパターンが依然として一般的である。
> "It is known that Japanese women's labor force participation rate forms an M-shaped curve, dropping significantly during the period of childbirth and child-rearing. The pattern where many women leave their jobs upon giving birth and return as part-time workers after child-rearing settles down is still common."

### Childcare leave and workplace culture

> 育児休暇の制度自体は整っているものの、実際に取得する男性は依然として少ない。「取りたいが、取れる雰囲気ではない」という声が多く、職場の文化が制度の活用を妨げている。
> "Although the childcare leave system itself is well-established, the number of men who actually take it remains small. There are many voices saying 'I want to take it, but the atmosphere doesn't allow it,' and workplace culture is hindering the use of the system."

> 管理職に占める女性の割合は依然として低く、いわゆるガラスの天井が存在すると指摘されている。政府は女性管理職の比率を三割に引き上げるという目標を掲げたが、達成には程遠い状況である。
> "The proportion of women in management positions remains low, and it has been pointed out that a so-called glass ceiling exists. The government set a goal of raising the ratio of female managers to thirty percent, but the situation is far from achieving this."

## 20.4 外国人労働者 — Foreign Workers and Immigration

As labor shortages deepen due to 少子高齢化, Japan has gradually expanded pathways for foreign workers while avoiding the word 移民 (immigration). The debate over 多文化共生 (multicultural coexistence) touches on identity, language policy, and community integration.

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 外国人労働者 | foreign worker |
| 移民 | immigrant, immigration |
| 技能実習生 | technical intern trainee |
| 在留資格 | residence status |
| 特定技能 | specified skilled worker (visa category) |
| 多文化共生 | multicultural coexistence |
| 受け入れ | acceptance, receiving |
| 人手不足 | labor shortage |
| 不法滞在 | illegal overstay |
| 日本語教育 | Japanese language education |
| 定住 | permanent settlement |
| 共生社会 | inclusive society |

### The technical intern trainee system

> 技能実習制度は、発展途上国への技術移転を目的として設立されたが、実態としては安価な労働力の確保手段になっているとの批判がある。低賃金、長時間労働、劣悪な住環境などの問題が繰り返し報道されている。
> "The Technical Intern Training Program was established with the purpose of technology transfer to developing countries, but there is criticism that in reality it has become a means of securing cheap labor. Problems such as low wages, long working hours, and poor living conditions have been repeatedly reported."

### Multicultural coexistence

> 外国人住民が増加する中で、地域社会における多文化共生のあり方が問われている。言葉の壁や文化の違いから生じるトラブルもあるが、互いの文化を尊重し、共に暮らしていくための取り組みが各地で進められている。
> "As the number of foreign residents increases, the question of how to achieve multicultural coexistence in local communities is being asked. While there are troubles arising from language barriers and cultural differences, efforts to respect each other's cultures and live together are being advanced in various regions."

> 外国人の子どもたちの中には、日本語も母語も十分に習得できない「ダブルリミテッド」と呼ばれる状態に陥るケースがある。日本語教育の充実が急務であると言われている。
> "Among foreign children, there are cases of falling into a state called 'double limited,' where neither Japanese nor the mother tongue is adequately acquired. It is said that enhancing Japanese language education is an urgent task."

## 20.5 ブラック企業 and 過労死 — Toxic Workplaces and Death from Overwork

ブラック企業 (black company, i.e., exploitative company) and 過労死 (death from overwork) are terms that have entered international discourse. They represent the extreme end of Japan's long-hours work culture.

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| ブラック企業 | exploitative company |
| 過労死 | death from overwork |
| 過労自殺 | suicide from overwork |
| 長時間労働 | long working hours |
| サービス残業 | unpaid overtime |
| 残業時間 | overtime hours |
| 働き方改革 | work style reform |
| 有給休暇 | paid leave |
| メンタルヘルス | mental health |
| パワハラ | power harassment |
| 労働基準法 | Labor Standards Act |
| 三六協定 | Article 36 agreement (overtime agreement) |

### The problem

> 過労死は、長時間労働による脳や心臓の疾患、あるいは精神的な負担による自殺を指す。日本では年間数百件の過労死が認定されているが、実際の数はさらに多いと推測されている。
> "Karoshi refers to brain and heart diseases caused by long working hours, or suicide due to mental strain. In Japan, several hundred cases of karoshi are officially recognized annually, but the actual number is estimated to be even higher."

> ブラック企業の特徴として、異常な長時間労働、サービス残業の常態化、パワーハラスメント、有給休暇を取らせないなどが挙げられる。若い社員が使い捨てにされるケースが社会問題となっている。
> "Characteristics of exploitative companies include abnormally long working hours, normalization of unpaid overtime, power harassment, and not allowing employees to take paid leave. Cases where young employees are treated as disposable have become a social problem."

### Work style reform

> 政府は「働き方改革」を掲げ、残業時間の上限規制や有給休暇の取得義務化などの法整備を進めてきた。しかし、法律を変えるだけでは企業文化は変わらないとの声もあり、実効性に疑問を呈する専門家もいる。
> "The government has promoted 'work style reform,' advancing legislation such as caps on overtime hours and mandatory use of paid leave. However, there are also voices saying that simply changing the law will not change corporate culture, and some experts question its effectiveness."

> テレワークの普及により、働き方が多様化する一方で、仕事とプライベートの境界が曖昧になり、かえって労働時間が増えたというケースも報告されている。
> "While the spread of telework has diversified work styles, cases have also been reported where the boundary between work and private life has become blurred and working hours have actually increased."

## 20.6 Reading Passage

次の文章は、あるオンラインメディアに掲載された社会問題に関する意見記事の抜粋である。

---

日本社会が直面する課題は多岐にわたるが、その根底にあるのは少子高齢化という構造的な問題ではないだろうか。

出生率の低下は、単に子どもの数が減るという問題にとどまらない。労働力人口の減少は経済成長を鈍化させ、社会保障費の増大は現役世代の負担を重くする。介護人材の不足は深刻化し、年金制度の持続可能性にも疑問が投げかけられている。

こうした状況の中で、非正規雇用の拡大が若者の生活基盤を不安定にしている。正社員になれないまま三十代、四十代を迎える人が増え、結婚や出産をあきらめざるを得ないケースも少なくない。つまり、格差の問題と少子化の問題は、切り離して考えることができないのである。

ジェンダーの問題も同様である。女性が出産後も安心して働き続けられる環境が整わなければ、少子化は止まらない。男性の育児参加を促進し、長時間労働を是正しなければ、女性だけに負担が集中する現状は変わらない。ブラック企業の問題は、単に労働者の権利の問題ではなく、社会全体の持続可能性に関わる問題である。

外国人労働者の受け入れ拡大は、労働力不足を補う方策として注目されているが、受け入れるからには、共に暮らす社会の仕組みを整える必要がある。言葉の支援、教育の保障、差別の防止。多文化共生は理念にとどまらず、具体的な施策として実行されなければならない。

これらの問題は、それぞれが独立しているように見えて、実は深く結びついている。一つの問題だけを取り出して解決することはできない。日本社会の未来を考えるとき、この複雑な連鎖を直視することが求められている。

---

**Translation:**

The challenges facing Japanese society are wide-ranging, but at their root, is it not the structural problem of declining birthrates and an aging population?

The decline in the birthrate is not merely a problem of fewer children. A shrinking labor force slows economic growth, and the increasing cost of social security places a heavier burden on the working generation. The shortage of caregiving workers is becoming severe, and questions are being raised about the sustainability of the pension system.

In this situation, the expansion of non-regular employment is destabilizing the economic foundation of young people's lives. The number of people reaching their thirties and forties without becoming regular employees is increasing, and there are not a few cases where people have no choice but to give up on marriage or having children. In other words, the problem of inequality and the problem of declining birthrates cannot be considered separately.

The issue of gender is similar. Unless an environment is established where women can continue working with confidence after giving birth, the declining birthrate will not stop. Unless men's participation in childcare is promoted and long working hours are corrected, the current situation where the burden is concentrated solely on women will not change. The problem of exploitative companies is not merely a matter of workers' rights but an issue that concerns the sustainability of society as a whole.

The expansion of accepting foreign workers is attracting attention as a measure to compensate for labor shortages, but since we are accepting them, it is necessary to establish social systems for living together. Language support, guaranteed education, prevention of discrimination. Multicultural coexistence must not remain merely an ideal but must be implemented as concrete policy measures.

These problems appear to be independent of each other, but in reality they are deeply interconnected. It is not possible to take out just one problem and solve it. When thinking about the future of Japanese society, we are called upon to face this complex chain of issues directly.
