# Chapter 22 — Legal and Bureaucratic Japanese

Anyone living in Japan will eventually encounter legal and bureaucratic documents: apartment lease contracts, employment agreements, government registration forms, and official notices. These documents use a distinctive register of Japanese that differs sharply from both spoken language and standard written prose. Sentences are long, heavily nominalized, and built around a small set of fixed expressions that carry precise legal meaning.

You do not need to produce this language. What you need is the ability to read it well enough to understand what you are signing, what the government is asking of you, and where the important conditions are buried in a wall of formal text. This chapter gives you the vocabulary, set phrases, and reading strategies to do exactly that.

The legal register relies heavily on Sino-Japanese vocabulary, uses ものとする instead of する, replaces casual conditionals with formal ones, and avoids ambiguity through exhaustive specification. Once you learn the patterns, you will find that legal documents are actually more predictable and formulaic than most other types of Japanese text.

## 22.1 Contract Vocabulary

Contracts (契約書) in Japan follow standard formats. Whether you are signing an employment contract, a service agreement, or a mobile phone plan, you will encounter the same core vocabulary.

### Parties to a contract

Japanese contracts traditionally refer to the parties as 甲 (こう, Party A) and 乙 (おつ, Party B). In a lease, 甲 is typically the landlord and 乙 is the tenant. In an employment contract, 甲 is the employer and 乙 is the employee.

> 甲と乙は、以下の条項に基づき、本契約を締結する。
> "Party A and Party B hereby conclude this contract based on the following provisions."

### Core contract terms

| Term | Reading | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| 契約 | けいやく | contract |
| 契約書 | けいやくしょ | contract document |
| 条項 | じょうこう | provision, clause |
| 第〜条 | だい〜じょう | Article (number) |
| 甲 | こう | Party A |
| 乙 | おつ | Party B |
| 締結 | ていけつ | conclusion (of a contract) |
| 有効期限 | ゆうこうきげん | expiration date, period of validity |
| 契約期間 | けいやくきかん | contract period |
| 解約 | かいやく | cancellation (of a contract) |
| 解除 | かいじょ | termination, rescission |
| 違約金 | いやくきん | penalty for breach of contract |
| 損害賠償 | そんがいばいしょう | compensation for damages |
| 合意 | ごうい | agreement, mutual consent |
| 署名 | しょめい | signature |
| 捺印 | なついん | affixing a seal |

### Common contract clauses

> 本契約の有効期間は、契約締結日から二年間とする。
> "The effective period of this contract shall be two years from the date of contract conclusion."

> 甲または乙が本契約に違反した場合、相手方は書面により通知の上、本契約を解除することができる。
> "In the event that Party A or Party B violates this contract, the other party may terminate this contract by providing written notice."

> 前項の解除により損害が生じた場合、違反した当事者はその損害を賠償するものとする。
> "In the event that damages arise from the termination described in the preceding paragraph, the party in violation shall compensate for such damages."

## 22.2 Lease Agreements

Renting an apartment in Japan means signing a 賃貸契約書 (lease contract). Understanding the key terms will help you know what you are committing to before you sign.

### Key lease terms

| Term | Reading | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| 賃貸契約書 | ちんたいけいやくしょ | lease contract |
| 賃貸人 | ちんたいにん | lessor (landlord) |
| 賃借人 | ちんしゃくにん | lessee (tenant) |
| 家賃 | やちん | rent |
| 敷金 | しききん | security deposit |
| 礼金 | れいきん | key money (non-refundable) |
| 共益費 | きょうえきひ | common area maintenance fee |
| 管理費 | かんりひ | management fee |
| 更新 | こうしん | renewal |
| 更新料 | こうしんりょう | renewal fee |
| 原状回復 | げんじょうかいふく | restoration to original condition |
| 退去 | たいきょ | vacating, moving out |
| 連帯保証人 | れんたいほしょうにん | joint guarantor |
| 仲介手数料 | ちゅうかいてすうりょう | brokerage fee |

### Understanding 敷金 and 礼金

The 敷金/礼金 system is unique to Japan. 敷金 is a refundable deposit (typically one to two months' rent) that covers damages beyond normal wear and tear. 礼金 is a non-refundable "gift" to the landlord (also typically one to two months' rent). In recent years, 礼金ゼロ (zero key money) properties have become more common.

> 敷金は、賃借人が本物件を退去した後、原状回復に要する費用を差し引いた残額を、退去後三十日以内に返還するものとする。
> "The security deposit shall be returned within thirty days after vacating, after deducting the costs required for restoration to original condition, following the lessee's departure from the property."

### Renewal

> 本契約の更新を希望する場合、賃借人は契約期間満了の三ヶ月前までに、書面にて賃貸人に申し出るものとする。更新の際は、更新料として家賃一ヶ月分を支払うものとする。
> "In the event that the lessee wishes to renew this contract, the lessee shall notify the lessor in writing no later than three months before the expiration of the contract period. Upon renewal, the lessee shall pay a renewal fee equivalent to one month's rent."

| 添付 | てんぷ | attachment (of documents) |

### Common instructions on forms

Government forms include standard instructions that use a consistent set of expressions:

> 以下の事項を記入の上、必要書類を添付して提出してください。
> "Please fill in the following items and submit with the required documents attached."

> 届出は、転入した日から十四日以内に行うものとする。
> "The notification shall be filed within fourteen days from the date of moving in."

> 本人確認のため、身分証明書の提示を求める場合があります。
> "For identity verification, you may be asked to present identification."

> 申請書を受理した後、証明書の交付まで約一週間かかります。
> "After accepting the application, it will take approximately one week until the certificate is issued."

## 22.4 Legal Set Phrases

Legal Japanese relies on a small set of fixed expressions that carry precise meaning. These expressions appear in contracts, regulations, and official documents throughout Japan. Learning them unlocks the entire register.

### ものとする — "shall"

ものとする is the legal equivalent of "shall" in English contracts. It indicates an obligation or a rule that the parties agree to follow.

> 賃借人は、毎月末日までに翌月分の家賃を支払うものとする。
> "The lessee shall pay the following month's rent by the last day of each month."

> 契約の変更は、甲乙双方の書面による合意を要するものとする。
> "Modification of the contract shall require written agreement from both Party A and Party B."

When negated, ものとする becomes ないものとする ("shall not"):

> 賃借人は、賃貸人の書面による承諾なく、本物件を第三者に転貸しないものとする。
> "The lessee shall not sublease this property to a third party without the written consent of the lessor."

### に限る — "limited to"

に限る restricts scope. In legal documents, it specifies that something applies only to the stated case.

> 解約の申し出は、書面による場合に限る。
> "Requests for cancellation are limited to those made in writing."

> 本サービスの利用は、登録会員に限る。
> "Use of this service is limited to registered members."

### を要する — "requires"

を要する indicates that something is required or necessary.

> 本契約の解除には、三ヶ月前の書面による通知を要する。
> "Termination of this contract requires written notice three months in advance."

> 変更届の提出には、本人確認書類の添付を要する。
> "Submission of a change notification requires the attachment of identity verification documents."

### その他の法律表現 — Other legal expressions

| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| ～に基づき | based on, pursuant to |
| ～の定めるところにより | in accordance with the provisions of |
| ～の場合において | in the event that |
| 前項の規定にかかわらず | notwithstanding the provisions of the preceding paragraph |
| 別途定める | to be separately determined |
| 速やかに | promptly |
| 遅滞なく | without delay |
| 直ちに | immediately |

Note the distinction between these three words for promptness:

> 速やかに: as soon as reasonably possible (some flexibility)
> 遅滞なく: without undue delay (less flexibility than 速やかに)
> 直ちに: immediately (no flexibility)

## 22.5 Reading Passage

次の文章は、日本の一般的なアパートの賃貸契約書の抜粋である。

---

賃貸借契約書

賃貸人（甲）　山本太郎
賃借人（乙）　リー・ウェイ

甲と乙は、以下の条項に基づき、下記物件の賃貸借契約を締結する。

第一条（物件の表示）
所在地：東京都杉並区高円寺南三丁目十二番五号　コーポ松風　二〇三号室
構造：鉄筋コンクリート造　三階建ての二階部分
間取り：1K（洋室六畳、キッチン三畳）
面積：二十五平方メートル

第二条（契約期間）
本契約の期間は、令和八年四月一日から令和十年三月三十一日までの二年間とする。

第三条（賃料等）
一、家賃：月額七万五千円
二、共益費：月額五千円
三、敷金：十五万円（家賃二ヶ月分）
四、礼金：七万五千円（家賃一ヶ月分）

第四条（賃料の支払い）
乙は、毎月二十八日までに翌月分の家賃および共益費を、甲の指定する銀行口座に振り込む方法により支払うものとする。振込手数料は乙の負担とする。

第五条（禁止事項）
乙は、甲の書面による承諾なく、以下の行為をしてはならない。
一、本物件の全部または一部を第三者に転貸すること
二、本物件の増築、改築、または構造を変更すること
三、本物件において動物を飼育すること

第六条（原状回復）
乙は、本物件を退去する際、通常の使用に伴う損耗を除き、自己の費用にて原状回復を行うものとする。原状回復の範囲については、国土交通省のガイドラインに準じるものとする。

第七条（解約）
乙が本契約を期間満了前に解約しようとする場合は、少なくとも一ヶ月前までに書面にて甲に通知しなければならない。ただし、解約の申し出から一ヶ月分の家賃に相当する違約金を甲に支払うことにより、即時解約することができる。

第八条（契約の解除）
乙が以下のいずれかに該当する場合、甲は催告なく本契約を解除することができる。
一、家賃を三ヶ月以上滞納した場合
二、第五条の禁止事項に違反した場合
三、その他、信頼関係を著しく破壊する行為があった場合

---

**Translation:**

Lease Contract

Lessor (Party A): Yamamoto Taro
Lessee (Party B): Lee Wei

Party A and Party B hereby conclude the following lease contract for the property described below, based on the following provisions.

Article 1 (Property Description)
Location: Corpo Matsukaze, Room 203, 3-12-5 Koenji-Minami, Suginami-ku, Tokyo
Structure: Reinforced concrete, second floor of a three-story building
Layout: 1K (Western-style room 6 tatami, kitchen 3 tatami)
Area: 25 square meters

Article 2 (Contract Period)
The period of this contract shall be two years, from April 1, Reiwa 8 to March 31, Reiwa 10.

Article 3 (Rent, etc.)
1. Rent: 75,000 yen per month
2. Common area fee: 5,000 yen per month
3. Security deposit: 150,000 yen (two months' rent)
4. Key money: 75,000 yen (one month's rent)

Article 4 (Payment of Rent)
The lessee shall pay the following month's rent and common area fee by the 28th of each month by bank transfer to the bank account designated by the lessor. Transfer fees shall be borne by the lessee.

Article 5 (Prohibited Acts)
The lessee shall not engage in the following acts without the written consent of the lessor:
1. Subleasing all or part of the property to a third party
2. Expanding, remodeling, or altering the structure of the property
3. Keeping animals on the property

Article 6 (Restoration to Original Condition)
The lessee shall, upon vacating the property, restore it to its original condition at their own expense, excluding wear and tear from normal use. The scope of restoration shall conform to the guidelines of the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism.

Article 7 (Cancellation)
In the event that the lessee wishes to cancel this contract before the end of the contract period, the lessee must notify the lessor in writing at least one month in advance. However, the lessee may cancel immediately by paying the lessor a penalty equivalent to one month's rent from the date of the cancellation request.

Article 8 (Termination of Contract)
In the event that the lessee falls under any of the following, the lessor may terminate this contract without notice:
1. Failure to pay rent for three or more months
2. Violation of the prohibited acts in Article 5
3. Any other act that significantly destroys the relationship of trust
