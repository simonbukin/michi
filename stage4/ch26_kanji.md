# Chapter 26 — Kanji: The Road to 1,000

By this point in your studies, you have encountered several hundred kanji through the vocabulary and reading passages of Stages 1 through 4. This chapter takes a systematic approach to expanding your kanji knowledge toward the one-thousand mark by organizing kanji into semantic clusters, examining how kanji illuminate the meaning of compound verbs, addressing commonly misread kanji and the special category of 熟字訓, and developing strategies for handling kanji with multiple readings.

The goal is not rote memorization but pattern recognition. When you see an unfamiliar compound, you should be able to make an educated guess about its meaning based on the components you recognize. When you encounter a kanji with multiple possible readings, you should have strategies for determining which one applies. This chapter gives you the tools to do both.

## 26.2 Kanji in Compound Verbs

Compound verbs (複合動詞) combine two verbs into one. The kanji used in these verbs often make the meaning transparent — if you know the individual kanji, you can understand the compound.

### Patterns with 見

| Compound verb | Reading | Literal kanji meaning | Actual meaning |
|--------------|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| 見つける | みつける | see + attach | to find, to discover |
| 見つかる | みつかる | see + attach (intransitive) | to be found |
| 見上げる | みあげる | see + raise | to look up at |
| 見下ろす | みおろす | see + lower | to look down on |
| 見直す | みなおす | see + redo | to review, to reconsider |
| 見落とす | みおとす | see + drop | to overlook, to miss |
| 見守る | みまもる | see + protect | to watch over |
| 見慣れる | みなれる | see + accustom | to be used to seeing |

> 母親は子どもたちが遊ぶ姿を見守っていた。
> "The mother was watching over her children as they played."

> 大事なポイントを見落としていたことに気づいた。
> "I realized I had overlooked an important point."

### Patterns with 取

| Compound verb | Reading | Literal kanji meaning | Actual meaning |
|--------------|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| 取り出す | とりだす | take + put out | to take out, to extract |
| 取り上げる | とりあげる | take + raise | to take up (a topic), to confiscate |
| 取り消す | とりけす | take + erase | to cancel, to retract |
| 取り替える | とりかえる | take + exchange | to replace, to swap |
| 取り組む | とりくむ | take + grapple | to tackle, to work on |
| 取り戻す | とりもどす | take + return | to take back, to recover |
| 取り除く | とりのぞく | take + remove | to remove, to eliminate |

> 新しい課題に取り組むためには、まず基本に立ち返る必要がある。
> "In order to tackle a new challenge, it is first necessary to return to the basics."

> 発言を取り消すことはできませんよ。
> "You cannot retract what you said."

### Patterns with 引

| Compound verb | Reading | Literal kanji meaning | Actual meaning |
|--------------|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| 引き受ける | ひきうける | pull + receive | to take on (responsibility) |
| 引き起こす | ひきおこす | pull + cause | to cause, to bring about |
| 引き出す | ひきだす | pull + put out | to draw out, to withdraw |
| 引き返す | ひきかえす | pull + return | to turn back |
| 引っ越す | ひっこす | pull + cross | to move (residence) |
| 引き延ばす | ひきのばす | pull + stretch | to postpone, to stretch out |

> その発言が大きな問題を引き起こした。
> "That remark caused a major problem."

> 彼は責任を引き受けると言った。
> "He said he would take on the responsibility."

### Patterns with 打

| Compound verb | Reading | Literal kanji meaning | Actual meaning |
|--------------|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| 打ち合わせる | うちあわせる | strike + put together | to have a meeting, to arrange |
| 打ち明ける | うちあける | strike + open | to confess, to reveal |
| 打ち切る | うちきる | strike + cut | to cut off, to discontinue |

## 26.3 Common Misread Kanji and 熟字訓

Some kanji compounds have readings that cannot be predicted from the individual characters. These are called 熟字訓 (じゅくじくん): readings assigned to the compound as a whole, rather than to each character independently.

### Essential 熟字訓

| 漢字表記 | 読み | 意味 | Why it is unpredictable |
|---------|------|------|------------------------|
| 大人 | おとな | adult | 大 is normally ダイ/おお, 人 is ジン/ひと |
| 今日 | きょう | today | 今 is コン/いま, 日 is ニチ/ひ |
| 明日 | あした/あす | tomorrow | 明 is メイ/あか, 日 is ニチ/ひ |
| 昨日 | きのう | yesterday | 昨 is サク, 日 is ニチ/ひ |
| 一人 | ひとり | one person | 一 is イチ/ひと, but ひとり as a unit reading |
| 二人 | ふたり | two people | 二 is ニ/ふた, but ふたり as a unit reading |
| 今朝 | けさ | this morning | 今 is コン/いま, 朝 is チョウ/あさ |
| 果物 | くだもの | fruit | 果 is カ, 物 is ブツ/もの |
| 土産 | みやげ | souvenir | 土 is ド/つち, 産 is サン/う-む |
| 大和 | やまと | Yamato (Japan) | 大 is ダイ/おお, 和 is ワ |
| 紅葉 | もみじ | autumn leaves | 紅 is コウ/べに, 葉 is ヨウ/は |
| 時雨 | しぐれ | late autumn rain | 時 is ジ/とき, 雨 is ウ/あめ |
| 田舎 | いなか | countryside | 田 is デン/た, 舎 is シャ |
| 梅雨 | つゆ | rainy season | 梅 is バイ/うめ, 雨 is ウ/あめ |
| 河原 | かわら | riverbed | 河 is カ/かわ, 原 is ゲン/はら |
| 眼鏡 | めがね | glasses | 眼 is ガン/め, 鏡 is キョウ/かがみ |
| 浴衣 | ゆかた | yukata (light kimono) | 浴 is ヨク, 衣 is イ |
| 雑魚 | ざこ | small fry | 雑 is ザツ, 魚 is ギョ/さかな |
| 素人 | しろうと | amateur | 素 is ソ/す, 人 is ジン/ひと |
| 玄人 | くろうと | expert, professional | 玄 is ゲン, 人 is ジン/ひと |

### How to handle 熟字訓

There is no trick for deriving these readings from the characters. They must be memorized as vocabulary items. The good news is that the number of common 熟字訓 is limited. The list above covers most of the ones you will encounter at the N2 level.

When you encounter a kanji compound that seems to have an impossible reading, check whether it is a 熟字訓. If so, simply learn it as a unit. Do not try to apply standard 音読み or 訓読み rules.

### Commonly misread non-熟字訓 words

Some words are not 熟字訓 but are still frequently misread because learners apply the wrong reading.

| 漢字 | Common misreading | Correct reading | Meaning |
|------|------------------|----------------|---------|
| 相殺 | そうさつ | そうさい | offset, cancellation |
| 早急 | そうきゅう | さっきゅう | urgent (そうきゅう is now also accepted) |
| 依存 | いぞん | いそん | dependence (いぞん is now also accepted) |
| 重複 | じゅうふく | ちょうふく | overlap (じゅうふく is now also accepted) |
| 貼付 | はりつけ | ちょうふ | pasting, affixing |
| 出納 | しゅつのう | すいとう | receipts and disbursements |
| 凡例 | ぼんれい | はんれい | explanatory notes, legend |
| 遵守 | そんしゅ | じゅんしゅ | compliance, observance |

Note: For several of these, the historically "incorrect" reading has become so widespread that dictionaries now accept both. The table shows the traditional correct reading first.

## 26.4 Multi-Reading Kanji: The Case of 生

The kanji 生 is perhaps the most notorious example of a character with numerous readings. Understanding how to determine which reading applies requires context awareness and knowledge of common patterns.

### Readings of 生

| Reading | Type | Meaning | Common examples |
|---------|------|---------|----------------|
| セイ | 音読み | life, living | 生活、人生、生命、学生、先生 |
| ショウ | 音読み | life, birth | 一生、誕生、生涯 |
| い-きる | 訓読み | to live | 生きる、生き方 |
| い-かす | 訓読み | to make use of | 生かす、経験を生かす |
| う-まれる | 訓読み | to be born | 生まれる、生まれ |
| う-む | 訓読み | to give birth | 生む、卵を生む |
| は-える | 訓読み | to grow (plants) | 生える、草が生える |
| は-やす | 訓読み | to grow (transitive) | 生やす、ひげを生やす |
| なま | 訓読み | raw, uncooked | 生ビール、生卵、生魚 |
| き | 訓読み | pure, unbleached | 生地（きじ）|
| お-う | 訓読み | to grow | 生い立ち |

### Strategies for determining the reading

**Strategy 1: Compound type**

If 生 appears in a two-kanji Sino-Japanese compound, it is usually read セイ or ショウ.

| Compound | Reading | Rule |
|----------|---------|------|
| 生活 | せいかつ | 音読み + 音読み = セイ |
| 学生 | がくせい | 音読み + 音読み = セイ |
| 誕生 | たんじょう | 音読み + 音読み = ショウ |
| 一生 | いっしょう | 音読み + 音読み = ショウ |
| 生涯 | しょうがい | 音読み + 音読み = ショウ |

How to distinguish セイ from ショウ? There is no perfect rule, but a rough pattern: when the compound relates to daily life, livelihood, or living things, セイ is more common (生活, 生物, 人生). When the compound relates to birth, a lifetime as a whole, or a fundamental nature, ショウ is more common (誕生, 一生, 生涯).

**Strategy 2: Okurigana**

If 生 is followed by hiragana (okurigana), the reading is a 訓読み. The okurigana itself tells you which one.

| Word | Okurigana | Reading |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 生きる | きる | い-きる |
| 生まれる | まれる | う-まれる |
| 生える | える | は-える |
| 生む | む | う-む |
| 生かす | かす | い-かす |

**Strategy 3: Position and context for なま**

When 生 appears before a food or material and means "raw" or "unprocessed," it is read なま.

> 生ビール (なまビール) — draft beer
> 生卵 (なまたまご) — raw egg
> 生放送 (なまほうそう) — live broadcast
> 生演奏 (なまえんそう) — live performance

**Strategy 4: Memorize the exceptions**

Some readings of 生 in specific compounds simply need to be memorized.

> 生い立ち (おいたち) — upbringing
> 生地 (きじ) — fabric, dough
> 芝生 (しばふ) — lawn
> 生業 (なりわい) — livelihood

### Other multi-reading kanji

生 is extreme, but other kanji also have multiple readings that require context awareness.

| 漢字 | Readings | Key examples |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 下 | カ・ゲ・した・しも・もと・さ-げる・くだ-る・お-りる | 地下(ちか)、下手(へた)、下(した)、下す(くだす) |
| 上 | ジョウ・ショウ・うえ・かみ・あ-げる・のぼ-る | 以上(いじょう)、上手(じょうず)、上(うえ)、上る(のぼる) |
| 日 | ニチ・ジツ・ひ・か | 日本(にほん)、休日(きゅうじつ)、三日(みっか) |
| 人 | ジン・ニン・ひと | 日本人(にほんじん)、人数(にんずう)、人(ひと) |
| 行 | コウ・ギョウ・アン・い-く・ゆ-く・おこな-う | 銀行(ぎんこう)、行事(ぎょうじ)、行灯(あんどん)、行く(いく) |
