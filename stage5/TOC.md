# 日本語の道 — Stage 5 (N2→N1 Bridge): Table of Contents

## Scope and Targets

**Prerequisite:** Stages 1-4 complete (~6,000 vocab, ~1,000 kanji, N2 grammar, keigo productive command, onomatopoeia system, academic/journalistic/literary style basics)
**Duration:** ~200 hours | **Kanji:** ~1,500 cumulative | **Vocabulary:** ~8,000 cumulative
**New in this stage:** N1 grammar inventory, 四字熟語 (50-100), proverbs (30-50), classical Japanese reading competence, extended onomatopoeia, reading stamina (10+ page texts), manga/light novel/web register, unscripted native-speed listening, formal debate structure

This stage exists because the N2→N1 gap is the steepest cliff in Japanese learning and the most common plateau. The focus shifts from "learning new grammar" to "building stamina, speed, and range." The learner reads longer, listens faster, and encounters registers they've never seen in a textbook.

---

## Front Matter

- **Stage 5 Introduction** — The N2→N1 wall: why learners plateau here, what breaks through it (volume, range, stamina). Reading targets shift to 10+ pages per sitting. Listening targets shift to fully unscripted native-speed media. New grammar is lower-frequency but high-impact in formal/written contexts.
- **What You Should Already Know** — Checklist of Stage 4 prerequisites

## Part One: N1 Grammar — Formal and Literary

3.3 ～とあれば — "if it's for the sake of": 君のためとあれば何でもする
