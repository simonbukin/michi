# Appendix D — 四字熟語 Reference

This appendix lists four-character compound idioms (四字熟語・よじじゅくご) introduced in Stage 5 of 日本語の道, organized by thematic domain. Use it as a quick-reference when reading, writing, or preparing for advanced examinations.

四字熟語 are fixed expressions composed of four kanji characters, most originating from classical Chinese (漢文). They function as compact, highly expressive units of meaning and appear frequently in formal writing, speeches, news articles, literature, and proverbs. Mastery of 四字熟語 is a hallmark of advanced Japanese literacy: they allow a speaker to convey complex ideas — perseverance in the face of hardship, the irony of unintended consequences, the nuances of human character — in just four syllables of meaning. Many 四字熟語 carry moral, philosophical, or historical weight, and choosing the right one in context demonstrates both cultural fluency and rhetorical skill.

In the tables below, each entry includes the expression, its reading, a literal breakdown of meaning, the actual idiomatic meaning, and a usage example. A small number of well-known expressions that exceed four characters are included where they are traditionally grouped with 四字熟語 in reference materials; these are marked as (extended).

| 一生懸命 | いっしょうけんめい | one life, suspended on the edge | with all one's might; giving everything one has | 彼女は一生懸命に練習して、ついに優勝した。 |
| 七転八起 | しちてんはっき | seven falls, eight risings | perseverance through repeated failure; never giving up | 七転八起の精神で、何度失敗しても挑戦し続けた。 |

| 不撓不屈 | ふとうふくつ | unbending, unyielding | indomitable spirit; refusing to give in | 不撓不屈の意志で困難を乗り越えた。 |

| 猪突猛進 | ちょとつもうしん | boar's charge, fierce advance | reckless, headlong charge; rushing forward without thinking | 彼は猪突猛進な性格で、周りの意見を聞かない。 |
| 精力絶倫 | せいりょくぜつりん | energy, beyond compare | extraordinary vitality and stamina | あの選手は精力絶倫で、試合終盤でも全く疲れを見せない。 |

## Consequence & Causality

| 四字熟語 | 読み | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning | Usage Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 自業自得 | じごうじとく | own deed, own result | reaping what one has sown; getting one's just deserts | 勉強しなかったのだから、落第は自業自得だ。 |
| 因果応報 | いんがおうほう | cause and effect, response and reward | karmic retribution; actions inevitably produce consequences | 因果応報を信じて、日頃から善い行いを心がけている。 |
| 本末転倒 | ほんまつてんとう | root and tip, inverted | putting the cart before the horse; confusing means and ends | 健康のために運動して怪我するのは本末転倒だ。 |
| 自暴自棄 | じぼうじき | self-violence, self-abandonment | giving up on oneself in despair; self-destructive desperation | 失恋して自暴自棄になり、仕事も手につかなくなった。 |
| 栄枯盛衰 | えいこせいすい | flourish and wither, rise and fall | the inevitable cycle of rise and decline | 歴史は栄枯盛衰の繰り返しである。 |
| 盛者必衰 | じょうしゃひっすい | the prosperous, surely decline | all who prosper must eventually fall | 平家物語は盛者必衰の理を語っている。 |
| 一蓮托生 | いちれんたくしょう | one lotus, entrusted life | sharing the same fate for better or worse | チーム全員が一蓮托生の覚悟で試合に臨んだ。 |
| 禍福は糾える縄の如し | かふくはあざなえるなわのごとし | fortune and misfortune are like a twisted rope | (extended) good and bad fortune are intertwined and alternate unpredictably | 禍福は糾える縄の如しと言うように、苦しい時期の後に幸運が訪れた。 |
| 善因善果 | ぜんいんぜんか | good cause, good fruit | good deeds lead to good outcomes | 善因善果を信じて、地道に人助けを続けている。 |
| 悪因悪果 | あくいんあっか | bad cause, bad fruit | bad deeds lead to bad outcomes | 嘘をつき続けた結果、信用を失ったのは悪因悪果だ。 |
