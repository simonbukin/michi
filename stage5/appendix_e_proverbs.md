# Appendix E — Proverbs (ことわざ) Reference

This appendix collects Japanese proverbs (ことわざ) introduced and referenced in Stage 5 of 道, organized by theme. Use it as a quick-reference when reading, writing, or preparing arguments.

Japanese proverbs function as compressed arguments. Where English might require a full sentence of reasoning, a well-placed ことわざ can close a point, concede an objection, or reframe an entire discussion. In formal writing and speech, proverbs carry the weight of shared cultural authority — citing one is not decoration but a rhetorical move that signals the speaker's position within a long tradition of collective wisdom. Many derive from classical Chinese (漢文), Buddhist teaching, or centuries of folk observation, and their force depends on listeners recognizing the source. Mastering even a handful gives a learner access to a layer of argumentation that is difficult to replicate with grammar alone.

In the tables below, each entry provides the proverb in kanji, its full reading, a literal translation, its actual meaning in use, and typical contexts where it appears.

| ことわざ | 読み | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning | Context of Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 石の上にも三年 | いしのうえにもさんねん | Even sitting on a stone for three years [warms it] | Patience and perseverance pay off | Encouraging someone to stick with a difficult job, study, or training |
| 継続は力なり | けいぞくはちからなり | Continuation is power | Steady, sustained effort builds real strength | Motivational contexts — study habits, sports training, skill development |
| 千里の道も一歩から | せんりのみちもいっぽから | Even a thousand-mile road begins with one step | Every great achievement starts with a small beginning | Encouraging someone who feels overwhelmed by the scale of a task |
| 七転び八起き | ななころびやおき | Fall seven times, get up eight | No matter how many times you fail, keep getting back up | Resilience after repeated setbacks — exams, business failures, recovery |
| 雨垂れ石を穿つ | あまだれいしをうがつ | Dripping water bores through stone | Constant small efforts eventually overcome great obstacles | Praising long-term dedication; arguing against impatience |
| 急がば回れ | いそがばまわれ | If you hurry, take the roundabout path | Shortcuts often backfire; the steady route is faster in the end | Warning against cutting corners — project planning, test preparation |
| 塵も積もれば山となる | ちりもつもればやまとなる | Even dust, if it piles up, becomes a mountain | Small amounts accumulate into something significant | Saving money, daily practice, incremental improvement |
| 習うより慣れろ | ならうよりなれろ | Rather than learning, get accustomed | Hands-on experience beats theoretical study | Encouraging practice over textbook study — cooking, language, crafts |

|---|---|---|---|---|
