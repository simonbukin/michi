# Chapter 3 — Conditional and Circumstantial — Formal

This chapter covers five formal conditional and circumstantial expressions that appear in news writing, essays, formal speech, and literary prose. These patterns describe situations where a particular condition or circumstance leads to a natural or expected consequence.

Several of these expressions use the particle と in ways that go beyond basic conditional grammar. Others employ なし or なく to express the absence of something as a condition. What they share is a formal register and a sense of logical or natural connection between circumstance and outcome. Mastering these patterns is essential for reading editorials, formal commentary, and advanced literary texts.

All patterns in this chapter belong to formal or written registers unless otherwise noted.

---

## 3.1 ～ともなると / ～ともなれば — "When It Comes to the Level of"

ともなると and ともなれば express that when someone or something reaches a certain status or level, a particular consequence naturally follows. The implication is that the status is high or significant enough that the result is expected or inevitable.

Both forms are interchangeable in meaning. ともなると is slightly more common in modern writing.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + ともなると / ともなれば |
| Verb (dictionary form) + ともなると / ともなれば |

### Examples

> 社長　ともなると、責任が　非常に　重くなる。
> "When you reach the level of company president, the responsibilities become extremely heavy."

> 大学生　ともなれば、自分の　ことは　自分で　管理すべきだ。
> "Once you're at the level of a university student, you should manage your own affairs."

> 師走　ともなると、どこの　商店街も　賑やかに　なる。
> "When it comes to December, every shopping street becomes lively."

> プロ　ともなれば、体調管理は　基本中の　基本だ。
> "When you're at the professional level, managing your physical condition is the most fundamental of basics."

> 十年も　働く　ともなると、見える　景色が　変わってくる。
> "When you've worked for as long as ten years, the view you see starts to change."

### ともなると vs ともなれば

| Expression | Nuance |
|---|---|
| ともなると | "when it comes to" — describes what naturally happens |
| ともなれば | "if it comes to" — slightly more hypothetical tone |

In practice, the difference is minimal and both are used interchangeably.

---

## 3.2 ～とあって — "Given That"

とあって presents a reason or circumstance that explains a subsequent situation. It is similar to "given that" or "since" in English, but with the added nuance that the circumstance is notable or special and the result is therefore natural or expected.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Plain form clause + とあって |
| Noun + とあって |

### Examples

> 三連休　とあって、高速道路は　大渋滞だ。
> "Given that it's a three-day weekend, the expressway is heavily congested."

> 人気歌手の　コンサート　とあって、チケットは　即日　完売した。
> "Given that it was a concert by a popular singer, tickets sold out the same day."

> 最後の　試合　とあって、選手たちの　目の色が　違う。
> "Given that it's the final game, the look in the players' eyes is different."

> 初めての　海外旅行　とあって、彼女は　何週間も　前から　準備していた。
> "Given that it was her first trip abroad, she had been preparing for weeks in advance."

> 無料　とあって、会場には　長い　行列が　できていた。
> "Given that it was free, a long line had formed at the venue."

### Usage note

とあって always describes an actual, real circumstance — not a hypothetical one. The situation is factual, and the result is presented as a natural or expected consequence of that fact. It is unsuitable for hypothetical scenarios; use とあれば (Section 3.3) for those.

---

## 3.3 ～とあれば — "If It's for the Sake of"

とあれば expresses a hypothetical or conditional willingness: "if it is for the sake of X" or "if the situation calls for X." It conveys that the speaker is willing to do whatever is necessary under the stated condition.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + とあれば |
| Plain form clause + とあれば |

### Examples

> 君の　ため　とあれば、何でも　する。
> "If it's for your sake, I'll do anything."

> 必要　とあれば、海外にも　飛んで　いく。
> "If the need arises, I'll fly overseas as well."

> 会社の　存続が　かかっている　とあれば、多少の　犠牲は　やむを得ない。
> "If the survival of the company is at stake, some sacrifices are unavoidable."

> 子供の　将来の　ため　とあれば、どんな　苦労も　厭わない。
> "If it's for the children's future, I won't shy away from any hardship."

> お客様の　ご要望　とあれば、特別に　対応いたします。
> "If it is the customer's request, we will provide special accommodation."

### とあって vs とあれば

| Expression | Meaning | Factual? |
|---|---|---|
| とあって | "given that" (reason for a real situation) | Yes — real circumstance |
| とあれば | "if it is for / if the situation calls for" | No — hypothetical or conditional |

This is a critical distinction. とあって looks backward at a fact. とあれば looks forward at a condition.

## 3.4 ～たりとも～ない — "Not Even (One)"

たりとも expresses an emphatic negative: "not even one (of something)." It is used with minimal quantity words (一秒, 一歩, 一瞬, 一円, etc.) to emphasize that absolutely zero deviation or exception is permitted.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Counter/quantity word + たりとも + Verb (negative) |

Note: The most common quantity words used with this pattern are 一瞬 (one moment), 一秒 (one second), 一歩 (one step), 一円 (one yen), 一人 (one person), and 一日 (one day).

### Examples

> 一瞬　たりとも　油断できない　状況だ。
> "It is a situation where you cannot let your guard down for even one moment."

> 一円　たりとも　無駄に　してはならない。
> "You must not waste even a single yen."

> 一歩　たりとも　ここから　動くな。
> "Don't move even one step from here."

> 一秒　たりとも　時間を　無駄にする　余裕は　ない。
> "There is no room to waste even a single second."

> 一人　たりとも　犠牲者を　出しては　ならない。
> "We must not produce even a single casualty."

> 一日　たりとも　練習を　休む　わけには　いかない。
> "I cannot afford to skip practice for even a single day."

### Usage note

たりとも is always used with negative predicates. The pattern emphasizes absolute strictness — not even the smallest unit is acceptable. It is formal and emphatic, appearing in serious contexts like military orders, corporate directives, and solemn declarations.

### Common collocations

| Quantity word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 一瞬たりとも | not even a moment | 一瞬たりとも目を離せない |
| 一円たりとも | not even one yen | 一円たりとも無駄にしない |
| 一歩たりとも | not even one step | 一歩たりとも譲らない |
| 一日たりとも | not even one day | 一日たりとも忘れない |
| 一人たりとも | not even one person | 一人たりとも見逃さない |

## 3.5 ～なしに(は) / ～なくして(は) — "Without"

なしに(は) and なくして(は) both mean "without" and express that a certain condition is indispensable — without it, the result cannot be achieved. They are formal alternatives to the more common なしで or なければ.

### Formation

| Pattern | Connection |
|---|---|
| Noun + なしに(は) | 努力なしには |
| Noun + なくして(は) | 努力なくしては |
| Verb (dictionary form) + ことなしに(は) | 経験することなしには |

### Examples

> 努力　なしには　成功は　あり得ない。
> "Without effort, success is impossible."

> 信頼　なくしては、人間関係は　成り立たない。
> "Without trust, human relationships cannot function."

> 国民の　協力　なしに、この改革は　実現できない。
> "Without the cooperation of the citizens, this reform cannot be realized."

> 犠牲　なくして、自由は　得られなかった。
> "Freedom could not have been obtained without sacrifice."

> 過去を　振り返る　こと　なしに、未来を　語ることは　できない。
> "Without looking back on the past, you cannot speak of the future."

### なしに(は) vs なくして(は)

| Expression | Nuance |
|---|---|
| なしに(は) | "without (the existence of)" — slightly more concrete |
| なくして(は) | "without (the act/state of)" — slightly more abstract, more literary |

Both are formal and largely interchangeable. なくして(は) has a slightly more elevated, literary ring. The addition of は makes the statement more emphatic: 努力なしに成功した ("succeeded without effort" — descriptive) vs 努力なしには成功はない ("without effort, there is no success" — emphatic/conditional).

### Comparison with similar expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Register |
|---|---|---|
| ～なしに(は) | "without" (indispensable condition) | Formal |
| ～なくして(は) | "without" (indispensable condition) | Formal / literary |
| ～なしで | "without" (simple absence) | Neutral |
| ～なければ | "if not" (conditional) | Neutral |
| ～ずに(は) | "without doing" (verb only) | Formal |

The key difference is that なしに(は) and なくして(は) emphasize that the absent element is indispensable, whereas なしで simply describes the absence without that emphasis.

## 3.6 Reading Passage

次の文章は、ある地方都市の観光に関する雑誌記事です。

---

人口わずか三万人の小さな町、月見町。それが今、全国から観光客を集めている。

きっかけは、三年前に始まった「月見祭り」だ。地元の若手起業家たちが企画したこの祭りは、伝統的な月見の風習と現代アートを融合させたユニークなイベントである。初年度から話題を呼び、今年は三日間で十万人を動員した。

無料で参加できるとあって、家族連れの来場者が特に多い。子供たちが一瞬たりとも飽きないよう、体験型の展示が数多く用意されている。

町長ともなると、この祭りへの思い入れも格別だ。「住民の協力なくしては、ここまでの成功はあり得ませんでした」と語る。実際、住民の九割以上がボランティアとして何らかの形で関わっているという。

完璧ではないまでも、地方創生の一つの成功モデルとして、月見町は注目に値する。大都市のような予算がなくとも、アイデアと情熱があれば、町は変われるのだ。

子供のためとあれば何でもするという親たちの姿勢が、この町の活気を支えている。観光客が増えたことで、若者のＵターンも増えつつある。空き家だった建物が次々とカフェや工房に生まれ変わり、一日たりとも退屈しない町になりつつある。

住民の結束なくしては、このような変化は起こり得なかっただろう。過疎化に悩む他の自治体にとって、月見町の取り組みは参考にすべき事例であるに相違ない。

---

**Translation:**

The following is a magazine article about tourism in a small regional town.

---

Tsukimi Town, a small town with a population of only thirty thousand. And yet it is now attracting tourists from across the country.

The catalyst was the "Tsukimi Festival," which began three years ago. Planned by local young entrepreneurs, this festival is a unique event that fuses the traditional custom of moon viewing with contemporary art. It attracted attention from its first year, and this year it drew one hundred thousand visitors over three days.

Given that participation is free, visitors with families are particularly numerous. Interactive exhibits are prepared in great numbers so that children do not grow bored for even a moment.

When you're the town mayor, the emotional investment in this festival is exceptional. "Without the cooperation of the residents, this level of success would have been impossible," he says. In fact, over ninety percent of residents are said to be involved in some capacity as volunteers.

Even if not perfect, Tsukimi Town is worthy of attention as one successful model of regional revitalization. Even without a budget like that of a major city, if there are ideas and passion, a town can change.

The attitude of parents who will do anything if it is for their children's sake supports the vitality of this town. As tourists have increased, the number of young people returning to the area has also been growing. Buildings that had been vacant are being reborn one after another as cafes and workshops, and the town is becoming a place where you would never be bored for even a single day.

Without the unity of the residents, such a transformation could not have occurred. For other municipalities struggling with depopulation, Tsukimi Town's efforts are undoubtedly an example worth studying.
