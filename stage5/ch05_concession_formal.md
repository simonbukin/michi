# Chapter 5 — Concession: Formal

In Stage 4 you encountered concessive expressions such as ～ものの, ～にもかかわらず, and ～といえども. Those forms ranged from neutral literary to formal. This chapter pushes deeper into the formal and literary register, introducing five concessive patterns that appear in academic writing, legal documents, speeches, and classical-flavored prose. These are expressions that educated native speakers recognize instantly but that many textbooks never explain clearly.

What unites these five patterns is their shared territory: all of them say, in one way or another, "even if X is the case, Y follows" or "despite X, Y." But each brings its own texture. ～であれ carries the gravity of legal or philosophical argument. ～(よ)うが is blunt and defiant. ～とて belongs to literary Japanese and sounds archaic in conversation. ～ところを shifts the focus to the moment of interruption or imposition. And ～をよそに conveys pointed criticism of someone's indifference.

Mastering these forms is not about replacing what you already know — it is about adding the precise shade of meaning that the situation demands. When you read a newspaper editorial or a formal speech, these are the forms that carry its authority.

---

## 5.1 ～であれ / ～であろうと — "Even If" (Formal)

～であれ and ～であろうと are formal concessive expressions meaning "even if (something) is" or "whether it be." They are used to state that a particular condition does not change the conclusion. Both forms carry a tone of principled assertion — the kind you find in laws, rules, speeches, and editorials.

～であれ derives from the classical copula with the imperative/concessive form of あり. ～であろうと uses the volitional form of である followed by the quotative と, creating the sense of "even supposing it is."

### Formation

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Noun + であれ | 誰であれ |
| Noun + であろうと | 何であろうと |
| なadj stem + であれ | 有名であれ |
| Repeated noun + であれ...であれ | 男であれ女であれ |

Note: These forms attach almost exclusively to nouns and な-adjective stems. They are not used with verbs or い-adjectives directly.

### Examples

> 誰であれ　例外では　ない。
> "No one is an exception, whoever they may be."

> 理由が　何であれ、暴力は　許されない。
> "Whatever the reason may be, violence is not permitted."

> 国籍が　何であろうと、この法律は　すべての　住民に　適用される。
> "Whatever one's nationality, this law applies to all residents."

> 男であれ　女であれ、能力に　応じて　評価されるべきだ。
> "Whether man or woman, people should be evaluated according to their ability."

> いかに　有名であろうと、ルールを　守らなければならない。
> "However famous one may be, one must follow the rules."

> 結果が　どうであれ、全力を　尽くしたことに　意味が　ある。
> "Whatever the result may be, there is meaning in having given one's all."

### ～であれ...であれ (Paired Pattern)

When used in a paired construction (A であれ B であれ), the meaning shifts to "whether A or B":

> 賛成であれ　反対であれ、まず　議論すべきだ。
> "Whether for or against, we should discuss it first."

### Register

Both ～であれ and ～であろうと are formal. They would sound stiff in casual conversation. In speech, ～でも or ～だろうと would be the natural equivalents.

## 5.2 ～(よ)うが / ～(よ)うと — "No Matter"

～(よ)うが and ～(よ)うと express strong concession: "no matter what happens" or "regardless of." The speaker declares that no condition in the first clause will alter the outcome in the second clause. The tone is resolute, sometimes defiant.

These forms use the volitional form of verbs (or the volitional-equivalent of adjectives and copula), followed by が or と.

### Formation

| Preceding element | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Godan verb (volitional) + が/と | 言おうが / 言おうと |
| Ichidan verb (volitional) + が/と | 食べようが / 食べようと |
| い-adjective (-かろう) + が/と | 高かろうが / 高かろうと |
| な-adjective + だろう/であろう + が/と | 静かだろうが / 静かであろうが |
| Noun + だろう/であろう + が/と | 社長だろうが / 社長であろうが |
| Interrogative + ～(よ)うが/と | 何を言おうが / どうなろうと |

### Examples

> 何を　言おうが　聞かない。
> "No matter what you say, I will not listen."

> どんなに　努力しようと、才能が　なければ　限界が　ある。
> "No matter how hard you try, without talent there are limits."

> 雨が　降ろうが　風が　吹こうが、毎朝　走る。
> "Whether it rains or the wind blows, I run every morning."

> 値段が　高かろうが、品質が　良ければ　買う。
> "No matter how expensive it is, if the quality is good I will buy it."

> 相手が　誰であろうと、自分の　意見は　はっきり　言う。
> "No matter who the other person is, I state my opinion clearly."

> どこへ　行こうと　君の　自由だ。
> "Wherever you go, that is your freedom."

### ～(よ)うが vs ～ても

Both express "even if," but ～(よ)うが is stronger and more resolute. Compare:

| Expression | Tone |
|---|---|
| 雨が降っても行く | "Even if it rains, I'll go." (Neutral) |
| 雨が降ろうが行く | "Rain or not, I'm going." (Defiant, resolved) |

## 5.3 ～とて — "Even" (Literary)

～とて is an archaic and literary concessive particle meaning "even if" or "even though." It survives in written Japanese, proverbs, and formal speeches. In modern conversational Japanese it sounds deliberately old-fashioned, but in writing it adds a note of gravitas or poetic weight.

Historically, とて combines the quotative と with the conjunctive particle て. It is close in meaning to ～ても or ～としても but belongs to a higher register.

### Formation

| Preceding element | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Verb (た form) + とて | 練習したとて |
| Verb (plain) + とて | 行くとて |
| い-adjective + とて | 若いとて |
| Noun + (だ) + とて | 教師とて / 教師だとて |

### Examples

> 百回　練習したとて、本番で　失敗することも　ある。
> "Even if you practice a hundred times, you may still fail in the real performance."

> 医者とて、すべての　病気を　治せるわけでは　ない。
> "Even a doctor cannot cure every illness."

> 若いとて、油断は　禁物だ。
> "Even if you are young, carelessness is forbidden."

> いくら　悔やんだとて、過去は　変えられない。
> "No matter how much you regret it, you cannot change the past."

> 一日や二日　休んだとて、仕事に　大きな　影響は　ない。
> "Even if you take a day or two off, there will be no major impact on the work."

### ～とて vs ～たところで

Both express "even if" with a nuance of futility, but they differ in register and tone:

| Expression | Register | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| ～とて | Literary, archaic | Dignified concession |
| ～たところで | Neutral-formal | Dismissive futility |

---

## 5.4 ～ところを — "When (in the Act of), Despite"

～ところを is a versatile expression with two main uses: (1) "at the moment when" — indicating interruption of an ongoing situation, and (2) "despite the fact that" — used in polite formulas to acknowledge an imposition on someone. It literally means "the situation/point of... (and yet)."

### Formation

| Preceding element | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Verb (ている) + ところを | 寝ているところを |
| Verb (plain) + ところを | 帰るところを |
| い-adjective + ところを | お忙しいところを |
| な-adjective + な + ところを | お元気なところを |

### Examples

**Interruption ("right when"):**

> 寝ている　ところを　起こされた。
> "I was woken up while I was sleeping."

> 逃げようと　した　ところを　捕まった。
> "He was caught just as he was trying to escape."

> 犯人が　証拠を　隠そうと　している　ところを　発見された。
> "The criminal was discovered in the act of trying to hide evidence."

**Polite imposition ("despite/I know you are..."):**

> お忙しい　ところを　恐縮ですが、少し　お時間を　いただけますか。
> "I am sorry to bother you when you are busy, but could I have a moment of your time?"

> お休みの　ところを　申し訳ございません。
> "I apologize for disturbing you on your day off."

> お楽しみの　ところを　失礼いたします。
> "Pardon me for interrupting your enjoyment."

### Two Faces of ～ところを

| Use | Meaning | Common context |
|---|---|---|
| Interruption | "right when, in the act of" | Narrative, news reports |
| Polite formula | "despite (your situation), I impose" | Business, formal requests |

The polite use is extremely common in business Japanese. Memorizing set phrases like お忙しいところを恐縮ですが is essential for professional communication.

## 5.5 ～をよそに — "Indifferent to, Ignoring"

～をよそに expresses that someone acts without regard for something — typically the feelings, opinions, or situation of others. The nuance is critical: the speaker judges this indifference negatively. It implies that the subject should have paid attention but did not.

よそ means "elsewhere" or "other," so をよそに literally suggests treating something as if it belongs to someone else, as if it is not one's concern.

### Formation

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Noun + をよそに | 心配をよそに |
| Noun phrase + をよそに | 周囲の反対をよそに |

Note: ～をよそに attaches to noun phrases. It does not attach directly to verbs.

### Examples

> 周囲の　心配を　よそに、彼は　一人で　旅に　出た。
> "Indifferent to the concerns of those around him, he set out on a trip alone."

> 親の　反対を　よそに、彼女は　俳優に　なる　道を　選んだ。
> "Ignoring her parents' opposition, she chose the path of becoming an actress."

> 国民の　不安を　よそに、政府は　計画を　強行した。
> "Ignoring the public's anxiety, the government pushed the plan through."

> 周りの　期待を　よそに、彼は　あっさり　辞退した。
> "Ignoring the expectations of those around him, he casually declined."

> 世間の　批判を　よそに、その　企業は　方針を　変えなかった。
> "Indifferent to public criticism, the company did not change its policy."

### ～をよそに vs ～にもかかわらず

Both can translate as "despite," but the nuance differs significantly:

| Expression | Focus | Judgment |
|---|---|---|
| ～にもかかわらず | Objective contrast | Neutral |
| ～をよそに | Deliberate indifference | Critical |

～をよそに always implies that the subject is choosing to ignore something they should care about.

## 5.6 Comparison Table

| Expression | Meaning | Register | Key nuance |
|---|---|---|---|
| ～であれ / ～であろうと | "even if it be" | Formal | Principled, universal statement |
| ～(よ)うが / ～(よ)うと | "no matter" | Formal-defiant | Resolute, unyielding |
| ～とて | "even if" | Literary, archaic | Dignified, poetic weight |
| ～ところを | "when / despite" | Polite-formal | Interruption or polite imposition |
| ～をよそに | "ignoring" | Written, critical | Negative judgment of indifference |

## 5.7 Reading Passage

次の文章は、ある大学教授が卒業式で行ったスピーチの一部です。

---

本日は、お忙しいところを多くの保護者の皆さまにご出席いただき、心より御礼申し上げます。

卒業生の皆さん、おめでとうございます。四年間の大学生活は、楽しいことばかりではなかったでしょう。困難があろうと、不安があろうと、皆さんは最後まで歩み続けました。

世の中には、努力したとて報われないこともあります。百回挑戦したとて、成功が保証されるわけではありません。しかし、結果がどうであれ、挑戦し続けたという事実そのものに価値があるのです。

これから皆さんは社会に出ます。周囲の不安をよそに、自信を持って自分の道を進んでください。ただし、それは周りの声を無視するということではありません。親であれ友人であれ、あなたを支えてくれた人々への感謝を忘れないでいただきたい。

どんな困難に直面しようと、皆さんには乗り越える力があります。それを信じて、一歩を踏み出してください。

---

**Translation:**

Today, I wish to express my heartfelt gratitude to the many parents who have attended despite your busy schedules.

Congratulations to all the graduates. These four years of university life were surely not all enjoyable. Whether there were difficulties or anxieties, you all continued walking until the end.

In this world, there are times when even effort goes unrewarded. Even if you challenge something a hundred times, success is not guaranteed. However, whatever the result may be, there is value in the very fact that you kept challenging yourself.

From now on, you will enter society. Undaunted by the worries of those around you, please move forward on your own path with confidence. However, that does not mean ignoring the voices around you. Whether parents or friends, please do not forget your gratitude to those who supported you.

No matter what difficulties you face, you have the strength to overcome them. Believe in that, and take the first step.
