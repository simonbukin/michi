# Chapter 7 — 四字熟語: Four-Character Compounds

Every language has its compressed wisdom — phrases that pack layers of meaning into a few syllables. English has idioms like "a wolf in sheep's clothing" or "the tip of the iceberg." Japanese has 四字熟語 (よじじゅくご): four-character compounds drawn from classical Chinese, historical anecdotes, Buddhist philosophy, and centuries of Japanese literary tradition. These are not ornaments. They are tools. A single four-character compound can replace an entire sentence, and in formal writing, speeches, and editorials, they appear constantly.

At the N1 level, you are expected to recognize and understand a substantial number of 四字熟語. More importantly, you should understand their internal logic. Most 四字熟語 are not arbitrary sequences of characters — they are compressed arguments. Once you see how the four characters relate to each other (often as two pairs of two), the meaning becomes transparent and memorable.

This chapter introduces over sixty commonly tested 四字熟語, organized by theme. For each compound, you will find the reading, a breakdown of the literal meaning, the actual meaning in use, and an example sentence. The goal is not rote memorization but comprehension — understanding why each compound means what it means.

---

## 7.1 What 四字熟語 Are — Compressed Cultural Logic

A 四字熟語 is a fixed expression consisting of exactly four kanji. Most originate from classical Chinese texts (漢文) that were read and studied in Japan for over a thousand years. Others arose within Japanese literary tradition itself.

The internal structure usually follows one of these patterns:

| Structure | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| AB + CD | Two two-character words combined | 試行 (trial) + 錯誤 (error) = 試行錯誤 |
| A+B+C+D | Four characters in parallel or sequence | 起承転結 (introduction, development, turn, conclusion) |
| AB は CD | The first pair modifies or contrasts with the second | 本末 (root and tip) + 転倒 (inversion) = 本末転倒 |
| Narrative | Compressed story or image | 四面楚歌 (surrounded on four sides by Chu songs) |

Understanding this internal structure is the key to remembering them. When you see 自業自得, you can parse it as 自 (self) + 業 (deed) + 自 (self) + 得 (obtaining) — "you reap what you sow."

### How 四字熟語 Are Used

四字熟語 function grammatically as nouns. They can be:

- Used with だ/です: あの人の行動は傍若無人だ。
- Used with な to modify nouns: 臨機応変な対応
- Used with に as adverbs: 独断専行に進める
- Used with する if the meaning allows: 試行錯誤する
- Used as stand-alone comments: まさに自業自得。

## 7.9 Reading Passage

次の文章は、ある新聞の社説です。

---

現代の日本社会は、かつてないほどの変化の中にある。グローバル化が進み、旧来の価値観だけでは対応できない場面が増えている。こうした時代に必要なのは、温故知新の姿勢——過去の知恵を大切にしながらも、新しい発想を取り入れる柔軟性だ。

しかし現実を見ると、因循姑息な対応に終始する組織が少なくない。朝令暮改のように方針がころころ変わる企業もあれば、本末転倒な施策に予算を費やす自治体もある。指導者が独断専行で物事を進め、周囲の意見に耳を貸さない。結果として、社員や市民は右往左往させられるばかりだ。

一方で、希望の光もある。若い世代の中には、試行錯誤を恐れず、悪戦苦闘しながらも新しい事業を立ち上げる人々がいる。彼らは付和雷同を嫌い、自分の頭で考え、仲間と切磋琢磨しながら成長している。その姿勢は、まさに不撓不屈と呼ぶにふさわしい。

もちろん、失敗もある。しかし大器晩成という言葉が示すように、すぐに結果が出なくても焦る必要はない。一心不乱に取り組み続けることこそが、やがて起死回生の一手を生むのである。

我々に求められているのは、臨機応変な知恵と、言行一致の誠実さだ。四面楚歌の状況であっても、七転八起の精神を忘れなければ、道は必ず開ける。

---

**Translation:**

Contemporary Japanese society is in the midst of unprecedented change. Globalization is advancing, and situations that cannot be handled with old values alone are increasing. What is needed in such times is the attitude of learning from the past to understand the present — the flexibility to value the wisdom of the past while incorporating new ideas.

However, looking at reality, there are not a few organizations that persist in conventional stopgap measures. There are companies whose policies change constantly, and local governments that spend budgets on measures that have their priorities backwards. Leaders push things forward on their own authority without lending an ear to the opinions of those around them. As a result, employees and citizens are left running around in confusion.

On the other hand, there are also rays of hope. Among the younger generation, there are people who are not afraid of trial and error and who launch new ventures while struggling desperately. They dislike following others blindly, think with their own heads, and grow through friendly competition with their peers. Their attitude truly deserves to be called indomitable.

Of course, there are failures too. But as the saying "great talents mature slowly" suggests, there is no need to rush even if results do not come immediately. Continuing to work with total concentration is precisely what eventually produces the miraculous comeback move.

What is demanded of us is the wisdom of flexible adaptation and the sincerity of practicing what we preach. Even in a situation of total isolation, if we do not forget the spirit of perseverance through repeated failure, the path will surely open.
