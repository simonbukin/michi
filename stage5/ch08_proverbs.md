# Chapter 8 — ことわざ: Proverbs

Proverbs are the distilled experience of generations. In Japanese, ことわざ (諺) carry a particular weight because they appear not only in casual conversation but in formal speeches, newspaper editorials, business meetings, and literature. A well-placed proverb can clinch an argument, soften criticism, or express a complex observation in a single breath. Unlike 四字熟語, which are compressed into four kanji and tend toward the literary, ことわざ are full sentences or phrases in ordinary Japanese — though many are centuries old and carry an unmistakably traditional flavor.

At the N1 level, you are expected to know the most common proverbs and, more importantly, to understand when and how they are used. A proverb is not just a piece of trivia — it is a rhetorical tool. Knowing the right proverb for the right moment marks you as someone who has gone beyond grammar and vocabulary into the deeper territory of cultural fluency.

This chapter introduces approximately forty proverbs organized by theme. For each proverb, you will find the reading, literal meaning, actual meaning, the context in which it is typically used, and an example sentence.

---

## 8.1 Why Proverbs Matter

Japanese speakers use proverbs to:

- **Justify a decision**: 石の上にも三年だから、もう少し頑張ろう。
- **Warn someone**: 猿も木から落ちるよ — even experts make mistakes.
- **Criticize indirectly**: 能ある鷹は爪を隠すって言うのに… — unlike someone who shows off.
- **Console**: 覆水盆に返らずだから、前を向こう — what's done is done.
- **Add authority to an argument**: As the proverb says...

Proverbs often appear in the pattern ～と言うように ("as the saying goes") or ～とはよく言ったものだ ("how well they put it when they said...").

## 8.2 Effort and Persistence

### 石の上にも三年（いしのうえにもさんねん）

- **Literal**: Even on a stone, three years (sitting on a cold stone for three years will warm it)
- **Meaning**: Patience and persistence pay off
- **Context**: Encouraging someone to stick with something difficult
- **Example**:

> 新しい　仕事に　慣れなくても、石の上にも　三年だ。続けていれば　必ず　できるようになる。
> "Even if you can't get used to the new job, remember: patience pays off. If you keep at it, you'll definitely be able to do it."

### 継続は力なり（けいぞくはちからなり）

- **Literal**: Continuation is power
- **Meaning**: Perseverance is strength; keep going and you will achieve
- **Context**: Motivational; often used in schools and sports
- **Example**:

> 毎日　少しずつでも　いい。継続は力なりだ。
> "Even a little bit every day is fine. Persistence is power."

### 千里の道も一歩から（せんりのみちもいっぽから）

- **Literal**: Even a road of a thousand ri starts with one step
- **Meaning**: Every great journey begins with a single step
- **Context**: Encouraging someone to start, however small the beginning
- **Example**:

> 論文を　書くのが　大変そうに　見えるかもしれないが、千里の道も　一歩からだ。まず　書き始めよう。
> "Writing the thesis may look daunting, but a journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. Let's start writing."

### 七転び八起き（ななころびやおき）

- **Literal**: Fall seven times, get up eight
- **Meaning**: Perseverance through repeated failure
- **Context**: Encouragement after setbacks
- **Example**:

> 何度　失敗しても　七転び八起きの　精神で　立ち上がる。
> "No matter how many times I fail, I get back up with the spirit of perseverance."

### 急がば回れ（いそがばまわれ）

- **Literal**: If you hurry, go around (take the longer route)
- **Meaning**: Haste makes waste; the safe way is the fast way
- **Context**: Advising against shortcuts
- **Example**:

> 急がば回れで、基礎から　しっかり　勉強した方が　いい。
> "As they say, haste makes waste — it's better to study the fundamentals thoroughly."

### 塵も積もれば山となる（ちりもつもればやまとなる）

- **Literal**: Even dust, when piled up, becomes a mountain
- **Meaning**: Many small efforts accumulate into something large
- **Context**: Encouraging small, consistent effort; also used about savings
- **Example**:

> 一日百円でも、塵も積もれば　山と　なる。一年で　三万六千円に　なる。
> "Even a hundred yen a day — little things add up. In a year, that's thirty-six thousand yen."

## 8.3 Social Wisdom

### 出る杭は打たれる（でるくいはうたれる）

- **Literal**: The nail that sticks out gets hammered down
- **Meaning**: Those who stand out attract criticism or punishment
- **Context**: Explaining conformity pressure; sometimes used critically about Japanese society
- **Example**:

> 出る杭は　打たれると　言うけど、それでも　自分の　意見を　言うべきだ。
> "They say the nail that sticks out gets hammered, but even so, you should state your opinion."

### 郷に入っては郷に従え（ごうにいってはごうにしたがえ）

- **Literal**: When you enter a village, follow the village
- **Meaning**: When in Rome, do as the Romans do
- **Context**: Advising cultural adaptation; common advice to travelers or new employees
- **Example**:

> 海外で　暮らすなら、郷に入っては　郷に従えだ。
> "If you're going to live abroad, when in Rome, do as the Romans do."

### 人の振り見て我が振り直せ（ひとのふりみてわがふりなおせ）

- **Literal**: Watch others' behavior and correct your own
- **Meaning**: Learn from others' mistakes and good examples
- **Context**: Self-improvement advice; observing behavior
- **Example**:

> あの先輩の　失敗を　見て、人の振り見て　我が振り直せと　思った。
> "Seeing that senior's mistake, I thought: learn from others' behavior and fix your own."

### 情けは人のためならず（なさけはひとのためならず）

- **Literal**: Compassion is not for others' sake
- **Meaning**: Kindness to others ultimately benefits yourself (often misunderstood as "kindness doesn't help others")
- **Context**: Encouraging altruism
- **Example**:

> 情けは人の　ためならずと　言うように、親切は　いつか　自分に　返ってくる。
> "As they say, kindness ultimately benefits yourself — your good deeds will come back to you someday."

### 長いものには巻かれろ（ながいものにはまかれろ）

- **Literal**: When it comes to long things, let yourself be wrapped up
- **Meaning**: Don't fight the powerful; go with the flow of authority
- **Context**: Pragmatic advice about power dynamics; sometimes said resignedly
- **Example**:

> 社長が　そう言うなら、長いものには　巻かれろだ。
> "If the president says so, don't fight the powers that be."

### 三人寄れば文殊の知恵（さんにんよればもんじゅのちえ）

- **Literal**: If three people gather, the wisdom of Monju (the bodhisattva of wisdom)
- **Meaning**: Two (or three) heads are better than one
- **Context**: Encouraging collaboration
- **Example**:

> 一人で　悩まないで。三人寄れば　文殊の知恵と　言うだろう。
> "Don't worry alone. They say three heads are better than one."

## 8.4 Caution and Humility

### 猿も木から落ちる（さるもきからおちる）

- **Literal**: Even monkeys fall from trees
- **Meaning**: Even experts make mistakes
- **Context**: Consoling someone after a failure; warning against overconfidence
- **Example**:

> 猿も木から　落ちるって　言うから、気を　つけてね。
> "They say even monkeys fall from trees, so be careful."

### 井の中の蛙大海を知らず（いのなかのかわずたいかいをしらず）

- **Literal**: The frog in the well does not know the great ocean
- **Meaning**: A person with limited experience has a narrow worldview
- **Context**: Criticizing narrow-mindedness; encouraging broader perspectives
- **Example**:

> 自分の　会社が　一番だと　思っているなら、井の中の蛙だ。
> "If you think your company is the best, you're a frog in a well."

### 能ある鷹は爪を隠す（のうあるたかはつめをかくす）

- **Literal**: The talented hawk hides its claws
- **Meaning**: Truly capable people do not show off
- **Context**: Praising modesty; criticizing boastfulness by contrast
- **Example**:

> 彼女は　目立たないが、能ある鷹は　爪を隠すタイプだ。
> "She doesn't stand out, but she's the type whose true talent stays hidden."

### 油断大敵（ゆだんたいてき）

- **Literal**: Carelessness is a great enemy
- **Meaning**: Never let your guard down
- **Context**: Warning against complacency
- **Example**:

> 試験に　受かったからと　油断大敵だ。次の　試験も　ある。
> "Don't let your guard down just because you passed. There's another exam."

### 灯台下暗し（とうだいもとくらし）

- **Literal**: It is dark at the base of the lighthouse
- **Meaning**: It is hard to see what is right under your nose
- **Context**: When the answer was close all along
- **Example**:

> 探していた　書類が　自分の　机の上に　あった。灯台下暗しだ。
> "The document I was looking for was on my own desk. It's darkest under the lighthouse."

### 弘法にも筆の誤り（こうぼうにもふでのあやまり）

- **Literal**: Even Koubou (the great calligrapher) makes brush errors
- **Meaning**: Even masters make mistakes (same concept as 猿も木から落ちる)
- **Context**: Consoling; slightly more literary
- **Example**:

> あの教授が　計算ミスとは。弘法にも　筆の誤りだ。
> "A calculation error from that professor? Even Homer nods."

## 8.5 Consequences

### 蒔かぬ種は生えぬ（まかぬたねははえぬ）

- **Literal**: Seeds you don't sow don't sprout
- **Meaning**: Nothing comes from nothing; you must make effort to get results
- **Context**: Criticizing laziness; encouraging action
- **Example**:

> 蒔かぬ種は　生えぬ。応募しなければ　受かることもない。
> "Seeds you don't sow don't sprout. If you don't apply, you can't be accepted either."

### 覆水盆に返らず（ふくすいぼんにかえらず）

- **Literal**: Spilled water does not return to the tray
- **Meaning**: What is done cannot be undone (no use crying over spilled milk)
- **Context**: Accepting an irreversible situation
- **Example**:

> 後悔しても　覆水盆に返らずだ。これからの　ことを　考えよう。
> "Regretting won't help — what's done is done. Let's think about what comes next."

### 泣きっ面に蜂（なきっつらにはち）

- **Literal**: A bee on a crying face
- **Meaning**: When it rains, it pours; misfortune piled on misfortune
- **Context**: Commiserating when bad luck continues
- **Example**:

> 財布を　落とした上に　雨に降られた。泣きっ面に蜂だ。
> "I dropped my wallet and then got caught in the rain. When it rains, it pours."

### 身から出た錆（みからでたさび）

- **Literal**: Rust that came from one's own body (from the blade)
- **Meaning**: You brought this trouble on yourself
- **Context**: Similar to 自業自得; pointing out self-caused trouble
- **Example**:

> 約束を　破ったのだから、信頼を　失ったのは　身から出た錆だ。
> "Since you broke your promise, losing people's trust is your own fault."

### 因果は巡る（いんがはめぐる）

- **Literal**: Cause and effect go around
- **Meaning**: What goes around comes around
- **Context**: Karmic justice; consequence of past actions
- **Example**:

> 因果は巡る　ものだ。人に　優しくしていれば、いつか　自分にも　返ってくる。
> "What goes around comes around. If you are kind to others, it will come back to you someday."

## 8.6 Relationships

### 類は友を呼ぶ（るいはともをよぶ）

- **Literal**: Like calls friend
- **Meaning**: Birds of a feather flock together
- **Context**: Observing that similar people gather
- **Example**:

> 類は友を呼ぶと　言うが、あの二人が　仲良くなったのは　納得だ。
> "Birds of a feather flock together — it makes sense that those two became friends."

### 親しき仲にも礼儀あり（したしきなかにもれいぎあり）

- **Literal**: Even in close relationships, there is etiquette
- **Meaning**: Maintain proper manners even with close friends
- **Context**: Reminding someone not to be too casual
- **Example**:

> いくら　親友でも、親しき仲にも　礼儀ありだ。お金の　貸し借りは　きちんと　しよう。
> "No matter how close you are, there should be manners even between friends. Let's handle money lending properly."

### 遠くの親戚より近くの他人（とおくのしんせきよりちかくのたにん）

- **Literal**: A nearby stranger is better than a distant relative
- **Meaning**: Neighbors you can rely on are more useful than relatives far away
- **Context**: Valuing local community and proximity
- **Example**:

> 遠くの親戚より　近くの他人と　言うように、ご近所付き合いは　大切だ。
> "As they say, a nearby stranger is better than a distant relative — neighborly relations are important."

### 袖振り合うも多生の縁（そでふりあうもたしょうのえん）

- **Literal**: Even brushing sleeves is a bond from a previous life
- **Meaning**: Even the most trivial encounter is connected by fate
- **Context**: Cherishing chance encounters; Buddhist overtone
- **Example**:

> 旅先で　出会った　人と　親友に　なった。袖振り合うも　多生の縁だ。
> "I became close friends with someone I met while traveling. Even a chance encounter is destined."

### 魚心あれば水心（うおごころあればみずごころ）

- **Literal**: If there is fish-heart, there is water-heart
- **Meaning**: If you show goodwill, the other side will respond in kind
- **Context**: Reciprocity in relationships
- **Example**:

> 魚心あれば水心で、こちらが　誠意を　見せれば　相手も　応えてくれる。
> "Goodwill begets goodwill — if we show sincerity, the other side will respond."

### 去る者は日々に疎し（さるものはひびにうとし）

- **Literal**: Those who leave become distant day by day
- **Meaning**: Out of sight, out of mind
- **Context**: Accepting the natural fading of relationships
- **Example**:

> 引っ越してから　連絡が　減った。去る者は日々に疎しだ。
> "Contact decreased after the move. Out of sight, out of mind."

## 8.7 Reading Passage

次の文章は、大学生二人の会話です。

---

**A**: 就活がうまくいかなくて、もう嫌になってきた。

**B**: 分かるよ。でも、石の上にも三年って言うじゃない。すぐに結果が出なくても続けることが大事だよ。

**A**: でも、もう何十社も落ちてるんだよ。

**B**: 蒔かぬ種は生えぬだから、応募し続けないと。千里の道も一歩からだよ。一社ずつ丁寧にやっていこう。

**A**: それはそうだけど……。友達はみんなもう内定もらってて、自分だけ取り残された気分だ。

**B**: 人と比べても仕方ない。人の振り見て我が振り直せって言うでしょう。成功した人のやり方を参考にすればいいんだよ。

**A**: まあね。でも、能ある鷹は爪を隠すタイプだから、面接で自分をアピールするのが苦手なんだよね。

**B**: それ、鷹じゃなくて、ただ自信がないだけでしょ。猿も木から落ちるんだから、失敗を恐れるな。

**A**: ……厳しいな。

**B**: 親しき仲にも礼儀あり、だけど、親しい仲だからこそ本当のことを言うんだよ。覆水盆に返らず——過去の不合格は忘れて、前を向こう。

**A**: ……ありがとう。もう少し頑張ってみるよ。

**B**: それでいい。継続は力なりだからね。

---

**Translation:**

**A**: Job hunting isn't going well, and I'm getting sick of it.

**B**: I get it. But you know what they say — patience pays off. Even if results don't come right away, continuing is what matters.

**A**: But I've been rejected by dozens of companies already.

**B**: Seeds you don't sow don't sprout — you have to keep applying. A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. Let's go carefully, one company at a time.

**A**: That's true, but... All my friends already have job offers, and I feel like I'm the only one left behind.

**B**: There's no point comparing yourself to others. They say you should watch others' behavior and fix your own. Just use the methods of people who succeeded as a reference.

**A**: I suppose. But I'm the type where the talented hawk hides its claws — I'm not good at selling myself in interviews.

**B**: That's not a hawk; that's just a lack of confidence. Even monkeys fall from trees, so don't be afraid of failure.

**A**: ...That's harsh.

**B**: Manners matter even between close friends, but it's precisely because we're close that I tell you the truth. What's done is done — forget the past rejections and look forward.

**A**: ...Thanks. I'll try a bit harder.

**B**: Good. Persistence is power, after all.
