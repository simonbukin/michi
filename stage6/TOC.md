# 日本語の道 — Stage 6 (N1 Mastery): Table of Contents

## Scope and Targets

**Prerequisite:** Stages 1-5 complete (~8,000 vocab, ~1,500 kanji, all N1 grammar, 四字熟語, classical reading competence, all registers encountered)
**Duration:** ~250 hours | **Kanji:** 2,000+ (常用漢字 complete) | **Vocabulary:** 10,000-12,000+ cumulative
**New in this stage:** No new grammar is explicitly taught. The focus shifts entirely to: nuance discrimination between near-synonymous forms, register fluidity, error recognition, specialized domain vocabulary, novel-length literary reading, sociolinguistic variation (gender, generation, region), humor/irony/indirectness, contrastive analysis (Japanese vs English structure), and 常用漢字 completion.

This is the stage where the learner stops "learning Japanese" and starts "refining Japanese." The goal is near-native comprehension across all registers and domains.

---

## Front Matter

- **Stage 6 Introduction** — What mastery means: not knowing everything, but being able to handle anything. No new grammar rules — instead, deepening intuition for why a speaker chose one form over another. Reading targets: novel-length works, legal documents, academic papers. Listening targets: any native content without subtitles.

## Part One: Nuance Discrimination

**Chapter 2 — Near-Synonym Grammar: Cause, Reason, Purpose**
2.1 から vs ので vs ため(に) vs によって vs につき — formality, objectivity, written vs spoken

2.4 からこそ vs だけあって vs だけに — "precisely because" at different emotional registers
2.5 Practice: identifying why a writer chose one causal connector over another

**Chapter 3 — Near-Synonym Grammar: Conjecture and Evidence**
3.1 ようだ vs みたいだ vs らしい vs そうだ(appearance) vs そうだ(hearsay) — the full five-way distinction revisited with subtle examples
3.2 はずだ vs わけだ vs に違いない vs に相違ない vs にほかならない — logical certainty at different registers
3.3 と思われる vs と考えられる vs とされている vs と見られている — academic/journalistic hedging hierarchy
3.4 Practice: reading a news article and identifying the evidence basis for each claim

**Chapter 4 — Near-Synonym Grammar: Scope, Extent, Addition**
4.1 について vs に関して vs をめぐって — general topic vs formal topic vs controversial topic
4.2 だけ vs ばかり vs のみ vs しか～ない vs きり — "only" at different registers and nuances
4.3 さえ vs すら vs まで vs でも — "even" with different emphasis patterns
4.4 はおろか vs どころか vs ばかりか vs のみならず — "not only, far from" at escalating formality

**Chapter 5 — Register Choice as Meaning**
