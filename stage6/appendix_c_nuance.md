# Appendix C — Nuance Discrimination Reference

Quick-reference comparison charts for near-synonym grammar groups from Chapters 1-4. Each table highlights the key axis of differentiation — register, emotional weight, typical context, and modality — so you can choose the right form at a glance.

---

## Concession and Contrast (Chapter 1)

### ものの vs けれども vs にもかかわらず vs とはいえ vs ながらも

| Form | Register | Emotional Weight | Common Context | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ものの | Formal | Mild surprise or disappointment | Literary prose, essays | Written |
| けれども | Neutral | Neutral | All contexts; versatile connector | Both |
| にもかかわらず | Formal | Strong emphasis on unexpectedness | Academic writing, formal speech | Written (primarily) |
| とはいえ | Semi-formal | Concessive qualification; "that said" | Commentary, editorials | Both |
| ながらも | Formal/literary | Sustained contrast; "while being X" | Descriptive prose, formal narrative | Written |

**Quick rule:** けれども is the safe default. Use ものの when the result mildly disappoints expectations. にもかかわらず stresses that the outcome defies a strong expectation. とはいえ acknowledges a point then pivots. ながらも highlights a simultaneous, enduring contradiction within a single subject.

---

### のに vs ても vs たところで

| Form | Register | Emotional Weight | Common Context | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| のに | Neutral–informal | Frustration, complaint, regret | Conversation, internal monologue | Spoken (primarily) |
| ても | Neutral | Neutral hypothetical concession | All contexts; "even if" | Both |
| たところで | Formal | Resigned futility; "even if one did X" | Written argument, pessimistic evaluation | Written |

**Quick rule:** のに carries the speaker's personal dissatisfaction about a real situation. ても is a neutral hypothetical ("even if"). たところで conveys futility — "even supposing you did X, it would be pointless."

---

### 一方(で) vs 反面 vs 他方 vs それに対して

| Form | Register | Emotional Weight | Common Context | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 一方(で) | Semi-formal | Neutral parallel or contrast | News, essays, general comparison | Both |
| 反面 | Semi-formal | Highlights a hidden flip side | Evaluative writing; pros-and-cons | Written (primarily) |
| 他方 | Formal | Neutral; more detached than 一方 | Academic, policy discussion | Written |
| それに対して | Semi-formal | Explicit point-counterpoint | Debate, structured comparison | Both |

**Quick rule:** 一方(で) is the general-purpose "on the other hand." 反面 specifically flags a downside or unexpected reverse side of the same thing. 他方 is a more formal, academic variant of 一方. それに対して sets up a direct, explicit contrast between two distinct items.

## Cause and Reason (Chapter 2)

### から vs ので vs ため(に) vs によって vs につき

| Form | Register | Emotional Weight | Common Context | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| から | Informal–neutral | Subjective; speaker's own reasoning | Conversation, personal explanation | Spoken (primarily) |
| ので | Neutral–polite | Objective-sounding; softer than から | Polite speech, general writing | Both |
| ため(に) | Formal | Neutral, objective cause | Academic, news, formal documents | Written (primarily) |
| によって | Formal | Neutral; agent or variable cause | Academic, legal, scientific writing | Written |
| につき | Very formal | Impersonal, bureaucratic | Notices, announcements, signage | Written |

**Quick rule:** から is subjective and assertive — the speaker owns the reason. ので sounds gentler and more objective. ため(に) is the go-to written/formal causal connector. によって marks the agent or varying cause ("depending on"). につき is restricted to formal notices ("due to X, we will...").

---

### ために vs ように

| Form | Register | Meaning Focus | Subject Constraint | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ために | Neutral–formal | Purpose with volitional verb; direct goal | Same or different subject | Both |
| ように | Neutral | Purpose toward a state or non-volitional outcome | Often different subject or intransitive | Both |

**Quick rule:** ために pairs with volitional verbs and names a concrete goal the subject actively pursues. ように aims at bringing about a state or condition, often one not directly under the subject's control ("so that it will become...").

---

### おかげで vs せいで vs ばかりに vs だけに

| Form | Register | Emotional Weight | Outcome Valence | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| おかげで | Neutral | Gratitude, positive attribution | Positive (or ironic) | Both |
| せいで | Neutral | Blame, negative attribution | Negative | Both |
| ばかりに | Semi-formal | Regret; "just because of that one thing" | Negative | Both |
| だけに | Semi-formal | Proportional; "precisely because" | Positive or negative | Both |

**Quick rule:** おかげで = "thanks to" (good outcome). せいで = "because of" (bad outcome, someone's fault). ばかりに = "just because" (regret over a single triggering cause). だけに = "precisely because / as you'd expect given..." (proportional to the cause; outcome can go either way).

---

### からこそ vs だけあって vs だけに

| Form | Register | Emotional Weight | Function | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| からこそ | Neutral–formal | Emphatic conviction | "It is precisely because X that Y" | Both |
| だけあって | Neutral | Impressed acknowledgement | "As you'd expect from someone/something of that caliber" | Both |
| だけに | Semi-formal | Proportional emphasis | "Precisely because X, Y all the more" | Both |

**Quick rule:** からこそ emphatically isolates the one true reason — "it is because X and nothing else." だけあって expresses admiration or unsurprised acknowledgement of quality — "as befitting." だけに says the degree of Y is proportional to X — "precisely because it's X, Y is even more so."

## Conjecture and Evidence (Chapter 3)

### ようだ vs みたいだ vs らしい vs そうだ (appearance) vs そうだ (hearsay)

| Form | Register | Evidence Basis | Speaker Certainty | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ようだ | Neutral–formal | Speaker's own sensory/situational evidence | Moderate; inferential | Both |
| みたいだ | Informal | Same as ようだ, but colloquial | Moderate; inferential | Spoken |
| らしい | Neutral | External information or typical characteristics | Moderate; secondhand or typicality | Both |
| そうだ (appearance) | Neutral | Visual impression of imminent state | High for appearance; "looks like it will" | Both |
| そうだ (hearsay) | Neutral | Reported information | Neutral; simply relaying | Both |

**Quick rule:** ようだ / みたいだ = "it seems" based on what the speaker personally observes (みたいだ is the casual variant). らしい = "apparently" based on external info or "typical of." そうだ (stem +) = "looks like it's about to / looks delicious" from visual impression. そうだ (plain form +) = "I heard that..." pure hearsay relay.

---

### はずだ vs わけだ vs に違いない vs に相違ない vs にほかならない

| Form | Register | Speaker Stance | Logical Basis | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| はずだ | Neutral | Expectation based on reasoning or knowledge | "It should be the case" | Both |
| わけだ | Neutral | Logical conclusion from known facts | "So that's why / that means" | Both |
| に違いない | Semi-formal | Strong personal conviction | "There's no doubt that" | Both |
| に相違ない | Very formal | Same as に違いない, literary register | "There is no discrepancy" | Written |
| にほかならない | Formal | Emphatic identification of essence | "It is nothing other than" | Written |

**Quick rule:** はずだ expresses a reasoned expectation ("should be"). わけだ draws a logical conclusion or explains a reason ("so that's why"). に違いない is a strong assertion of certainty ("must be"). に相違ない is its literary/legal equivalent. にほかならない identifies the essential nature — "X is nothing but Y."

---

### と思われる vs と考えられる vs とされている vs と見られている

| Form | Register | Nuance | Agency/Source | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| と思われる | Formal | Passive impression; cautious opinion | Impersonal or general sentiment | Written |
| と考えられる | Formal | Analytical judgment after deliberation | Academic/expert reasoning | Written |
| とされている | Formal | Established consensus or convention | Received knowledge, established view | Written |
| と見られている | Formal | Observation-based assessment by observers | Media, analysts, outside observers | Written |

**Quick rule:** と思われる = "it is thought that" (gentle, distanced opinion). と考えられる = "it is considered that" (reasoned, analytical). とされている = "it is regarded as / it is established that" (conventional wisdom). と見られている = "it is seen as / viewed as" (observers' assessment, often used in news).

## Scope and Extent (Chapter 4)

### について vs に関して vs をめぐって

| Form | Register | Scope/Nuance | Common Context | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| について | Neutral | General topic marker; "about/regarding" | All contexts | Both |
| に関して | Formal | Broader or more formal "regarding" | Official documents, formal speech | Written (primarily) |
| をめぐって | Semi-formal | Controversy or debate surrounding a topic | News, political/social discussion | Written (primarily) |

**Quick rule:** について is the all-purpose "about." に関して is its formal upgrade, often implying a broader or more official scope. をめぐって specifically signals that the topic is surrounded by debate, conflict, or multiple competing perspectives.

---

### だけ vs ばかり vs のみ vs しか～ない vs きり

| Form | Register | Nuance | Emotional Weight | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| だけ | Neutral | Simple, objective limitation | Neutral | Both |
| ばかり | Neutral–informal | "Nothing but"; excess or monotony | Slight exasperation or emphasis | Both |
| のみ | Formal | Formal equivalent of だけ | Neutral; literary/official tone | Written |
| しか～ない | Neutral | "Only" with negative; emphasizes insufficiency | Disappointment or scarcity | Both |
| きり | Informal | "Only" with finality; "since X and nothing after" | Finality, emotional weight | Spoken (primarily) |

**Quick rule:** だけ is the neutral default for "only." ばかり stresses excess ("nothing but X"). のみ is the formal/written version of だけ. しか～ない emphasizes that the amount is insufficient ("only X and that's not enough"). きり marks a definitive limit, often with emotional finality ("he left and that was the last time").

---

### さえ vs すら vs まで vs でも

| Form | Register | Nuance | Scalar Direction | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| さえ | Neutral–formal | "Even X" (extreme/minimal example) | Bottom of scale upward | Both |
| すら | Formal/literary | Same as さえ, more literary | Bottom of scale upward | Written |
| まで | Neutral | "Even X" (surprising inclusion) | Extends to unexpected endpoint | Both |
| でも | Informal | "Even" (casual); or "something like" | General, less scalar | Spoken (primarily) |

**Quick rule:** さえ presents an extreme minimum to imply everything above it ("if even X, then surely Y"). すら is the literary/written equivalent of さえ. まで extends to a surprising endpoint ("it went so far as to include even X"). でも is the casual, conversational "even" without strong scalar implication.

---

### はおろか vs どころか vs ばかりか vs のみならず

| Form | Register | Nuance | Structure | Written/Spoken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| はおろか | Formal | "Let alone X; even Y" | X は おろか Y も/さえ | Written (primarily) |
| どころか | Neutral | "Far from X; in fact, the opposite/more" | Contradicts or exceeds expectation | Both |
| ばかりか | Semi-formal | "Not only X but also Y" | Additive escalation | Both |
| のみならず | Very formal | "Not only X but also Y" (literary) | Formal additive escalation | Written |

**Quick rule:** はおろか dismisses X as obvious and escalates to a more surprising Y — "let alone X, even Y." どころか flatly contradicts the premise — "far from X, it's actually Y." ばかりか adds Y on top of X in an escalating "not only... but also" pattern. のみならず is the formal/literary equivalent of ばかりか.

---

## How to Use This Reference

1. **Identify the function** you need (concession, cause, conjecture, scope).
2. **Check the register** — is your context formal writing, casual speech, or somewhere in between?
3. **Check emotional weight** — are you expressing frustration, gratitude, neutrality, or emphasis?
4. **Read the Quick rule** to confirm your choice fits the intended nuance.

When in doubt, start with the most neutral form in each group (けれども, ので, ようだ, について, だけ) and adjust toward specificity as your command of the distinctions grows.
