## 熟字訓 (Irregular Compound Readings)

Words where the reading applies to the whole compound rather than to individual kanji. The reading is assigned to the word as a unit, so knowing each kanji's on'yomi or kun'yomi will not predict the pronunciation. These are among the most commonly tested items at N1 and are essential for fluent reading of native-level texts.

## Notes on Usage

**熟字訓 in modern Japanese:** These readings are officially recognized in the 常用漢字表 (Jōyō Kanji List) appendix. They must be learned as fixed vocabulary items because their pronunciation cannot be derived from standard kanji readings.

**当て字 in modern Japanese:** Many loanword 当て字 have been largely replaced by katakana in contemporary usage (e.g., コーヒー is far more common than 珈琲 in everyday writing). However, they still appear in literary texts, shop signage, product packaging, and N1-level reading passages. Country-name 当て字 survive primarily in abbreviations (e.g., 米 for アメリカ, 仏 for フランス, 独 for ドイツ, 英 for イギリス).

**Testing at N1:** Both categories appear in the JLPT N1 reading and vocabulary sections. Examinees are expected to recognize these compounds and select the correct reading or meaning from multiple-choice options.
