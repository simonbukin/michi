# Appendix F — Specialized Domain Vocabulary

This appendix collects the specialized vocabulary introduced across Chapters 12 through 15 of Stage 6. Each section corresponds to a professional or academic domain and provides two resources: a vocabulary table with kanji, reading, pitch accent, and English meaning, and a set of sentence patterns characteristic of that domain.

Pitch accent notation follows the standard numbering system: ⓪ indicates a flat (heiban) pattern, and ①②③④⑤ indicate the mora after which the pitch drops.

## Cross-Domain Notes

Several patterns appear across multiple domains with slightly different nuances.

**～に基づく / ～に基づいて**: In legal writing, this means "pursuant to" and carries the force of law. In academic writing, it means "based on" and signals the evidentiary foundation of a claim. In business, it is closer to "in accordance with" and often refers to internal guidelines.

**～を踏まえ(て)**: In business, this pattern frames a decision as having considered relevant data. In academic writing, it signals that the author has engaged with prior work before advancing a new argument.

**～が認められる**: In medical Japanese, this describes observable clinical findings. In legal Japanese, it indicates that a court has recognized or acknowledged a fact or right.

**～ものとする**: Almost exclusively legal or contractual. Learners encounter ものだ and ものの in general Japanese, but ものとする has a narrower and more binding meaning — "shall" in the sense of a legal obligation or presumption.

These overlaps are natural. Professional Japanese draws from a shared classical and formal register, but each field has refined these patterns to serve its own communicative needs. Recognizing the domain is the first step to reading them correctly.
