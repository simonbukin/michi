# Appendix H — Extended Counters

Stage 1 introduced the nine essential counters: つ, 人, 本, 枚, 台, 匹, 杯, 冊, 階. These cover the bulk of everyday counting situations, but real Japanese — novels, news, cooking, manga, technical writing — uses far more. This appendix collects roughly forty additional counters you will encounter regularly. Together with the nine from Stage 1, this gives you fluent recognition for nearly every counted noun in non-specialist text.

Each entry gives the counter, its semantic domain, the sound-change pattern (using the same labels as Stage 1's Appendix E), and a brief example of usage. Counters that take irregular forms get a small forms table; the rest follow patterns you already know.

---

## H.1 People (Beyond ～人)

### ～名（めい） — People (Formal)

The formal counter for people. You will see it constantly in business, hospitality, and writing.

> 予約は何名様ですか。 *How many in your party?*
>
> 参加者は二十名でした。 *There were twenty participants.*

No sound changes. Forms: いちめい, にめい, さんめい, よんめい, ごめい, etc. The honorific form ～名様 (めいさま) is standard in restaurants and reservations.

### ～様（さま） — Honored People

A honorific counter used in formal address, ceremony, and traditional contexts. Less common in everyday speech but ubiquitous on signage and in writing.

> お一人様 — *One person* (used on ticketing, restaurant signage)

---

## H.2 Animals (Beyond ～匹)

### ～頭（とう） — Large Animals

For animals roughly the size of a person or larger: cattle, horses, elephants, lions, bears. Also conventionally used for some smaller livestock (sheep) and zoo animals.

> 牧場には牛が三十頭いる。 *There are thirty cows on the farm.*

Sound-change pattern: same as ～本/～杯 in the か行 family — 1 いっとう, 6 ろっとう, 8 はっとう, 10 じゅっとう trigger 促音. 3 さんとう does not voice (because the next mora is already voiced in some analyses; in practice 三頭 is read さんとう).

### ～羽（わ） — Birds (and Rabbits)

For birds, including waterfowl and chickens. Also the traditional counter for rabbits — a famous quirk attributed historically to monastic dietary restrictions.

> 庭にスズメが三羽いる。 *There are three sparrows in the garden.*

Forms: いちわ, にわ, さんば, よんわ, ごわ, ろくわ (or ろっぱ), ななわ, はちわ (or はっぱ), きゅうわ, じゅうわ (or じっぱ), なんば.

### ～尾（び） — Fish (Formal)

For counting fish, particularly in formal, culinary, or fishing contexts. In casual speech, 匹 covers fish too.

> 鮮魚売り場には鯛が五尾並んでいる。 *Five sea bream are laid out at the fresh-fish counter.*

Mostly regular. 1 いちび, 8 はちび, 10 じゅうび — no consistent 促音, the counter ～尾 resists the standard alternations.

## H.3 Vehicles and Vessels (Beyond ～台)

### ～隻（せき） — Ships, Boats

For vessels of any size — fishing boats, freighters, cruise ships, naval vessels.

> 港に漁船が二十隻停泊している。 *Twenty fishing boats are anchored in the harbor.*

Sound changes: 1 いっせき, 8 はっせき, 10 じゅっせき trigger 促音. Otherwise regular.

### ～機（き） — Aircraft, Machines

For airplanes, helicopters, and large industrial machinery. Distinct from ～台, which covers ground vehicles and consumer machines.

> 滑走路に旅客機が三機並んでいる。 *Three passenger jets are lined up on the runway.*

No sound changes.

### ～両（りょう） — Train Cars

For railroad cars and traditional vehicle units. A train of ten cars is 十両編成 (じゅうりょうへんせい).

> この電車は十二両だ。 *This train has twelve cars.*

No sound changes.

### ～艘（そう） — Small Boats

For smaller vessels, often traditional or fishing craft. Less common than ～隻 in modern writing but present in literature and historical texts.

---

## H.4 Long, Cylindrical, or Strand-Like (Beyond ～本)

### ～通（つう） — Letters, Documents, Emails

For mailed correspondence and formal documents. Email increasingly uses ～通 in formal writing, ～件 in casual.

> 手紙が三通届いた。 *Three letters arrived.*

1 いっつう, 8 はっつう, 10 じゅっつう trigger 促音.

### ～着（ちゃく） — Clothing, Outfits

For complete pieces of clothing — suits, dresses, kimonos, school uniforms, jackets. Underwear and socks have their own counters.

> スーツを二着買った。 *I bought two suits.*

1 いっちゃく, 8 はっちゃく, 10 じゅっちゃく trigger 促音.

### ～足（そく） — Footwear, Pairs of Socks

For pairs of shoes, sandals, socks, tabi.

> 靴下が三足ある。 *I have three pairs of socks.*

1 いっそく, 8 はっそく, 10 じゅっそく trigger 促音.

### ～振（ふり） — Swords

For swords, daggers, and ceremonial blades. Niche, but appears in historical fiction, samurai films, and museum descriptions.

---

## H.5 Books, Documents, and Series

### ～巻（かん） — Volumes (Multi-Volume Works)

For volumes of a series — manga, light novels, encyclopedias, classical anthologies. ～冊 counts physical books generically; ～巻 counts volumes-of-a-set.

> 漫画は全二十巻だ。 *The manga is twenty volumes total.*

No sound changes for most numbers; some speakers say いっかん/はっかん with 促音.

### ～部（ぶ） — Copies of Printed Material

For printed copies — newsletters, reports, mass-distributed documents.

> 報告書を百部印刷してください。 *Please print 100 copies of the report.*

1 いちぶ, 8 はちぶ — generally no 促音.

### ～章（しょう） — Chapters

For chapters of a book.

> 本書は十章から成る。 *This book consists of ten chapters.*

### ～節（せつ） — Sections, Stanzas

For numbered sections within a chapter, or stanzas of a song or poem.

### ～条（じょう） — Articles (Legal, Formal)

For numbered articles in laws, treaties, contracts, and historical edicts. Constitution articles, contract clauses, and the classical 十七条憲法 all use ～条.

> 憲法第九条 — *Article 9 of the Constitution*

### ～項（こう） — Items, Sub-Items

For numbered items in a list, especially within a legal article or formal document.

> 第三条第二項 — *Article 3, Paragraph 2*

## H.6 Buildings and Spaces (Beyond ～階)

### ～軒（けん） — Houses, Shops

For residential dwellings, shops, restaurants — anything you would describe as a 建物 with an entrance.

> この通りには寿司屋が三軒ある。 *There are three sushi restaurants on this street.*

1 いっけん, 6 ろっけん, 8 はっけん, 10 じゅっけん trigger 促音. 3 さんげん voices — 三軒.

### ～棟（とう・むね） — Buildings (Large)

For large buildings, especially in apartment complexes, hospitals, school campuses. Read とう in formal/written contexts, むね in some compound forms.

> アパートは三棟ある。 *There are three apartment buildings.*

### ～戸（こ） — Households, Dwelling Units

For housing units within a multi-family building, or households as administrative entries.

> このマンションは八十戸ある。 *This apartment complex has eighty units.*

### ～室（しつ） — Rooms

For rooms — hotel rooms, classrooms, hospital rooms. Less common in casual speech (where ～部屋 is typical) but standard in formal contexts.

---

## H.7 Things, Items, and Events

### ～個（こ） — Generic Small Objects

The most flexible everyday counter after ～つ. Used for fruits, eggs, candies, small packages, and anything roughly fist-sized that lacks its own counter. ～個 is gradually displacing ～つ in casual speech for many items.

> りんごを五個買った。 *I bought five apples.*

1 いっこ, 6 ろっこ, 8 はっこ, 10 じゅっこ trigger 促音.

### ～件（けん） — Cases, Matters, Incidents

For incidents, news items, queries, customer requests, business inquiries — anything you would describe as a "case" or "matter." Ubiquitous in news, business, and crime reporting.

> 事故は今年三十件発生した。 *Thirty accidents occurred this year.*

1 いっけん, 6 ろっけん, 8 はっけん, 10 じゅっけん trigger 促音. 3 さんけん stays voiceless.

### ～点（てん） — Items, Points

For counted items in a list (especially merchandise, exhibits, criticisms) and for scoring points (tests, games).

> 美術館には絵画が二百点展示されている。 *Two hundred paintings are on display at the museum.*
>
> テストで八十五点取った。 *I got 85 points on the test.*

No sound changes.

### ～品（しな・ひん） — Goods, Items

For merchandise items in commerce. Read ひん in compounds; the bare counter ～品 is rare.

### ～種類（しゅるい） — Kinds, Types

For varieties or types of something.

> このお店ではコーヒーを十種類提供している。 *This shop offers ten kinds of coffee.*

No sound changes.

### ～組（くみ） — Sets, Pairs, Groups

For sets, matched pairs, performing groups, families considered as units.

> カップが三組ある。 *There are three sets of cups.*

No sound changes.

### ～対（つい） — Matched Pairs

For paired objects considered as a single unit: pairs of vases, pairs of dolls, paired statues.

### ～箱（はこ） — Boxes (Containers)

For boxes of something — boxes of cigarettes, gift boxes, boxed lunches counted as boxes.

1 ひとはこ or いっぱこ, 8 はっぱこ — follows the は行 pattern.

### ～袋（ふくろ） — Bags

For bagged items — bags of rice, bags of chips, paper bags considered as bags.

## H.8 Time and Frequency

These counters were partially introduced in Stage 1 (時間, 分, 月, 年). This section consolidates and extends.

### ～回（かい） — Times, Occurrences

For times an action is repeated.

> 一回も行ったことがない。 *I have never been there even once.*

1 いっかい, 6 ろっかい, 8 はっかい, 10 じゅっかい trigger 促音. 3 さんかい usually stays voiceless (3 doesn't voice with か行 in this counter, unlike ～階).

### ～度（ど） — Times, Degrees, Occasions

For occasions (often more formal than ～回), for degrees of temperature/angle, and for instances tinged with reflection ("once," "twice in my life").

> 京都には二度行った。 *I have been to Kyoto twice.*
>
> 今日は三十度ある。 *It is 30 degrees today.*

No sound changes.

### ～日間（にちかん） — Span of Days

For a duration of days. Stage 1 covered ～日 for dates of the month; ～日間 specifies a span.

> 一週間 (いっしゅうかん) — *one week*; 三日間 (みっかかん) — *three days (duration)*

The ～日 part follows the irregular date readings up to 10 (一日 ついたち as date / いちにち as duration; the duration use is regular にち).

### ～週間（しゅうかん） — Weeks (Duration)

For spans of weeks.

> 二週間休む予定だ。 *I plan to take two weeks off.*

1 いっしゅうかん, 8 はっしゅうかん, 10 じゅっしゅうかん trigger 促音.

### ～か月（かげつ） — Months (Duration)

For spans of months. Note the か is written in hiragana between number and 月.

> 三か月住んでいた。 *I lived there for three months.*

1 いっかげつ, 6 ろっかげつ, 8 はっかげつ, 10 じゅっかげつ trigger 促音.

### ～年間（ねんかん） — Years (Duration)

For spans of years.

> 五年間勤めた。 *I worked there for five years.*

No sound changes.

### ～秒（びょう） — Seconds

For seconds of time.

> 十秒待って。 *Wait ten seconds.*

No sound changes.

### ～時間（じかん） — Hours (Duration)

For spans of hours, distinct from ～時 which marks clock times.

> 三時間勉強した。 *I studied for three hours.*

No sound changes.

## H.9 Quantities and Measurements

### ～円（えん） — Yen

The currency counter. You used this in Stage 1 vocabulary. Listed here for completeness.

No sound changes for the counter itself; the numbers are read normally.

### ～グラム / ～キロ / ～メートル

Loanword counters from English. Always regular.

### ～杯（はい）— for Servings

Stage 1 covered this for cups and bowls. Note that 杯 is also used for spoonfuls in cooking: スプーン一杯 (one spoonful).

### ～滴（てき） — Drops (Liquid)

For drops of liquid — water, oil, medicine.

> 醤油を二、三滴垂らす。 *Add two or three drops of soy sauce.*

1 いってき, 8 はってき, 10 じゅってき trigger 促音.

---

## H.10 Documents, Words, and Linguistic Units

### ～語（ご） — Words

For counting words in a text or vocabulary list.

> 単語を五十語覚えた。 *I memorized fifty words.*

No sound changes.

### ～字（じ） — Characters

For characters in writing — kanji, kana, alphabet letters, total character count of a document.

> この論文は一万字ある。 *This paper is 10,000 characters.*

No sound changes.

### ～行（ぎょう） — Lines (of Text)

For lines of text on a page.

> 詩は三行で構成されている。 *The poem is composed of three lines.*

No sound changes.

### ～頁／ページ — Pages

For pages. ～頁 is the kanji form; ページ is more common in casual writing.

> 三十ページ読んだ。 *I read thirty pages.*

## H.11 Quick Reference: Counter Selection

When you encounter a noun and need to know which counter to expect, this rough decision tree handles most cases.

| If the noun is... | Use... |
|---|---|
| A person, formal | ～名 |
| A person, casual | ～人 |
| A large animal (cow, horse, lion) | ～頭 |
| A small animal (cat, dog, fish) | ～匹 |
| A bird (or rabbit) | ～羽 |
| A long thin object (pencil, bottle, tree) | ～本 |
| A flat object (paper, ticket, shirt) | ～枚 |
| A machine, vehicle, appliance | ～台 |
| An aircraft | ～機 |
| A ship | ～隻 |
| A train car | ～両 |
| A pair of shoes/socks | ～足 |
| A piece of clothing (full outfit) | ～着 |
| A book (generic) | ～冊 |
| A volume of a series | ～巻 |
| A printed copy (report, flyer) | ～部 |
| A house or shop | ～軒 |
| A building (large) | ～棟 |
| A room | ～室 |
| A small generic item (apple, egg) | ～個 |
| A case, matter, incident | ～件 |
| An item in a list, point in a score | ～点 |
| A kind, variety | ～種類 |
| A set, group | ～組 |
| A box (container) | ～箱 |
| A time, occasion | ～回 / ～度 |
| A drop of liquid | ～滴 |
| A character (kanji/kana) | ～字 |
| A line of text | ～行 |
| A page | ～ページ / ～頁 |

When in doubt, default to ～つ (1-10) or ～個 (any quantity). Native speakers will not misunderstand you. The specialized counters give your language precision and register-appropriateness, but ～つ and ～個 will never be wrong, only blunt.

## H.12 Sound Change Patterns Recap

The patterns from Stage 1's Appendix E generalize to the new counters in this appendix:

- **か行 counters** (～回, ～階, ～軒, ～巻, ～着 (initial ち behaves like か here for 促音), etc.): 1, 6, 8, 10 → 促音; 3, なん often voice (か → が).
- **さ行 counters** (～冊, ～歳, ～足, ～隻, ～章, ～節, ～条 (initial じょ inherent), etc.): 1, 8, 10 → 促音; 3 typically does not voice.
- **た行 counters** (～通, ～点, ～度, ～頭, ～滴, etc.): 1, 8, 10 → 促音.
- **は行 counters** (～杯, ～匹, ～本, ～袋, ～箱 (initial は), etc.): 1, 6, 8, 10 → っ + 半濁音 (ぱ・ぴ・ぽ); 3, なん → 濁音 (ば・び・ぼ).
- **ま行, な行, や行, ら行, わ行 counters** (～枚, ～名, ～年, ～名, ～両, ～羽, etc.): generally no sound changes.
- **Voiced-initial counters** (～台, ～段, ～組): no further voicing (already voiced).

Once you internalize the pattern by initial mora, every new counter you meet is predictable to within one or two irregularities. Memorize the categories, not the individual forms.
