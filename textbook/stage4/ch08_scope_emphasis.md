# Chapter 8 — Scope Limiters and Emphasis

This chapter covers expressions that narrow or expand the scope of a statement and particles that add emphasis. Some of these patterns restrict ("limited to," "only when"), while others expand ("not only... but also," "let alone"). Together, they give you the tools to make precise, rhetorically effective statements in Japanese — the kind that appear in persuasive writing, news commentary, and everyday complaints alike.

Many of these expressions are built from familiar words. 限る means "to limit." ばかり means "only." どころ can mean "place" or "situation." こそ and さえ are particles with long histories in the language. Understanding how these core words generate multiple expressions will make each pattern easier to remember and use.

All patterns in this chapter attach to plain forms unless otherwise noted.

---

## 8.1 ～に限って — "When It Comes to... (Ironic)"

に限って has two uses. The first is an ironic observation about bad luck or unexpected situations: "It's always when X that Y (something unwanted) happens." The second is a strong assertion about a specific person or thing: "X of all people/things would never..."

### Formation

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Noun + に限って | "When it comes to X" (ironic) |
| Noun + に限って + negative | "X of all people/things would never..." |

### Examples (ironic coincidence)

> 急いでいる　時に　限って、電車が　遅れる。
> "It's always when I'm in a hurry that the train is late."

> 傘を　持っていない　日に　限って、雨が　降る。
> "It's always on the day I don't have an umbrella that it rains."

> 大事な　会議の　日に　限って、体調が　悪くなる。
> "It's always on the day of an important meeting that I feel unwell."

### Examples (strong assertion)

> うちの　子に　限って、そんなことを　するはずがない。
> "My child of all people would never do such a thing."

> あの店に　限って、品質の　悪いものを　出すわけがない。
> "That shop of all places would never serve something of poor quality."

### Usage note

The ironic use is far more common in everyday speech. The assertion use (に限って + negative) often sounds like a parent or supporter defending someone they are biased toward. It can sound naive — and Japanese speakers sometimes use it with self-awareness or humor.

## 8.2 ～に限らず — "Not Limited To"

に限らず expands the scope of a statement. Where に限って narrows focus to one thing, に限らず opens it up: "Not just X, but also Y and Z." It is formal and commonly used in writing, announcements, and speeches.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + に限らず |

### Examples

> この問題は　日本に　限らず、世界中で　起きている。
> "This problem is not limited to Japan — it is happening all over the world."

> 若者に　限らず、高齢者にも　スマホの　利用が　広がっている。
> "Smartphone use is spreading not just among young people but among the elderly as well."

> 男性に　限らず、女性も　積極的に　参加してほしい。
> "We want not only men but women to actively participate as well."

> 平日に　限らず、土日も　営業しています。
> "We are open not only on weekdays but on weekends as well."

> 食事に　限らず、運動も　健康には　大切だ。
> "Not only diet but exercise is also important for health."

## 8.3 ～はおろか — "Let Alone"

はおろか presents an extreme case by stating that not even a lesser or more expected thing is true, let alone a greater one. The pattern is: "X はおろか, even Y (lesser thing) is/isn't..." It emphasizes how extreme the situation is.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + はおろか + Noun + も/さえ + predicate |

### Examples

> 漢字は　おろか、ひらがなさえ　読めない。
> "He can't read hiragana, let alone kanji."

> 海外旅行は　おろか、国内旅行にも　行ったことがない。
> "I've never even taken a domestic trip, let alone traveled abroad."

> 新車は　おろか、中古車を　買う　お金も　ない。
> "I don't even have the money to buy a used car, let alone a new one."

> 英語は　おろか、日本語も　まだ　怪しい。
> "My Japanese is still shaky, let alone my English."

> 謝罪は　おろか、返事さえ　来なかった。
> "I didn't even get a reply, let alone an apology."

## 8.4 ～ばかりか / ～ばかりでなく — "Not Only... But Also"

ばかりか and ばかりでなく express "not only X but also Y." They expand the scope of a statement, and the Y element often represents something surprising or additional that goes beyond what was expected.

### Formation

| Preceding element | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Verb (plain form) | 話せるばかりか / 話せるばかりでなく |
| い-adjective | 高いばかりか / 高いばかりでなく |
| な-adjective + な/である | 有名なばかりか |
| Noun (+ である) | 学生であるばかりか |

### Examples

> 彼は　英語が　話せる　ばかりか、フランス語も　できる。
> "He can not only speak English but French as well."

> この店は　安い　ばかりでなく、味も　いい。
> "This shop is not only cheap but tastes good too."

> 彼女は　歌が　上手な　ばかりか、ダンスも　プロ級だ。
> "She is not only a good singer but her dancing is also professional level."

> 雨が　降った　ばかりか、雷まで　鳴り出した。
> "Not only did it rain, but thunder started as well."

> 遅刻した　ばかりでなく、宿題も　忘れてきた。
> "Not only was he late, but he also forgot his homework."

---

## 8.5 ～どころか — "Far From"

どころか denies the premise of a statement and asserts something that contradicts or goes well beyond it. The English equivalents include "far from," "not at all," and "on the contrary." It can be used in two directions: to say the reality is worse than expected, or better than expected.

### Formation

| Preceding element | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Verb (plain form) | 助けるどころか |
| い-adjective | 安いどころか |
| な-adjective + な/である | 簡単なところか |
| Noun | 休みどころか |

### Examples

> 助ける　どころか、邪魔された。
> "Far from helping, they got in my way."

> 日本語が　上手に　なる　どころか、前より　下手に　なった気がする。
> "Far from getting better at Japanese, I feel like I've gotten worse than before."

> 休み　どころか、毎日　残業だ。
> "Far from getting time off, I'm working overtime every day."

> 嫌がる　どころか、喜んで　引き受けてくれた。
> "Far from being reluctant, she happily took it on."

> 安い　どころか、倍の　値段だった。
> "Far from being cheap, it was double the price."

### Comparison: はおろか vs ばかりか vs どころか

These three patterns all expand scope, but they work differently:

| Pattern | Structure | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| はおろか | "X let alone — even Y" | Emphasizes that even a lesser/easier thing fails; X is the harder benchmark |
| ばかりか | "not only X but also Y" | Adds surprising additional information; relatively neutral |
| どころか | "far from X — actually Y" | Contradicts or dramatically exceeds the expectation of X |

Compare:

> 漢字**はおろか**、ひらがなも　読めない。
> "Can't read hiragana, **let alone** kanji." (Lesser thing also fails)

> 漢字が　読める**ばかりか**、書くことも　できる。
> "Can **not only** read kanji but write them too." (Additional ability)

> 漢字が　読める**どころか**、古典も　読めるらしい。
> "**Far from** just reading kanji — apparently she can read classical texts." (Dramatically exceeds expectation)

はおろか typically appears with negative predicates. ばかりか can appear with either positive or negative. どころか contradicts the stated expectation, so the result clause goes in the opposite direction from what was suggested.

## 8.6 ～こそ — "It Is Precisely X"

こそ is an emphatic particle that has been part of Japanese since the classical language. It highlights the word or phrase it follows as the most important element — "it is precisely X" or "X above all else." It adds conviction, emphasis, or emotional weight.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + こそ |
| Phrase + からこそ (already covered in Ch. 4, reviewed here) |
| Phrase + てこそ |

### Examples

> 今**こそ**　行動する　時だ。
> "**Now** is the time to act."

> これ**こそ**　私が　探していた　ものだ。
> "**This** is exactly what I was looking for."

> 「ありがとう。」「こちら**こそ**。」
> "'Thank you.' 'No, thank **you**.'" (It is I who should be thanking you.)

> 失敗した　から**こそ**、成長できた。
> "It is **precisely because** I failed that I was able to grow."

> 実際に　やって　みて**こそ**、分かることが　ある。
> "There are things you can only understand **by** actually trying them."

### てこそ — "only by doing"

てこそ emphasizes that the te-form action is a necessary condition for the result. It conveys "only when you do X does Y become possible":

> 苦労して**こそ**、成功の　喜びが　分かる。
> "Only through hardship can you understand the joy of success."

> 自分で　経験して**こそ**、本当に　理解できる。
> "Only by experiencing it yourself can you truly understand."

## 8.7 ～さえ / ～すら — "Even"

さえ and すら are particles meaning "even." They highlight an extreme or unexpected case to emphasize a point. If even X (an extreme case) is true, then everything else certainly is too. すら is more literary and formal than さえ.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + さえ / すら |
| Verb (ます stem) + さえ + する |

### Examples

> 名前**さえ**　知らない。
> "I don't even know their name."

> 子ども**にさえ**　分かる　問題だ。
> "It's a problem that even a child could understand."

> 水**すら**　飲めない　状態だった。
> "It was a state where I couldn't even drink water."

> 彼は　挨拶**さえ**　しないで　帰った。
> "He left without even saying hello."

> そんなことは　考え**さえ**　しなかった。
> "I didn't even consider such a thing."

### さえ vs すら

| Feature | さえ | すら |
|---|---|---|
| Register | Neutral to formal | Formal / literary |
| Frequency | Very common | Less common in speech |
| Meaning | Identical | Identical |

In modern spoken Japanese, さえ is the standard choice. すら appears primarily in written language, news, and formal speech. They are grammatically interchangeable.

## 8.8 ～さえ～ば — "If Only"

さえ...ば combines the particle さえ ("even, at least") with the conditional ば to express a minimal sufficient condition: "If only X, then everything would be fine." It implies that X is the one thing needed — nothing else matters.

### Formation

| Type | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Noun + さえ + あれば | お金さえあれば |
| Verb (ます stem) + さえ + すれば | 努力しさえすれば |
| Verb (て form) + さえ + いれば | 待っていさえすれば |
| い-adjective (く) + さえ + あれば | 安くさえあれば |

### Examples

> 時間**さえ**　あれ**ば**、行けるのに。
> "If only I had time, I could go."

> 君**さえ**　いれ**ば**、他に　何も　いらない。
> "If only you're here, I don't need anything else."

> 薬を　飲み**さえ**　すれ**ば**、すぐ　治る。
> "If you just take the medicine, you'll get better right away."

> 天気**さえ**　よけれ**ば**、ピクニックに　行こう。
> "If only the weather is good, let's go on a picnic."

> 健康で**さえ**　あれ**ば**、何でも　できる。
> "If you're just healthy, you can do anything."

### Nuance

さえ...ば always implies that the stated condition is sufficient on its own. It often carries a tone of longing (if only...) or simplification (all you need to do is...).

## 8.9 Reading Passage

次の文章は、テレビのトーク番組で、ある作家がインタビューを受けている場面です。

---

司会者：先生の新しい小説、大変話題になっていますね。

作家：ありがとうございます。読んでいただけただけでもありがたいのに、こうして話題にしていただけるとは。

司会者：先生の作品は日本に限らず、海外でも翻訳されているそうですね。

作家：ええ。英語ばかりか、フランス語や中国語にも訳されています。自分でも驚いています。

司会者：すごいですね。若い頃から才能があったのでしょうか。

作家：いえいえ。若い頃は才能どころか、自信さえなかったんです。最初の十年は、出版はおろか、原稿を読んでもらうことすらできませんでした。

司会者：そうだったんですか。

作家：ええ。でも、書くことさえやめなければ、いつか誰かに届くと信じていました。苦しい時期があったからこそ、今の自分があると思います。

司会者：急いでいる時に限って電車が来ないように、うまくいかない時期もあったということですね。

作家：<ruby>正<rt>まさ</rt></ruby>にそうです。投稿しても返事すら来ない。落選ばかりか、ペンネームさえ覚えてもらえない。それでも書き続けた。諦めなかったことこそが、今につながっていると思います。

司会者：健康でさえあれば、まだまだ書き続けられますね。

作家：ええ、体が元気な限り、書き続けるつもりです。

---

**Translation:**

The following is a scene from a TV talk show where a writer is being interviewed.

---

Host: Your new novel has been getting a lot of attention.

Writer: Thank you. I'm grateful just to have people read it, and to have it talked about like this is more than I could ask for.

Host: I hear your works are translated not only in Japan but overseas as well.

Writer: Yes. They've been translated not only into English but into French and Chinese too. Even I'm surprised.

Host: That's amazing. Did you have talent from a young age?

Writer: Not at all. When I was young, far from having talent, I didn't even have confidence. For the first ten years, let alone getting published, I couldn't even get someone to read my manuscripts.

Host: Is that so?

Writer: Yes. But I believed that if I just didn't stop writing, my work would reach someone someday. It is precisely because I went through that difficult period that I am who I am today.

Host: Like how the train never comes when you're in a hurry — there are times when things just don't go well.

Writer: Exactly. I'd submit work and not even get a reply. Not only would I lose competitions, but they wouldn't even remember my pen name. But I kept writing. I think it is precisely the fact that I didn't give up that led to where I am now.

Host: As long as you're healthy, you can keep writing for a long time to come.

Writer: Yes, as long as my body holds up, I intend to keep writing.
