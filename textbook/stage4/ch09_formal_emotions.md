# Chapter 9 — Formal Actions and Emotions

This chapter brings together two related themes: formal expressions for being compelled to act and expressions for emotions that cannot be suppressed. These patterns are essential for reading news, formal essays, and literary writing. They also appear in JLPT N2 testing with high frequency.

The first half of the chapter covers expressions used in formal or written contexts to describe situations where someone is forced into action or where the speaker makes an emphatic identification. The second half covers a graduated system of expressions for uncontrollable feelings — from mild to intense — that every Japanese learner needs to distinguish. Several of these patterns have near-synonyms that differ only in formality or intensity, so comparison tables are included throughout.

All patterns in this chapter attach to plain forms unless otherwise noted.

---

## 9.1 ～を余儀なくされる — "Be Forced To" (Formal)

を余儀なくされる is a formal expression meaning "to be forced to" or "to be compelled to." The word 余儀ない means "unavoidable" or "having no alternative." This pattern appears in news articles, academic writing, and formal reports — almost never in casual conversation.

The active form を余儀なくする ("to force someone to") also exists but is less common.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + を余儀なくされる |

Note: The noun is typically an action noun (a noun that can take する): 変更, 中止, 撤退, 延期, etc.

### Examples

> 台風の　影響で、イベントの　中止を　余儀なくされた。
> "Due to the typhoon, the event was forced to be canceled."

> 会社は　経営悪化により、大規模な　リストラを　余儀なくされた。
> "The company was forced to carry out large-scale restructuring due to deteriorating management."

> 住民は　洪水のため、避難を　余儀なくされた。
> "Residents were forced to evacuate because of the flooding."

> 計画の　変更を　余儀なくされたが、結果的には　良い方向に　進んだ。
> "We were forced to change the plan, but ultimately things progressed in a good direction."

> 選手は　怪我により、今シーズンの　引退を　余儀なくされた。
> "The athlete was forced into retirement this season due to injury."

### Formality comparison

| Expression | Formality | Context |
|---|---|---|
| ～しなければならなくなった | Neutral | Conversation, general writing |
| ～せざるを得なくなった | Formal | Written, somewhat formal speech |
| ～を余儀なくされた | Very formal | News, reports, academic writing |

All three express compulsion, but を余儀なくされる is reserved for serious, formal situations. You would not use it for everyday inconveniences.

## 9.2 ～を禁じ得ない — "Cannot Suppress" (Emotion)

を禁じ得ない means "cannot suppress" or "cannot help but feel." It is used for strong emotions that arise involuntarily. The verb 禁じる means "to prohibit" or "to suppress," and 得ない means "cannot." Together: "one cannot suppress (the emotion)."

This is a highly formal, literary expression. It appears in editorials, speeches, eulogies, and formal commentary.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun (emotion) + を禁じ得ない |

Common emotion nouns used with this pattern: 涙 (tears), 驚き (surprise), 怒り (anger), 感動 (being moved), 同情 (sympathy), 失望 (disappointment).

### Examples

> 被災地の　映像を　見て、涙を　禁じ得なかった。
> "Seeing the footage of the disaster area, I could not hold back my tears."

> この判決には　驚きを　禁じ得ない。
> "I cannot help but feel surprise at this verdict."

> 彼の　無責任な　発言に、怒りを　禁じ得ない。
> "I cannot suppress my anger at his irresponsible remarks."

> 長年の　努力が　実を　結んだことに、感動を　禁じ得ない。
> "I cannot help but be moved that years of effort have borne fruit."

> このような　結果に　なったことに、失望を　禁じ得ない。
> "I cannot help but feel disappointment at this outcome."

## 9.3 ～てならない / ～てたまらない / ～てしょうがない — "Unbearably, Can't Help But Feel"

These three expressions all convey that a feeling or sensation is so strong that the speaker cannot control or suppress it. They attach to the te-form of adjectives and verbs of feeling, and they form a scale of formality and intensity.

### Formation

| Preceding element | + ならない / たまらない / しょうがない |
|---|---|
| い-adjective (て form) | 寂しくて + ならない |
| な-adjective + で | 心配で + たまらない |
| Verb (て form) | 気になって + しょうがない |

Note: These patterns are used with feelings, sensations, and desires — not with voluntary actions. You can say 会いたくてたまらない ("I want to see you so badly") but not 食べてたまらない in the sense of doing the action.

### Examples

> 合格の　結果が　気になって　ならない。
> "I can't help but be anxious about the exam results."

> 暑くて　たまらない。エアコンを　つけてくれ。
> "It's unbearably hot. Turn on the air conditioner."

> あの映画の　結末が　気になって　しょうがない。
> "I can't stop thinking about how that movie ended."

> 故郷が　恋しくて　ならない。
> "I miss my hometown unbearably."

> 歯が　痛くて　たまらない。
> "My tooth hurts unbearably."

> 彼女のことが　心配で　しょうがない。
> "I can't help worrying about her."

> 新しい　仕事が　楽しみで　ならない。
> "I can't help but look forward to the new job."

### Formality and intensity comparison

| Expression | Formality | Intensity | Typical context |
|---|---|---|---|
| ～てならない | Formal / literary | High | Written language, formal speech |
| ～てたまらない | Neutral | High | Both spoken and written |
| ～てしょうがない | Casual | Moderate-high | Casual speech, informal writing |

All three are grammatically interchangeable in most cases, but the register differs. ならない sounds most literary; しょうがない sounds most colloquial. たまらない sits in the middle and is the most versatile.

### Variant: ～てしかたがない

しかたがない ("it can't be helped") can also be used in this pattern with the same meaning as しょうがない. しかたがない is slightly more formal:

> 眠くて　しかたがない。
> "I'm unbearably sleepy."

## 9.4 ～ずにはいられない / ～ないではいられない — "Can't Help Doing"

These two expressions convey that the speaker cannot resist doing something — the action happens involuntarily because the feeling is too strong. The English equivalent is "can't help doing" or "can't help but do."

Where てならない / てたまらない describe a state or feeling, ずにはいられない / ないではいられない describe an action that results from an uncontrollable impulse.

### Formation

| Pattern | Base |
|---|---|
| ずにはいられない | Verb (ない stem) + ずにはいられない |
| ないではいられない | Verb (ない form) + ではいられない |

Note: する becomes せずにはいられない (using the classical negative ず). For all other verbs, replace ない with ず: 笑わない → 笑わずにはいられない.

### Examples

> あの映画を　見たら、泣かずには　いられなかった。
> "When I watched that movie, I couldn't help crying."

> 彼の　冗談を　聞くと、笑わないでは　いられない。
> "When I hear his jokes, I can't help but laugh."

> その話を　聞いて、心配せずには　いられなかった。
> "Hearing that story, I couldn't help worrying."

> あんなに　<ruby>酷<rt>ひど</rt></ruby>い　ことを　されたら、怒らずには　いられない。
> "If someone does something that terrible to you, you can't help but get angry."

> 美しい　景色を　見ると、写真を　撮らないでは　いられない。
> "When I see beautiful scenery, I can't help but take photos."

### ずにはいられない vs ないではいられない

These two are essentially interchangeable in meaning. The difference is purely stylistic:

| Expression | Register |
|---|---|
| ずにはいられない | Slightly more literary / formal |
| ないではいられない | Slightly more conversational |

In practice, ずにはいられない is more commonly seen in writing, while ないではいられない may feel more natural in speech. Both are correct in all contexts.

### How 9.3 and 9.4 relate

| Pattern | What it describes | Example |
|---|---|---|
| てならない / てたまらない / てしょうがない | An uncontrollable **feeling or state** | 心配でたまらない (I'm unbearably worried) |
| ずにはいられない / ないではいられない | An uncontrollable **action** | 心配せずにはいられない (I can't help worrying) |

The feeling patterns (9.3) describe what you feel. The action patterns (9.4) describe what you end up doing because of what you feel.

## 9.5 ～に他ならない — "Is Nothing Other Than"

に他ならない makes a strong identification: "X is nothing other than Y" or "X is precisely Y." It is used to assert that the true nature or cause of something is exactly what the speaker states. This is a formal, emphatic expression found in essays, editorials, and formal speeches.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + に他ならない |
| Phrase (nominalized) + に他ならない |

### Examples

> これは　差別に　他ならない。
> "This is nothing other than discrimination."

> 彼が　成功した　のは、努力の　結果に　他ならない。
> "His success is nothing other than the result of effort."

> この問題が　解決しないのは、コミュニケーション不足に　他ならない。
> "The reason this problem isn't being solved is nothing other than a lack of communication."

> 彼女を　選んだのは、彼女が　最も　適任だからに　他ならない。
> "The reason we chose her is none other than the fact that she is the most qualified."

> 今回の　事故は、管理体制の　不備に　他ならない。
> "This accident is nothing other than a failure in the management system."

### Usage note

に他ならない always conveys certainty and emphasis. The speaker is not hedging or guessing — they are making a definitive statement about what something truly is. It is too emphatic for casual conversation and would sound unnatural in everyday speech.

## 9.6 ～に相違ない — "Undoubtedly" (Formal)

に相違ない is the formal equivalent of に違いない, which was covered in earlier stages. 相違 means "difference" or "discrepancy," so に相違ない literally means "there is no discrepancy" — that is, "it is certainly the case." This expression appears in legal documents, formal writing, and academic prose.

### Formation

| Preceding element | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Verb (plain form) | 起きたに相違ない |
| い-adjective | 難しいに相違ない |
| な-adjective + である | 重要であるに相違ない |
| Noun + である | 事実であるに相違ない |

### Examples

> 彼が　犯人であるに　相違ない。
> "He is undoubtedly the culprit."

> この遺跡は　千年以上前の　ものに　相違ない。
> "These ruins are undoubtedly from over a thousand years ago."

> このまま　放置すれば、状況は　悪化するに　相違ない。
> "If left as is, the situation will undoubtedly worsen."

> 彼女の　証言は　事実に　相違ない。
> "Her testimony is undoubtedly factual."

> この計画が　成功すれば、大きな　利益が　得られるに　相違ない。
> "If this plan succeeds, substantial profits will undoubtedly be gained."

### Formality comparison: certainty expressions

| Expression | Formality | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| に違いない | Neutral | Everyday speech and writing |
| に相違ない | Very formal | Legal, academic, formal prose |
| に決まっている | Casual | Spoken, emphatic, sometimes dismissive |

All three express strong certainty, but their registers are very different. に決まっている would be rude in a formal document; に相違ない would sound bizarre in casual conversation. に違いない is the safe, all-purpose middle ground.

### Formality and intensity: full comparison table

The following table summarizes all the patterns in this chapter by formality and function:

| Function | Casual | Neutral | Formal / Literary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Compulsion ("forced to") | ～しなきゃ | ～せざるを得ない | ～を余儀なくされる |
| Suppressed emotion | — | — | ～を禁じ得ない |
| Uncontrollable feeling | ～てしょうがない | ～てたまらない | ～てならない |
| Uncontrollable action | ～ないではいられない | — | ～ずにはいられない |
| Identification ("is precisely") | — | — | ～に他ならない |
| Certainty ("undoubtedly") | ～に決まっている | ～に違いない | ～に相違ない |

## 9.7 Reading Passage

次の文章は、ある新聞の社説の一部です。自然災害への対策について論じています。

---

先月発生した大地震は、多くの住民に避難を余儀なくさせた。家屋の倒壊、道路の寸断、ライフラインの途絶。被災地の映像を見るたびに、涙を禁じ得ない。

しかし、悲しんでばかりはいられない。我々が今なすべきことは、同様の災害が再び起きた時に被害を最小限に抑える体制を構築することに他ならない。

過去の教訓を生かさなければ、同じ悲劇が繰り返されるに相違ない。防災訓練の重要性は、繰り返し指摘されてきた。にもかかわらず、訓練への参加率は年々低下している。この現状を憂慮せずにはいられない。

「自分だけは大丈夫だ」という意識こそが、最も危険な考え方である。災害は、準備していない者のもとに容赦なくやってくる。備えが不十分であれば、助かるはずの命さえ失われかねない。

被災された方々の苦しみを思うと、心が痛くてならない。だからこそ、今こそ行動すべきである。一人一人が防災意識を高めることが、社会全体の安全につながる。これは理想論ではなく、我々全員の責任に他ならない。

---

**Translation:**

The following is an excerpt from a newspaper editorial. It discusses measures against natural disasters.

---

The major earthquake that occurred last month forced many residents to evacuate. Collapsed buildings, severed roads, disrupted lifelines. Every time I see footage of the disaster area, I cannot hold back my tears.

However, we cannot simply remain in grief. What we must do now is nothing other than building a system that minimizes damage when a similar disaster strikes again.

If we do not make use of lessons from the past, the same tragedy will undoubtedly be repeated. The importance of disaster preparedness drills has been pointed out repeatedly. Nevertheless, participation rates in drills decline year after year. I cannot help but feel concern about this current situation.

The belief that "it won't happen to me" is precisely the most dangerous way of thinking. Disasters come mercilessly to those who are unprepared. If preparedness is insufficient, even lives that should have been saved may be lost.

When I think of the suffering of those who were affected, my heart aches unbearably. That is precisely why now is the time to act. Each individual raising their disaster preparedness awareness leads to the safety of society as a whole. This is not idealism — it is nothing other than the responsibility of every one of us.
