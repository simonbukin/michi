# Chapter 18 — Business and Economics

This chapter builds your vocabulary for reading and understanding Japanese business news, corporate communications, and economic reporting. Japan's economy is the fourth-largest in the world, and Japanese-language media covers business and economics with a depth and specificity that English translations rarely capture. If you plan to work in Japan, work with Japanese companies, or simply follow Japanese economic developments, the vocabulary in this chapter is essential.

This is a thematic vocabulary chapter. The grammar is not new — you already have the tools to read business Japanese from Stages 3 and 4. What you need now is the domain-specific vocabulary that makes business and economic texts accessible. The chapter is organized into two main sections: business operations and economic concepts. The reading passage at the end is a business news article that integrates vocabulary from both sections.

### Business document vocabulary

Reading Japanese business news also requires familiarity with the terms used in corporate announcements and financial reports.

| 単語 | 読み | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 決算 | けっさん | financial settlement, earnings report |
| 連結決算 | れんけつけっさん | consolidated financial results |
| 中間決算 | ちゅうかんけっさん | interim financial results |
| 業績 | ぎょうせき | business performance |
| 見通し | みとおし | outlook, forecast |
| 業績予想 | ぎょうせきよそう | earnings forecast |
| 前年度 | ぜんねんど | previous fiscal year |
| 前期 | ぜんき | previous period |
| 増収 | ぞうしゅう | revenue increase |
| 減収 | げんしゅう | revenue decrease |
| 増益 | ぞうえき | profit increase |
| 減益 | げんえき | profit decrease |

These terms combine predictably. Once you know 増 (increase), 減 (decrease), 収 (revenue), and 益 (profit), you can decode any combination.

> 増収増益：revenue up, profits up (the best result)
> 増収減益：revenue up, profits down (growing but less efficient)
> 減収増益：revenue down, profits up (shrinking but more efficient)
> 減収減益：revenue down, profits down (the worst result)

### Common economic expressions

The following expressions appear frequently in business news. They combine vocabulary you already know with domain-specific usage.

> 景気が回復する
> "The economy recovers."

> 業績が悪化する / 改善する
> "Business results deteriorate / improve."

> 前年比～パーセント増 / 減
> "A ~% increase / decrease compared to the previous year."

> 過去最高を記録する
> "To set a record high."

> 市場が拡大する / 縮小する
> "The market expands / contracts."

> 需要が伸びる / 落ち込む
> "Demand grows / drops."

> ～に上る / ～に達する
> "To amount to / to reach (a figure)."

> 前期比
> "Compared to the previous period."

> 大台に乗る
> "To reach a major milestone (in numbers)."

## 18.3 Reading Passage

次の文章は、経済紙の記事です。

---

大手電機メーカーのミライ電機は十五日、二〇二五年度の連結決算を発表した。売上高は前年度比八パーセント増の二兆三千億円で、三年連続の増収となった。営業利益は千五百億円と過去最高を更新し、純利益も一千億円の大台を初めて超えた。

好調な業績の背景には、半導体関連事業の急成長がある。世界的なAI需要の拡大を受け、同社の半導体製造装置の受注が大幅に増加した。半導体事業の売上高は前年度比三十五パーセント増と、全社の成長を牽引した。

一方、家電事業は苦戦が続いている。円安による原材料費の高騰に加え、海外メーカーとの価格競争が激化しており、同事業の営業利益は前年度比十五パーセント減となった。同社は不採算製品からの撤退を進めるとともに、高付加価値製品へのシフトを加速させる方針だ。

山田誠一社長は記者会見で、「半導体事業への投資を今後三年間で倍増させる。AIとロボティクスの分野で世界トップクラスの技術力を目指す」と述べた。また、海外売上比率を現在の四十パーセントから五十五パーセントに引き上げる中期経営計画を発表した。

アナリストの間では、半導体事業への集中投資を評価する声がある一方で、「家電事業の立て直しが遅れれば、収益構造の偏りがリスクになる」との指摘も出ている。株価は決算発表後に三パーセント上昇し、市場は好意的に反応した。

---

**Translation:**

Major electronics manufacturer Mirai Electric announced its consolidated financial results for fiscal year 2025 on the fifteenth. Revenue increased eight percent year-on-year to 2.3 trillion yen, marking three consecutive years of revenue growth. Operating profit reached 150 billion yen, setting a new record high, and net profit surpassed the 100-billion-yen milestone for the first time.

Behind the strong performance was the rapid growth of the semiconductor-related business. Driven by the global expansion of AI demand, orders for the company's semiconductor manufacturing equipment increased significantly. Revenue from the semiconductor business rose thirty-five percent year-on-year, driving overall corporate growth.

On the other hand, the home electronics business continues to struggle. In addition to rising raw material costs due to the weak yen, price competition with overseas manufacturers has intensified, and the division's operating profit fell fifteen percent year-on-year. The company plans to accelerate its withdrawal from unprofitable products while shifting toward high-value-added products.

President Seiichi Yamada stated at a press conference, "We will double our investment in the semiconductor business over the next three years. We aim to achieve world-class technological capability in the fields of AI and robotics." He also announced a medium-term management plan to raise the overseas sales ratio from the current forty percent to fifty-five percent.

Among analysts, while there are voices praising the concentrated investment in the semiconductor business, there are also observations that "if the restructuring of the home electronics business is delayed, the imbalance in the earnings structure could become a risk." The stock price rose three percent following the earnings announcement, as the market reacted favorably.
