# Chapter 2 — Worth, Sufficiency, and Judgment

This chapter presents six patterns used to evaluate whether something meets a standard, deserves recognition, or falls short of expectations. These expressions are essential for formal writing, literary criticism, academic prose, and editorial commentary. They allow speakers and writers to pass judgment on the worth, adequacy, or appropriateness of actions, people, and things.

Several of these patterns draw on classical Japanese grammar, particularly まじき and ずとも. Understanding their modern usage requires recognizing that they survive as fixed or semi-fixed forms in contemporary formal Japanese, even though their grammatical origins are archaic. The chapter closes with two concessive patterns — ないまでも and ずとも — that express the idea of "even if not quite reaching" a certain standard.

All patterns in this chapter belong to formal or written registers unless otherwise noted.

---

## 2.1 ～に足る / ～に足りる — "Worthy of"

に足る (にたる) and に足りる (にたりる) express that something is "worthy of" or "sufficient for" a given purpose. The verb 足る / 足りる means "to be sufficient," so the literal sense is "sufficient for (doing something)." The negative form に足らない / に足りない means "not worthy of."

に足る is slightly more literary than に足りる, but both are formal.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Verb (dictionary form) + に足る / に足りる |
| Noun + に足る / に足りる |

### Examples

> 彼は　信頼に　足る　人物だ。
> "He is a person worthy of trust."

> この研究は　注目に　足る　成果を　上げた。
> "This research has produced results worthy of attention."

> 取るに　足らない　問題に　時間を　費やすべきではない。
> "You should not spend time on problems not worthy of attention."

> 彼女の　実績は、昇進に　足りる　ものだ。
> "Her track record is sufficient for promotion."

> 論じるに　足る　テーマが　見つからない。
> "I cannot find a topic worthy of discussion."

### に足る vs に足りる

| Expression | Register |
|---|---|
| に足る | More literary, often in fixed phrases (信頼に足る, 取るに足らない) |
| に足りる | Slightly more modern, same meaning |

The negative 取るに足らない ("not worth taking up, trivial") is one of the most common fixed expressions using this pattern.

## 2.2 ～に値する — "Deserving of, Worth"

に値する (にあたいする) means "to be deserving of" or "to be worth." The noun 値 means "value" or "worth." This pattern is used in formal evaluation — praising or condemning something as worthy of a particular response.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + に値する |
| Verb (dictionary form) + に値する |

### Examples

> この発見は　注目に　値する。
> "This discovery is worthy of attention."

> 彼の　勇気は　賞賛に　値する。
> "His courage deserves praise."

> この映画は　一見に　値する　傑作だ。
> "This film is a masterpiece worth seeing."

> 批判に　値する　行為だと　言わざるを　得ない。
> "I must say it is an act deserving of criticism."

> 読むに　値する　本は　そう　多くない。
> "Books worth reading are not so numerous."

### に値する vs に足る

| Expression | Nuance |
|---|---|
| に値する | "deserves" — evaluative, implies the thing has earned or merited something |
| に足る | "worthy of, sufficient for" — more about meeting a standard |

に値する carries a stronger evaluative judgment. 賞賛に値する ("deserves praise") implies active merit. 信頼に足る ("worthy of trust") implies meeting a threshold. In many contexts they overlap, but the connotation differs slightly.

---

## 2.3 ～に堪える — "Withstand / Worthy of"

に堪える (にたえる) is the affirmative counterpart of に堪えない (covered in Chapter 1). While に堪えない means "unbearable," に堪える means "can withstand" or, by extension, "is worthy of" — that is, something is good enough to endure scrutiny or appreciation.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + に堪える |
| Verb (dictionary form) + に堪える |

### Examples

> この作品は　十分に　鑑賞に　堪える　出来だ。
> "This work is of a quality worthy of appreciation."

> 長年の　使用に　堪える　丈夫な　素材を　使用しています。
> "We use durable materials that can withstand years of use."

> 批判に　堪える　論文を　書かなければならない。
> "You must write a paper that can withstand criticism."

> この建物は　大地震にも　堪える　設計に　なっている。
> "This building is designed to withstand even a major earthquake."

> 読むに　堪える　文章を　書くには、訓練が　必要だ。
> "Training is necessary to write prose that is worth reading."

### に堪える vs に堪えない — Summary

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| に堪える | "can endure / worthy of" | 鑑賞に堪える (worthy of appreciation) |
| に堪えない (+ emotion noun) | "deeply filled with" | 感謝に堪えない (deeply grateful) |
| に堪えない (+ verb) | "unbearable to" | 見るに堪えない (unbearable to watch) |

## 2.4 ～まじき — "Unbecoming of" (Classical)

まじき is the attributive form of the classical auxiliary まじ, which expresses negative obligation or prohibition ("should not"). In modern Japanese, まじき survives in the fixed pattern あるまじき, meaning "unbecoming of" or "should not exist in." It is used to condemn behavior as inappropriate for someone of a given status.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Noun + にあるまじき + Noun |
| Noun + としてあるまじき + Noun |

Note: The pattern is essentially fixed. The noun before にあるまじき indicates the status or role (teacher, doctor, leader), and the noun after indicates the condemned behavior.

### Examples

> 教師に　あるまじき　行為として、厳しい　処分を　受けた。
> "As an act unbecoming of a teacher, severe disciplinary action was taken."

> 医者に　あるまじき　発言だ。
> "That is a remark unbecoming of a doctor."

> 指導者として　あるまじき　振る舞いに、批判が　集まった。
> "Criticism gathered over behavior unbecoming of a leader."

> プロとして　あるまじき　ミスを　犯した。
> "A mistake unbecoming of a professional was made."

> 公務員に　あるまじき　不正が　発覚した。
> "Corruption unbecoming of a public servant was uncovered."

### Usage note

まじき is archaic grammar that has survived as a fixed phrase in modern Japanese. You do not need to learn the full conjugation of まじ — simply memorize the pattern [role] + にあるまじき + [bad behavior]. This pattern always carries condemnation; it is never used neutrally or positively.

The related form 許すまじき ("unforgivable") also appears occasionally: 許すまじき犯罪 ("an unforgivable crime").

## 2.5 ～ないまでも — "Even If Not to the Extent of"

ないまでも expresses a concession: "even if it doesn't go as far as X, at least Y." The speaker acknowledges that a high standard may not be met, but argues that a more moderate standard should still apply.

### Formation

| Pattern |
|---|
| Verb (ない form) + までも |
| Noun + で(は)ないまでも |
| な-adjective + で(は)ないまでも |

### Examples

> 完璧では　ないまでも、十分な　出来だと　思う。
> "Even if it's not perfect, I think it's of sufficient quality."

> 賛成しないまでも、理解は　してほしい。
> "Even if you don't agree, I'd like you to at least understand."

> プロ級では　ないまでも、かなりの　腕前だ。
> "Even if not at the professional level, it's considerable skill."

> 毎日とは　言わないまでも、週に　三回は　運動すべきだ。
> "Even if I won't say every day, you should exercise at least three times a week."

> 全額では　ないまでも、半分は　返済してほしい。
> "Even if not the full amount, I'd like you to repay at least half."

### Structure of the concession

The logic of ないまでも always follows this pattern:

| Part | Role | Example |
|---|---|---|
| X ないまでも | "even if not X" (the higher standard) | 完璧ではないまでも |
| Y | "at least Y" (the moderate standard) | 十分な出来だ |

## 2.6 ～ずとも — "Even Without Doing" (Classical)

ずとも uses the classical negative ず (equivalent to modern ない) plus とも ("even if"). It means "even without doing" or "even if one does not do." This is a formal, literary expression.

### Formation

| Verb type | Formation |
|---|---|
| 五段 verbs | ない stem + ずとも: 言わずとも |
| 一段 verbs | ない stem + ずとも: 見ずとも |
| する | せずとも |
| くる | こずとも |

### Examples

> 言わずとも　分かる　関係だ。
> "It's a relationship where you understand without being told."

> 聞かずとも、結果は　目に見えている。
> "Even without asking, the result is obvious."

> 説明せずとも、彼なら　理解できるだろう。
> "Even without an explanation, he should be able to understand."

> 急がずとも、時間は　十分にある。
> "Even without hurrying, there is plenty of time."

> 無理せずとも、自然に　うまくいくだろう。
> "Even without forcing it, things will probably go well naturally."

### ずとも vs なくても

| Expression | Register | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ずとも | Formal / literary | 言わずとも分かる |
| なくても | Neutral | 言わなくても分かる |

The meaning is identical. ずとも is the elevated, literary version of なくても. In conversation, always use なくても. In formal writing or set phrases, ずとも appears naturally.

## 2.7 Reading Passage

次の文章は、ある文芸評論家がとある新人作家の小説について書いた書評です。

---

近年、注目に値する新人作家が次々と登場しているが、その中でも田中誠一氏の最新作『沈黙の庭』は特筆すべき一冊である。

率直に言えば、文体はまだ完成されたとは言いがたい。プロ級ではないまでも、読むに堪える文章力は十分に備わっている。何より、物語の構成力が際立っている。複雑な時間軸を操りながらも、読者を混乱させない手腕は、信頼に足るものだ。

主人公の葛藤の描写は、鑑賞に堪える深みがある。表面的な感情の羅列ではなく、人間の内面に切り込む筆致は、賞賛に値する。言わずとも伝わる登場人物たちの心情が、行間から立ち上ってくる。

一方で、終盤の展開にはやや強引さが感じられる。作家としてあるまじきとまでは言わないが、読者の信頼を損ないかねない構成上の弱点である。完璧ではないまでも、この弱点さえ克服すれば、田中氏は長く読み継がれる作家になるだろう。

今後の成長を期待せずにはいられない。

---

**Translation:**

The following is a book review written by a literary critic about a new author's novel.

---

In recent years, new authors worthy of attention have appeared one after another, but among them, Seiichi Tanaka's latest work "The Silent Garden" is a particularly noteworthy volume.

To be frank, the writing style cannot yet be called complete. Even if not at the professional level, the ability to write prose worth reading is more than present. Above all, the compositional skill of the narrative stands out. The skill of manipulating a complex timeline without confusing the reader is worthy of trust.

The depiction of the protagonist's inner conflict has a depth worthy of appreciation. Rather than a superficial enumeration of emotions, the brushwork that cuts into the human interior deserves praise. The feelings of the characters, understood without being stated, rise up from between the lines.

On the other hand, the development toward the end feels somewhat forced. I would not go so far as to say it is unbecoming of an author, but it is a structural weakness that could undermine the reader's trust. Even if not perfect, if this weakness alone is overcome, Mr. Tanaka will likely become an author read for a long time to come.

I cannot help but look forward to his future growth.
