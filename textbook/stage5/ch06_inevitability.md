# Chapter 6 — Inevitability and Compulsion: Formal

Some things cannot be helped. Some outcomes are unavoidable. Some actions must be taken whether one wants to or not. Japanese has a remarkably nuanced vocabulary for expressing these ideas — far more varied than the English "can't help but" or "have no choice but to." Each expression in this chapter captures a different facet of inevitability: the force that compels an emotional reaction, the external pressure that leaves no alternative, the social obligation that cannot be escaped, and the urgency that makes continued inaction impossible.

Two of the expressions in this chapter — ～を余儀なくされる and ～ざるを得ない — appeared in Stage 4. They return here not as review exercises but as grammar whose deeper structure can now be fully appreciated. With your understanding of classical Japanese forms growing, the archaic ざる (the negative attributive of ず) and the literary flavor of 余儀なく will make complete sense. The three new patterns — ～ずにはおかない, ～ないではすまない, and ～てはいられない — round out your ability to express every shade of compulsion and inevitability at the N1 level.

---

## 6.1 ～ずにはおかない / ～ないではおかない — "Cannot Help but (Cause)"

～ずにはおかない and ～ないではおかない express that something inevitably produces a certain effect — it "cannot fail to" cause a reaction. The subject (often a work of art, an event, or a person's actions) possesses such power that the result is unavoidable. The おかない comes from 置く in its sense of "to leave (as is)" — literally, "cannot leave it without doing."

The ～ず form uses the classical negative stem; ～ないで is its modern equivalent. Both are correct; ～ずには is slightly more formal.

### Formation

| Verb type | ～ずにはおかない | ～ないではおかない |
|---|---|---|
| Godan (書く) | 書かずにはおかない | 書かないではおかない |
| Ichidan (感じる) | 感じずにはおかない | 感じないではおかない |
| する | せずにはおかない | しないではおかない |
| くる | こずにはおかない | こないではおかない |

Note: The subject is usually a thing, event, or situation — not a person acting deliberately.

### Examples

> この映画は　人を　感動させずには　おかない。
> "This film cannot help but move people."

> 彼の　スピーチは　聴衆を　考えさせずには　おかなかった。
> "His speech could not fail to make the audience think."

> あの事件は　社会に　衝撃を　与えずには　おかないだろう。
> "That incident will surely send a shockwave through society."

> 彼女の　歌声は　聞く者を　魅了せずには　おかない。
> "Her singing voice cannot fail to captivate those who hear it."

> この問題を　放置すれば、深刻な　結果を　招かないでは　おかない。
> "If this problem is left alone, it will inevitably bring about serious consequences."

### Key Point

～ずにはおかない describes an irresistible force or inevitable outcome. It is not about the speaker's personal inability to resist (that would be ～ずにはいられない, "I can't help but..."). Compare:

| Expression | Subject | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 泣かずにはいられない | The speaker | "I can't help but cry." |
| 人を泣かせずにはおかない | The thing/event | "It cannot fail to make people cry." |

## 6.2 ～を余儀なくされる — Review in Extended Formal Contexts

～を余儀なくされる means "to be forced to" or "to have no choice but to." It appeared in Stage 4, where its basic usage was introduced. Here we examine its full range in formal writing.

余儀ない is a literary adjective meaning "unavoidable." Combined with される (passive of する), the expression literally means "to be made unavoidable." The active form ～を余儀なくさせる ("to force someone to") also exists.

### Formation

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Noun + を余儀なくされる | 変更を余儀なくされる |
| Noun + を余儀なくさせる | 撤退を余儀なくさせる |

Note: The noun before を is typically a change, departure, or unwelcome action — something the subject would not choose freely.

### Examples

> 台風の　影響で、イベントは　中止を　余儀なく　された。
> "Due to the typhoon, the event was forced into cancellation."

> 財政難が　多くの　企業に　人員削減を　余儀なく　させている。
> "Financial difficulties are forcing many companies to reduce staff."

> 感染症の　拡大により、学校は　休校を　余儀なく　された。
> "Due to the spread of the infectious disease, schools were forced to close."

> 長年　住んだ　土地からの　退去を　余儀なく　された　住民も　多い。
> "There are also many residents who were forced to evacuate from land where they had lived for years."

> 戦争が　彼に　祖国を　離れることを　余儀なく　させた。
> "The war forced him to leave his homeland."

### Common Nouns with ～を余儀なくされる

| 変更 (change) | 中止 (cancellation) | 撤退 (withdrawal) |
|---|---|---|
| 延期 (postponement) | 辞任 (resignation) | 退去 (evacuation) |

## 6.3 ～ざるを得ない — Review with Classical ざる

～ざるを得ない means "cannot help but" or "have no choice but to." This expression was introduced in Stage 4 and is one of the most frequently tested N2/N1 grammar points. Now that you have encountered more classical Japanese, the structure becomes transparent.

ざる is the classical negative attributive form of the auxiliary ず. It modifies the noun implied before を得ない. The literal meaning is: "one cannot obtain (the state of) not doing" — in other words, "not doing it is not an option."

### Formation

| Verb type | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Godan (行く) | 行かざるを得ない |
| Ichidan (認める) | 認めざるを得ない |
| する | せざるを得ない |
| くる | こざるを得ない |

### Examples

> 証拠が　ある以上、事実と　認めざるを　得ない。
> "Given that there is evidence, I have no choice but to acknowledge it as fact."

> 体調が　悪くても、仕事に　行かざるを　得ない。
> "Even if I feel unwell, I have no choice but to go to work."

> この状況では　計画を　変更せざるを　得ない。
> "In this situation, we have no choice but to change the plan."

> 上司の　命令であれば、従わざるを　得ない。
> "If it is the boss's order, I have no choice but to comply."

> 予算の　制約上、規模を　縮小せざるを　得なかった。
> "Due to budget constraints, we had no choice but to reduce the scale."

### Classical ざる Explained

The auxiliary ず conjugates as follows in classical Japanese:

| Form | Classical | Modern equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 未然形 | ず | — |
| 連用形 | ず | ～ないで / ～なくて |
| 終止形 | ず | ～ない |
| 連体形 | ざる | ～ない (attributive) |
| 已然形 | ざれ | — |
| 命令形 | ざれ | — |

In ～ざるを得ない, ざる is the 連体形 (attributive form), modifying an implied noun. This is the same ざる that appears in 見ざる、聞かざる、言わざる — the famous "three monkeys" (see no evil, hear no evil, speak no evil).

## 6.4 ～ないではすまない / ～ずにはすまない — "Cannot Get Away Without"

～ないではすまない and ～ずにはすまない express that a certain action is socially or morally unavoidable — one "cannot get away without doing" it. The すまない comes from 済む (to be settled, to be sufficient), in the negative: "it will not be settled unless..."

The nuance is one of social or moral obligation: the situation demands that the action be taken, and failing to act would leave the matter unresolved or would violate expectations.

### Formation

| Verb type | ～ないではすまない | ～ずにはすまない |
|---|---|---|
| Godan (謝る) | 謝らないではすまない | 謝らずにはすまない |
| Ichidan (返す→返す) | 返さないではすまない | 返さずにはすまない |
| する | しないではすまない | せずにはすまない |

### Examples

> これだけの　迷惑を　かけたのだから、謝らないでは　すまない。
> "Having caused this much trouble, I cannot get away without apologizing."

> 借りた　ものは　返さずには　すまない。
> "You cannot get away without returning what you borrowed."

> 上司に　報告せずには　すまない　事態だ。
> "This is a situation where we cannot avoid reporting to the boss."

> ここまで　大きな　ミスを　したら、責任を　取らないでは　すまないだろう。
> "Having made such a major mistake, one probably cannot avoid taking responsibility."

> 法律に　違反した以上、罰金を　払わずには　すまない。
> "Having violated the law, one cannot get away without paying a fine."

### ～ずにはすまない vs ～ざるを得ない

Both express "must do," but from different angles:

| Expression | Focus | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| ～ざるを得ない | No alternative exists | Situational compulsion |
| ～ずにはすまない | Social/moral obligation | "It won't be settled otherwise" |

～ざるを得ない emphasizes that circumstances leave no choice. ～ずにはすまない emphasizes that social norms or moral duty require the action.

## 6.5 ～てはいられない — "Can't Keep Doing"

～てはいられない expresses that continuing a current state or action is no longer acceptable — the situation demands a change. It combines the て-form with the は particle (contrastive) and いられない (cannot remain). The meaning is "I can't just keep [doing X]" or "I can't afford to remain [in state X]."

This expression is relatively common even in spoken Japanese. It appears when urgency, impatience, or a sense of crisis makes the current state untenable.

### Formation

| Preceding element | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Verb (て form) | 待っていてはいられない |
| Verb (て form) | ぼんやりしてはいられない |
| ～ないで + はいられない | 笑わないではいられない |

Note: ～ないではいられない (with ないで) means "cannot help but do" — a different meaning from ～てはいられない, which means "cannot keep doing." Be careful not to confuse them.

### Examples

> ぼんやり　してはいられない。締め切りが　明日だ。
> "I can't just sit around. The deadline is tomorrow."

> いつまでも　泣いてはいられない。前に　進まなければ。
> "I can't keep crying forever. I have to move forward."

> こんな　状況で　のんびり　してはいられない。
> "I can't take it easy in a situation like this."

> 黙って　見てはいられなかった　ので、声を　かけた。
> "I couldn't just stand by and watch, so I spoke up."

> もう　子どもでは　いられない。自分の　力で　生きて　いかなければ。
> "I can't remain a child any longer. I have to live by my own strength."

> 驚いてばかり　いてはいられない。すぐに　対策を　考えよう。
> "We can't just keep being surprised. Let's think of countermeasures right away."

### ～てはいられない vs ～ないではいられない

These look similar but express opposite ideas:

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ～てはいられない | "Can't keep doing X" | 寝てはいられない "Can't keep sleeping" |
| ～ないではいられない | "Can't help but do X" | 笑わないではいられない "Can't help but laugh" |

---

## 6.6 Summary of Compulsion and Inevitability

| Expression | Meaning | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| ～ずにはおかない | "Cannot fail to cause" | Irresistible effect of a thing/event |
| ～を余儀なくされる | "Be forced to" | External pressure, no alternative |
| ～ざるを得ない | "Have no choice but to" | Situational compulsion |
| ～ないではすまない | "Cannot get away without" | Social/moral obligation |
| ～てはいられない | "Can't keep (doing)" | Urgency, need for change |

## 6.7 Reading Passage

次の文章は、ある企業の危機管理に関する報告書の一部です。

---

先月発生した製品リコール問題について報告する。

当社の主力製品に設計上の欠陥が発見され、全製品の回収を余儀なくされた。発見が遅れたことは認めざるを得ない。この問題は消費者の信頼を損なわずにはおかないものであり、早急な対応が求められた。

まず、公式に謝罪しないではすまない事態であることは明白であった。社長自らが記者会見を行い、経緯を説明した。黙って見てはいられないと判断した品質管理部門の担当者が内部告発を行ったことが、問題の早期発見につながった。

今後、同様の問題を繰り返さないために、品質管理体制の見直しをせざるを得ない。検査基準の強化、製造工程の改善、そして社員教育の徹底——いずれも避けては通れない課題である。

この事態は当社にとって深刻な打撃であるが、いつまでも落ち込んではいられない。信頼回復に向けて、全社一丸となって取り組む所存である。

---

**Translation:**

I report on the product recall issue that occurred last month.

A design defect was discovered in our company's flagship product, and we were forced to recall all products. We must acknowledge that the discovery was delayed. This problem was one that could not fail to damage consumer trust, and a prompt response was required.

First, it was clear that this was a situation where we could not avoid issuing a formal apology. The president himself held a press conference and explained the circumstances. The fact that a quality control staff member, who judged that he could not simply stand by and watch, made an internal report led to the early discovery of the problem.

Going forward, in order not to repeat a similar problem, we have no choice but to review the quality control system. Strengthening inspection standards, improving manufacturing processes, and thorough employee training — all of these are challenges that cannot be avoided.

This situation is a serious blow to our company, but we cannot remain discouraged forever. We intend to work together as an entire company toward the recovery of trust.
