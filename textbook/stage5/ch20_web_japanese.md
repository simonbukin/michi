# Chapter 20 — Web Japanese and Internet Culture

The internet has given birth to a distinct register of Japanese that sits alongside formal writing and casual speech as a language variety in its own right. Understanding web Japanese is essential not only for navigating social media, forums, and online communities, but also because internet-born expressions increasingly migrate into everyday spoken Japanese. This chapter surveys the major conventions of Japanese internet language, from legacy 2ch culture to modern Twitter/X usage, and examines where web Japanese overlaps with casual speech and where it diverges sharply.

---

## 20.1 Abbreviations and Laugh Markers

Japanese internet culture has developed a rich system of abbreviations. Many originated on anonymous message boards in the early 2000s and remain in active use today.

### Laughter markers

The most fundamental internet abbreviation is the laugh marker. Japanese has several layers.

| Marker | Origin | Nuance |
|--------|--------|--------|
| w | 笑い (warai) の頭文字 | standard internet laugh — one w is mild |
| www | multiple w | stronger laughter |
| wwwwww | many w in a row | uncontrollable laughter; resembles grass (草) |
| 草 (くさ) | www looking like grass | "lol" — now extremely common even in speech |
| 草生える | 草 + 生える (to grow) | "that's hilarious" (lit. "grass grows") |
| 大草原 | big grassland | "I'm dying laughing" |
| (笑) | わらい in parentheses | older style; still used in semi-formal online writing |

> ちょっと待ってwww それマジで言ってんの草
>
> *Wait wait wait lol. You're seriously saying that? I'm dying.*

### Common abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Full form | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| 乙 (おつ) | お疲れ（さま） | "good work" / "thanks for the effort" |
| ksk | 加速 (かそく) | "speed up" (bump the thread) |
| kwsk | 詳しく (くわしく) | "tell me more" / "details please" |
| wktk | ワクワクテカテカ | excited anticipation |
| ktkr | キタコレ | "here it comes!" / "yes!" |
| gkbr | ガクガクブルブル | trembling with fear |
| mjk | マジか | "seriously?" |
| おk | オーケー | "OK" |
| り | 了解 (りょうかい) | "got it" (extreme abbreviation) |
| それな | — | "that's so true" / strong agreement |

> A: 明日テストなのに何も勉強してない
> B: 乙www
> C: kwsk どの科目？
>
> *A: I haven't studied at all even though there's a test tomorrow.*
> *B: RIP lol.*
> *C: Details? Which subject?*

Note that り as an abbreviation of 了解 is particularly striking — it reduces a four-mora word to a single mora. This kind of extreme abbreviation is characteristic of younger internet users.

## 20.2 2ch/5ch Conventions

2channel (now 5channel) is the anonymous bulletin board that shaped much of Japanese internet culture from the late 1990s onward. Its conventions permeate Japanese web language even today.

### Core terminology

| Term | Reading | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| スレ | すれ | thread (from "thread") |
| レス | れす | reply/response (from "response") |
| 板 | いた | board (forum section) |
| sage | さげ | posting without bumping the thread (lit. "lower") |
| age | あげ | bumping the thread to the top (lit. "raise") |
| >>1 | いちさん | reference to the original poster |
| 安価 | あんか | referencing another post (from "anchor") |
| コテハン | こてはん | a user with a fixed handle (固定ハンドル) |
| 名無し | ななし | anonymous poster (lit. "nameless") |
| ROMる | ロムる | to lurk without posting (Read Only Member) |
| 過疎 | かそ | a dead/inactive thread |
| 祭り | まつり | when a thread explodes with activity |

### AA (アスキーアート)

ASCII art is a major element of 2ch culture. Characters are built from full-width text characters and are used to express emotions, tell stories, or create elaborate visual compositions.

Famous examples include:

> （　´∀｀）　— smiling, carefree face (often called モナー)
>
> （´・ω・`）　— Shobon, a sad/cute face
>
> ｷﾀ━━━(ﾟ∀ﾟ)━━━!!　— "It's here!!" excitement face

These are not simply decoration — they carry specific emotional nuances that text alone cannot convey in the anonymous context of 2ch.

### Unique grammatical features

2ch Japanese has its own grammatical tendencies.

| Feature | Example | Standard equivalent |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Sentence-final だお | 今日は暑いだお | 今日は暑いよ |
| ～な件 | 全然面白くない件 | 全然面白くないということ |
| ～についてkwsk | 詳しく教えて | |
| ガチで | ガチで泣いた | 本当に泣いた |

> このスレ過疎りすぎだろ。sage進行でいこうぜ。
>
> *This thread is way too dead. Let's keep it sage (don't bump).*

## 20.3 Twitter/X Conventions

Twitter (now X) is the dominant social media platform in Japan and has developed its own set of conventions distinct from 2ch culture.

### Core Twitter vocabulary

| Term | Reading | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| リプ | りぷ | reply (from "reply") |
| リプ欄 | りぷらん | the reply section |
| RT（リツイート） | アールティー | retweet |
| 引用RT | いんようRT | quote retweet |
| いいね | いいね | like (the heart button) |
| フォロバ | フォロバ | follow-back |
| FF外から失礼します | — | "Excuse me for replying even though we don't follow each other" |
| TL | タイムライン | timeline |
| バズる | ばずる | to go viral (from "buzz") |
| 炎上（する） | えんじょう | to be engulfed in online controversy (lit. "catch fire") |
| 鍵垢 | かぎあか | locked/private account |
| 裏垢 | うらあか | secret alternate account |
| 本垢 | ほんあか | main account |
| 浮上 | ふじょう | becoming active on the TL again (lit. "surfacing") |
| 低浮上 | ていふじょう | being less active than usual |

> FF外から失礼します。この件についてですが、元ツイの方が既に訂正されているようです。
>
> *Excuse me for jumping in (we don't follow each other). Regarding this matter, it seems the original tweeter has already posted a correction.*

### Hashtag culture

Japanese Twitter makes heavy use of hashtags, often with long descriptive tags.

> #朝活している人と繋がりたい
> #読書好きな人と繋がりたい
> #今日のコーディネート

These "繋がりたい" (want to connect) tags function as community-building tools.

###炎上 (online firestorm)

炎上 is one of the most important concepts in Japanese internet culture. When a post attracts negative attention, the flood of critical replies and quote-retweets is called 炎上. Related vocabulary:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 炎上商法 | deliberately courting controversy for attention |
| まとめサイト | aggregator sites that compile controversial threads |
| 特定する | to identify someone's real identity (doxxing) |
| 魚拓 | web archive/screenshot (lit. "fish print") — preserving evidence |

> あの芸能人、またSNSで炎上してるらしい。リプ欄がすごいことになってる。
>
> *That celebrity is apparently caught up in another online firestorm. The reply section is a mess.*

## 20.4 Emoji and Kaomoji Culture

Japan has a deep tradition of text-based emotional expression that predates and influences the global emoji standard.

### Kaomoji (顔文字)

Unlike Western emoticons that are read sideways :), Japanese kaomoji are read face-on and use a much wider character set.

| Kaomoji | Emotion |
|---------|---------|
| (´・ω・`) | sad, disappointed (Shobon) |
| (ﾟ∀ﾟ) | excited, manic |
| (；´Д`) | distressed, sweating |
| (´；ω；`) | crying |
| ( ˘ω˘ ) | peaceful, content |
| ┗(＾o＾)┓ | dancing, celebrating |
| orz / OTL | on hands and knees in despair |
| _(:3 」∠)_ | lying down, given up |
| (΄◉◞౪◟◉`) | creepy smile |
| m(_ _)m | bowing in apology |

> 今日の仕事やばかった(；´Д`)　もう寝るorz
>
> *Today's work was brutal. I'm going to bed now (collapses).*

### (笑) and its variants

The (笑) notation predates internet culture and was originally used in print to indicate laughter in transcribed speech. It has evolved online.

| Marker | Register |
|--------|----------|
| (笑) | neutral — print, semi-formal online |
| (笑い) | slightly more formal |
| (爆笑) | explosive laughter |
| (苦笑) | bitter/wry laugh |
| (微笑) | gentle smile |
| (泣) | crying |
| (怒) | angry |

### Emoji (絵文字)

Japan invented emoji — the word itself is Japanese (絵 "picture" + 文字 "character"). Originally developed for Japanese mobile carriers in the late 1990s, emoji were standardized into Unicode and spread globally.

Japanese emoji usage conventions differ from Western ones. For instance, the 🙏 emoji is commonly used to mean "please" or "thank you" (合掌, pressing hands together) rather than "prayer."

## 20.5 ネットスラング in Mainstream Language

Many words that originated as internet slang have crossed over into mainstream spoken Japanese, particularly among younger speakers. This section examines the most significant cases.

### ヤバい — semantic broadening

ヤバい originally meant "dangerous" or "risky" (from 矢場, a target-shooting gallery associated with the underworld). On the internet and among young people, it underwent extreme semantic broadening.

| Usage | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Traditional | dangerous, risky | この橋、ヤバくない？ |
| Negative | terrible, awful | 今回のテスト、ヤバかった |
| Positive | amazing, incredible | このラーメン、ヤバい（おいしい） |
| Intensifier | extremely | ヤバいくらい眠い |

> A: 昨日のライブどうだった？
> B: ヤバかった。マジでヤバかった。
> A: いい意味で？悪い意味で？
> B: いい意味で！
>
> *A: How was yesterday's concert?*
> *B: It was insane. Seriously insane.*
> *A: In a good way? Or bad way?*
> *B: Good way!*

### Other internet-to-mainstream crossovers

| Word | Origin/Original meaning | Current mainstream meaning |
|------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| エモい | "emotional" (English) | emotionally moving, nostalgic, aesthetically touching |
| ガチ | ガチンコ (sumo: a real match) | serious, genuine, for real |
| 推し (おし) | 推す (to push/support) | one's favorite idol, character, or person — "my bias" |
| 沼 (ぬま) | swamp | an interest so deep you cannot escape — "rabbit hole" |
| 尊い (とうとい) | noble, precious (classical) | so cute/good it's transcendent (fandom) |
| 無理 (むり) | impossible | "I can't (handle how good/cute this is)" |
| ワンチャン | "one chance" | "there's a slim possibility" |
| 陽キャ / 陰キャ | 陽気なキャラ / 陰気なキャラ | extrovert / introvert (personality types) |
| マウントを取る | from "mount" (martial arts) | to assert dominance, to one-up someone |
| ディスる | from "disrespect" | to disrespect, to bad-mouth |

> 最近、推しが尊すぎて無理。完全に沼にハマった。
>
> *Lately my favorite is so precious I can't handle it. I've completely fallen down the rabbit hole.*

This sentence would be incomprehensible to someone unfamiliar with internet-influenced Japanese, yet it is perfectly natural among younger speakers.

## 20.6 Web Japanese vs. Spoken Casual — Overlap and Divergence

Web Japanese and spoken casual Japanese share some features but diverge in important ways.

### Shared features

| Feature | Web example | Spoken example |
|---------|------------|----------------|
| Sentence-final particle省略 | 行く | 行く |
| ら抜き言葉 | 食べれる | 食べれる |
| Abbreviation of ている → てる | 何してる？ | 何してる？ |
| Casual question without か | これ何？ | これ何？ |

### Web-only features (not used in speech)

| Feature | Example | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| w / 草 | 草生える | You don't say "kusa" aloud to mean "lol" (though some younger speakers do) |
| Hashtags | #わかりみが深い | Text-only convention |
| AA/kaomoji | (´・ω・`) | Cannot be spoken |
| Deliberate misspelling | すこ (好き), ぬるぽ | Text-based play |
| Punctuation avoidance | 今日暑いね明日も暑いらしい | Run-on style common in casual posts |

### Speech-only features (not common in web writing)

| Feature | Example | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Filler words | えーと、あのー | Rarely written online |
| Intonation-based questions | 行く↑？ | Conveyed differently in text |
| Dialectal particles | ～じゃん、～やん | Sometimes written but less consistently |
| Trailing off | あのさー… | Written with … but loses vocal nuance |

### Register mixing

One of the most distinctive features of web Japanese is register mixing — combining formal and informal elements for comedic or emphatic effect.

> 本日のランチ、大変美味しゅうございましたwww
>
> *Today's lunch was most exceedingly delicious lololol*

The humor comes from the clash between the ultra-polite 美味しゅうございました and the casual www.

> 弊社のプロジェクト、完全に詰んでて草
>
> *Our company's project is completely stuck and I'm dying laughing (草)*

Here, 弊社 (humble term for "our company") clashes with the slang 詰んでて草.

### Reading 2 — Forum discussion

次の文章は、掲示板の書き込みを想定したものである。

5 名前：名無しさん 投稿日：20XX/03/15 14:50
>>4
それな。コンビニバイトしてた留学生の敬語が完璧すぎて草生えた記憶ある。

6 名前：名無しさん 投稿日：20XX/03/15 15:01
まあ敬語ミスっても大抵の日本人は気にしないよ。
丁寧に話そうとしてるのが伝われば十分。ガチで怒る人は少数派。

**Translation:**

**[Question] Isn't keigo basically an impossible game for foreigners?**

1: I have a foreign friend studying Japanese, and they're completely stuck on keigo. Even Japanese people are shaky on the difference between sonkeigo and kenjougo — isn't it too harsh to expect it from foreigners?

2: >>1 I get that. Honestly, even I, a Japanese person, don't have confidence I'm using keigo correctly. I was casually using "ossharareru" and everything.

3: >>2 That's double keigo. Just "ossharu" is fine.

4: Keigo is all about practice. It's better to learn by actually using it at a part-time job than from a textbook. There are foreigners who use it properly.

5: >>4 Exactly. I remember a foreign exchange student working at a convenience store whose keigo was so perfect it was hilarious.

6: Well, even if you make keigo mistakes, most Japanese people won't mind. As long as your effort to be polite comes through, that's enough. People who genuinely get angry are a minority.

7: >>6 This. In the end it's a communication issue, so it doesn't have to be perfect. Stop lurking and tell >>1's friend too.
