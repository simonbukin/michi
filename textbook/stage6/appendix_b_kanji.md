# Appendix B — Stage 6 Kanji Index

This appendix lists kanji introduced in Stage 6 to complete the 常用漢字 (Jouyou Kanji) set of 2,136 characters. By the end of Stage 5, the learner has acquired approximately 1,500 kanji spanning N5 through N2 levels. The remaining characters catalogued here — roughly 500+ in total — represent the final push to full 常用漢字 coverage. They tend to appear in formal writing, specialized domains, legal and governmental texts, literary prose, and academic discourse.

Entries are organized into semantic clusters for efficient study. Each entry provides the character, its standard ON-yomi (カタカナ) and kun-yomi (ひらがな) readings, stroke count, and two to three key compounds. Where a character has no common kun-yomi, only the ON reading is listed.

This appendix presents approximately 200 representative entries. A supplementary note on 表外漢字 (hyougai kanji) follows at the end.

## Note on 表外漢字 (Hyougai Kanji)

The 常用漢字 list, revised most recently in 2010, contains 2,136 characters and serves as the official benchmark for general literacy. However, literate adults — especially those who read literature, legal documents, classical texts, or specialized publications — will inevitably encounter characters outside this list. These are known as **表外漢字** (literally, "characters outside the table").

Common categories of 表外漢字 include:

- **人名用漢字** (Jinmeiyou Kanji): An additional set of approximately 860 characters approved for use in Japanese personal names. Examples include 翔（ショウ・かける）, 凜（リン）, 煌（コウ・きらめく）, and 琥（コ）.

- **Literary and classical characters**: Characters that appear frequently in pre-modern texts, poetry, and formal prose but are not part of the 常用 set. Examples: 鑓（やり）, 薔薇（ばら）, 彷徨（ほうこう）, 蠱惑（こわく）.

- **Specialized and technical fields**: Medical, legal, scientific, and Buddhist terminology often employs characters beyond the 常用 list. Examples: 腱（けん, tendon）, 疱（ほう, blister）, 膠（こう/にかわ, glue）, 祀（し/まつる, enshrine）.

- **Place names**: Many Japanese place names use non-常用 characters, such as 檜原（ひのはら）, 祇園（ぎおん）, 鑁阿寺（ばんなじ）, and 嬬恋（つまごい）.

When encountering 表外漢字, readers may rely on furigana, context, or dictionary lookup. Advanced learners aiming for true fluency are encouraged to build passive recognition of at least 200-300 表外漢字 beyond the 常用 set, focusing first on those most commonly encountered in their areas of interest.
