# Appendix H — Recommended Resources

Resources organized by format and difficulty for continued study beyond the textbook. Difficulty is rated on a five-star scale, where ★☆☆☆☆ is accessible to upper-intermediate learners and ★★★★★ represents material that challenges even advanced native readers.

---

### Approachable → Challenging

|---|---|---|---|

| 吉本ばなな | キッチン | Short, emotionally direct sentences; limited kanji burden | ★★★☆☆ |
| 東野圭吾 | 容疑者Xの献身 | Page-turning mystery; conversational dialogue | ★★★☆☆ |
| 宮部みゆき | 火車 | Social realism; natural dialogue mixed with exposition | ★★★☆☆ |
| 伊坂幸太郎 | 重力ピエロ | Playful narrative structure; modern vocabulary | ★★★☆☆ |
| 小川洋子 | 博士の愛した数式 | Gentle pacing; precise but not dense prose | ★★★☆☆ |
| 恩田陸 | 蜜蜂と遠雷 | Rich descriptions of music; varied register across characters | ★★★★☆ |
| 川上弘美 | センセイの鞄 | Understated literary style; subtle emotional vocabulary | ★★★★☆ |
| 中村文則 | 掏摸（スリ） | Dark, philosophical; compressed sentences with layered meaning | ★★★★☆ |
| 三島由紀夫 | 金閣寺 | Classical-leaning literary Japanese; dense imagery | ★★★★★ |

| Source | Notes | Difficulty |

| 朝日新聞デジタル (asahi.com) | Center-left; strong editorials (天声人語 is a classic study text) | ★★★★☆ |
| 毎日新聞 (mainichi.jp) | Balanced reporting; slightly more accessible headlines | ★★★☆☆ |

| 日本経済新聞 (nikkei.com) | Business/economics focus; essential for professional Japanese | ★★★★★ |

**Study method:** Read 天声人語 (朝日) daily. It is roughly 600 characters, uses formal written style, and covers current events. Many Japanese learners and even native students use it as a writing model.

## 4. Reading — Manga

Manga provides exposure to casual speech, dialect, slang, onomatopoeia, and gendered language that formal study materials rarely cover.

| Title | Why | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| よつばと！ | Everyday situations; furigana throughout; natural casual speech | ★★☆☆☆ |
| ちはやふる | Competitive karuta; mix of formal and passionate speech | ★★★☆☆ |
| 鬼滅の刃 | Archaic/samurai speech patterns mixed with modern slang | ★★★☆☆ |
| SLAM DUNK | Sporty male casual speech; Kansai dialect (三井、流川) | ★★★☆☆ |
| のだめカンタービレ | Music world vocabulary; quirky female speech patterns | ★★★☆☆ |
| 銀魂 | Dense slang, parody, wordplay; rapid-fire dialogue | ★★★★☆ |
| ゴルゴ13 | Political/military vocabulary; terse, formal male speech | ★★★★☆ |
| 蟲師 | Literary narration; archaic rural dialect; poetic register | ★★★★☆ |
| ヴィンランド・サガ | Historical setting; varied speech registers across characters | ★★★★☆ |

**Note on dialect:** For Kansai-ben exposure, try SLAM DUNK or じゃりン子チエ. For Tohoku/rural speech, 蟲師 is excellent. For yakuza/rough male speech, 闇金ウシジマくん provides authentic (if uncomfortable) examples.

### Specialized Reference

| Resource | Use |
|---|---|
| 日本国語大辞典 (小学館) | Historical dictionary; traces word origins across centuries |
| 類語例解辞典 (小学館) | Synonym dictionary; critical for nuance at advanced level |

**Transition advice:** If you are still reaching for a bilingual dictionary as your first instinct, force yourself to check a monolingual dictionary first. Look up the same word in 新明解 and 大辞林 — the different definition styles will deepen your understanding.

## 10. JLPT N1 Preparation

| Resource | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 新完全マスター N1 シリーズ | Textbook set | Grammar, reading, listening, vocabulary, kanji — comprehensive |
| 日本語能力試験 N1 公式問題集 | Past exams | Official practice tests from the JEES; start here |
| TRY! 日本語能力試験 N1 | Grammar + reading | Clear grammar explanations with context |
| 日本語総まとめ N1 シリーズ | Review book | Six-week structured review; good for final preparation |
| 日本語能力試験 N1 読解 スピードマスター | Reading drill | Timed reading practice; builds speed |
| Kanzen Master N1 聴解 | Listening | Realistic listening scenarios; above-test difficulty |

**Strategy notes:**

- N1 grammar: Focus on distinguishing near-synonyms (ものの vs ながらも vs つつも). The test rewards precision.
- N1 reading: Speed matters. Practice reading 1000-character passages in under 4 minutes.
- N1 listening: The test uses natural speed with minimal repetition. Daily podcast listening is the best long-term preparation.
- Take at least two full practice tests under timed conditions before the exam.

## Final Note

The best resource is the one you actually use. At N1 level and beyond, progress comes from volume — reading widely, listening daily, and engaging with material you genuinely find interesting. Textbooks brought you here; authentic material carries you forward.

言葉は使えば使うほど、自分のものになる。
