# Chapter 4 — Near-Synonym Grammar: Scope, Extent, Addition

Japanese has a remarkable number of ways to say "about," "only," "even," and "not only." English speakers tend to treat these as simple vocabulary choices, but in Japanese each variant encodes specific information about the speaker's attitude, the formality of the context, and the logical relationship between elements. Choosing the wrong "only" or the wrong "even" can sound unnatural or shift the meaning in ways the speaker did not intend.

This chapter examines four clusters of near-synonyms dealing with topic scope, limitation, extreme examples, and escalation. As with the rest of Stage 6, no new grammar is introduced. The goal is to develop the precision that separates competent use from masterful use.

---

## 4.1 について vs に関して vs をめぐって

All three mark the topic of discussion, but they differ in register and in what kind of discussion they imply.

### Comparison Table

| Expression | Register | Implication | Typical context |
|---|---|---|---|
| について | Neutral | General topic — "about, concerning" | Conversation, essays, presentations |
| に関して | Formal | Formal topic — "regarding, with respect to" | Official documents, academic papers |
| をめぐって | Formal | Contested topic — "surrounding, over" | Debates, controversies, disputes |

### について — The default "about"

について is the most versatile and most common. It works in almost any register and carries no special implication about the nature of the discussion.

> 日本の　教育制度について　話し合った。
> "We discussed Japan's education system."

> この件について、何か　ご質問は　ありますか。
> "Do you have any questions about this matter?"

### に関して — Formal "regarding"

に関して elevates the register. It signals that the discussion is official, academic, or structured. Using it in casual conversation sounds stiff.

> 個人情報の　取り扱いに関して、以下の　規定が　適用されます。
> "Regarding the handling of personal information, the following regulations apply."

> 今回の　事故に関して、調査委員会が　設置された。
> "Regarding this accident, an investigation committee was established."

### をめぐって — "Surrounding" (contested, debated)

をめぐって implies that the topic is controversial, contested, or the subject of competing views. It is never used for neutral informational topics.

> 移民政策を　めぐって、激しい　議論が　交わされた。
> "A heated debate took place surrounding immigration policy."

> 遺産の　分配を　めぐって、兄弟が　争っている。
> "The siblings are fighting over the distribution of the inheritance."

> 新空港の　建設を　めぐって、賛否が　分かれている。
> "Opinions are divided over the construction of a new airport."

### Diagnostic

"We discussed the new policy."

| Version | Impression |
|---|---|
| 新しい政策について話し合った。 | Neutral discussion. |
| 新しい政策に関して話し合った。 | Formal discussion (meeting, hearing). |
| 新しい政策をめぐって話し合った。 | The policy is controversial; people disagree. |

## 4.2 だけ vs ばかり vs のみ vs しか～ない vs きり

These all express "only" or "nothing but," but the nuance and register vary considerably.

### Comparison Table

| Expression | Register | Nuance | Emphasis |
|---|---|---|---|
| だけ | Neutral | Simple limitation — "only this much" | Quantity or scope |
| ばかり | Neutral to casual | Excess or repetition — "nothing but" | Complaint or pattern |
| のみ | Formal, written | Strict limitation — "only, solely" | Formal restriction |
| しか～ない | Neutral | Insufficient — "only (and it's not enough)" | Speaker's dissatisfaction |
| きり | Casual | Last occurrence or sole remainder | Finality |

### だけ — Neutral "only"

だけ states a limit without judgment. It is the most neutral of the five.

> 水だけ　飲んだ。
> "I drank only water."

> あと　3日だけ　待ってください。
> "Please wait just three more days."

> 見ただけで　分かる。
> "I can tell just by looking."

### ばかり — "Nothing but" (excess, complaint)

ばかり implies repetition or excess. The speaker often (but not always) views this negatively.

> 最近、甘いものばかり　食べている。
> "Lately I've been eating nothing but sweets."

> 文句ばかり　言わないで。
> "Stop doing nothing but complaining."

> 彼は　遊んでばかりいる。
> "He does nothing but play around."

### のみ — Formal "only"

のみ is the formal, written equivalent of だけ. It appears in rules, regulations, academic texts, and official notices.

> 会員のみ　入場できます。
> "Only members may enter."

> 本品は　外用のみと　してください。
> "This product is for external use only."

> 事実のみを　述べる。
> "I will state only the facts."

### しか～ない — "Only (and it's not enough)"

しか～ない always takes a negative verb and conveys that the quantity or scope is insufficient. The speaker wishes there were more.

> 3人しか　来なかった。
> "Only three people came." (I expected more.)

> あと　1000円しか　ない。
> "I only have 1,000 yen left." (Not enough.)

> 日本語は　少ししか　話せない。
> "I can only speak a little Japanese." (I wish I could speak more.)

### きり — "Only, since" (finality)

きり (also っきり) expresses that something happened once and nothing followed, or that only a small amount remains. It has a sense of finality.

> 彼とは　あの日　会ったきり、連絡が　ない。
> "Since meeting him that day, I've had no contact." (That was the last time.)

> 二人きりで　話したい。
> "I want to talk with just the two of us." (Nobody else.)

> あれっきり　来なくなった。
> "Since that time, he stopped coming."

### Substitution test

"Only three people came."

| Version | Nuance |
|---|---|
| 3人だけ来た。 | Neutral fact — three, not more. |
| 3人しか来なかった。 | Disappointment — only three, not enough. |
| 3人のみ参加した。 | Formal, factual — precisely three participated. |
| 3人きりだった。 | Only three were there — sense of isolation or smallness. |

## 4.3 さえ vs すら vs まで vs でも

These expressions all mean "even," but they differ in register, emotional weight, and what kind of extreme they highlight.

### Comparison Table

| Expression | Register | Nuance | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| さえ | Neutral to formal | Extreme minimum or extreme example | "If even X, then certainly everything else" |
| すら | Formal, literary | Same as さえ, but more emphatic and literary | Stronger surprise or criticism |
| まで | Neutral | Extension to an unexpected extreme | "It went so far as to include X" |
| でも | Casual | Casual "even" or "or something" | Lightest, most conversational |

### さえ — "Even" (extreme minimum)

さえ presents X as the minimum or most basic case. If even X is true, then everything beyond X is certainly true as well.

> 名前さえ　覚えていない。
> "I don't even remember the name." (Let alone details.)

> 水さえ　あれば、生きられる。
> "If I even have water, I can survive." (The bare minimum.)

> 子どもでさえ　分かることだ。
> "It's something even a child would understand."

### すら — "Even" (literary, emphatic)

すら has the same logical function as さえ but belongs to formal or literary register. It adds a tone of gravity or surprise.

> 彼は　挨拶すら　しなかった。
> "He did not even greet us." (Stronger criticism than さえ.)

> 自分の　名前すら　書けなくなった。
> "He could no longer even write his own name." (Grave, sympathetic.)

> 専門家ですら　答えられない　問題だ。
> "It's a problem that even experts cannot answer."

### まで — "Even, so far as to"

まで (in its "even" sense, not its spatial/temporal sense) expresses that something has extended to an unexpected or excessive degree. It often implies surprise.

> 親友まで　裏切った。
> "He even betrayed his best friend." (It went that far.)

> そんなことまで　知っているの？
> "You even know something like that?"

> 彼女は　泣くまで　怒った。
> "She got so angry she even cried." (Note: this is the extent use, not pure "even.")

### でも — Casual "even" or "or something"

でも is the lightest and most casual. It can mean "even" or it can soften a suggestion ("something like").

> 子どもでも　できるよ。
> "Even a child can do it."

> コーヒーでも　飲まない？
> "Want to have some coffee or something?"

> 辞書でも　引いてみたら？
> "Why don't you try looking it up in a dictionary or something?"

### Choosing the right "even"

| Context | Best choice |
|---|---|
| Extreme minimum in neutral speech | さえ |
| Extreme case in formal/literary writing | すら |
| Surprising extension of a situation | まで |
| Casual speech, softening | でも |

## 4.4 はおろか vs どころか vs ばかりか vs のみならず

These four all express "not only X but also Y" with an escalating emphasis, but they differ in register and what kind of escalation they perform.

### Comparison Table

| Expression | Register | Direction | Nuance |
|---|---|---|---|
| はおろか | Formal | Dismisses X, escalates to Y | "Let alone X, even Y" — X is obvious |
| どころか | Neutral | Contradicts expectation, escalates | "Far from X, actually Y" — reversal |
| ばかりか | Neutral to formal | Adds Y on top of X | "Not only X but also Y" — accumulation |
| のみならず | Formal, written | Same as ばかりか | "Not only X but also Y" — formal |

### はおろか — "Let alone"

はおろか dismisses X as obvious and escalates to the more surprising Y. X is treated as too basic to even mention.

> 漢字は　おろか、ひらがなさえ　読めない。
> "Let alone kanji, he can't even read hiragana."

> 旅行は　おろか、外食する　余裕も　ない。
> "Let alone traveling, I don't even have the budget to eat out."

> 英語は　おろか、母語の　文法も　怪しい。
> "Let alone English, even his native language grammar is questionable."

### どころか — "Far from"

どころか contradicts an expectation and presents a reality that is much more extreme — either much better or much worse than expected.

> 簡単どころか、ものすごく　難しかった。
> "Far from being easy, it was extremely difficult."

> 感謝どころか、文句を　言われた。
> "Far from being thanked, I was complained to."

> 痩せるどころか、3キロも　太った。
> "Far from losing weight, I gained three kilos."

### ばかりか — "Not only... but also"

ばかりか adds an additional, often surprising element on top of what was already stated.

> 彼は　日本語ばかりか、中国語も　韓国語も　話せる。
> "He can speak not only Japanese but also Chinese and Korean."

> 成績が　下がったばかりか、友人関係も　悪くなった。
> "Not only did my grades drop, but my friendships also deteriorated."

> 雨ばかりか、雷まで　鳴り出した。
> "Not only rain, but thunder even started."

### のみならず — Formal "not only"

のみならず is the formal, written equivalent of ばかりか. It appears in essays, news, and academic writing.

> この問題は　日本のみならず、世界各国で　議論されている。
> "This issue is being discussed not only in Japan but in countries around the world."

> 経済的な　影響のみならず、社会的な　影響も　大きい。
> "Not only the economic impact but the social impact is also significant."

> 学生のみならず、教員も　参加が　求められている。
> "Not only students but also faculty are expected to participate."

### Choosing the right escalation

| Situation | Best choice |
|---|---|
| Dismissing X as obvious, escalating to more extreme Y | はおろか |
| Contradicting an expectation entirely | どころか |
| Adding a surprising additional element (neutral to formal) | ばかりか |
| Same as above in formal writing | のみならず |

## 4.6 Answer Key

### Exercise A

1. **a) でも** — Casual context ("even a child can understand this simple thing"). さえ and すら would work grammatically but are too formal/emphatic for this straightforward observation.

2. **c) きり** — Emphasizes finality: "I met him at graduation and that was the last time." だけ would be neutral; しか would need a negative verb.

3. **b) に関して** — Formal governmental investigation. について is possible but less formal. をめぐって would imply the safety is controversial/contested, which is not the focus here — it is being investigated, not debated.

4. **b) ばかり** — Expresses excess/complaint: "does nothing but play games." だけ would be neutral limitation; のみ is too formal for casual speech about weekends.

5. **b) ばかりか** — Adds a second negative element on top of the first. はおろか would dismiss the food as obvious and escalate to service; どころか would contradict an expectation (which was not set up here).

6. **a) さえ** — Presents the phone number as the minimum piece of information, implying "let alone anything more personal." すら would work but is more literary. まで would imply surprising extension rather than minimum.

### Exercise B

1. に関して → **について**: ねえ、この映画について知ってる？(に関して is too formal for casual chat.)

2. でも → **のみならず** or **ばかりか**: お客様のみならず、スタッフも楽しめるイベントです。(でも is too casual for a company newsletter, and のみならず was already started in the first part.)

3. ばかり is correct for "nothing but," but the sentence structure needs adjustment. The complaint about excess is natural with ばかり. Actually, this sentence is correct as written — ばかり meaning "nothing but" fits the complaint context. The error is elsewhere: if the intent is "only hiragana" (limitation) rather than "nothing but hiragana" (excess), then だけ or しか would be better. In this context of complaining, ばかり is natural.

### Exercise C

1. 漢字は**おろか**、カタカナ**さえ**（**すら**）読めない。
   "Let alone kanji, he can't even read katakana."

2. 成績が上がる**どころか**、むしろ下がってしまった。
   "Far from improving, my grades actually dropped."

3. この技術は医療**のみならず**、農業や工業にも応用されている。
   "This technology is applied not only in medicine but also in agriculture and industry."
