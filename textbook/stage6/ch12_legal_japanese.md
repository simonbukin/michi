# Chapter 12 — Legal Japanese

Legal Japanese — 法律日本語 — is not simply formal Japanese. It is a separate written dialect with its own sentence architecture, its own vocabulary, and its own logic. Statutes, constitutions, contracts, and regulations all follow conventions that even native speakers find difficult without training. Sentences run to extraordinary lengths. Nominalization replaces finite verbs almost everywhere. Conditional and permissive constructions are stacked in ways that feel alien to ordinary prose.

This chapter does not try to make you a lawyer. It gives you the tools to read legal documents — to understand what a constitutional article actually says, to parse the clauses of a contract, and to recognize the formulaic expressions that carry precise legal meaning. If you can read legal Japanese, you can read anything.

---

## 12.1 Legal Sentence Structure

Legal Japanese is built on a principle of extreme nominalization. Where ordinary Japanese uses verbs to end clauses and sentences, legal Japanese converts those verbs into noun phrases, chains them together with particles, and delays the predicate until the very end. A single sentence in a statute may contain five or six nominalized clauses before reaching its main verb.

### Core structural patterns

The following patterns appear in virtually every legal document in Japan. They are not optional stylistic choices — they are the grammar of law.

### ～ものとする

This is the most characteristic expression in Japanese statutory language. It converts a statement into an obligation or a stipulation. It does not mean "shall" in the sense of a command directed at a person — it means "it shall be the case that," establishing a legal fact or rule.

> 委員の任期は、二年とする。
> "The term of office for committee members shall be two years."

> 会議は、委員の過半数の出席がなければ開くことができないものとする。
> "Meetings shall not be opened unless a majority of committee members are present."

The difference between する and ものとする is the difference between a simple statement and a legal stipulation. する states a fact; ものとする establishes that fact as a binding rule within the legal framework.

### Formation

| Base | + ものとする | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Verb dictionary form | 届け出るものとする | "shall submit notification" |
| Verb ない form | 公開しないものとする | "shall not be made public" |
| Noun + とする | 二年とする | "shall be two years" |
| Adjective + ものとする | 有効なものとする | "shall be valid" |

### ～に限る

This pattern restricts the scope of a provision. It means "limited to" or "only." In legal contexts, it draws a hard boundary around what is permitted, required, or applicable.

> この規定は、正社員に限る。
> "This provision applies only to full-time employees."

> 申請は、書面による場合に限る。
> "Applications shall be limited to those made in writing."

> 延長は、一回に限るものとする。
> "Extensions shall be limited to one time."

Note how ～に限る frequently combines with ～ものとする. This stacking of legal expressions is typical: each pattern adds a layer of legal precision.

### ～を妨げない

This expression is a permissive clause. It means "does not preclude" or "does not prevent." It is used when a general rule has been stated, and the drafter wants to clarify that the rule does not prohibit some specific action or right.

> 前項の規定は、当事者が別段の合意をすることを妨げない。
> "The provision of the preceding paragraph does not preclude the parties from reaching a separate agreement."

> 本条の規定は、他の法令に基づく権利の行使を妨げない。
> "The provisions of this article do not preclude the exercise of rights under other laws and regulations."

The structure is always: [general rule reference] は [specific action] を妨げない. The specific action is nominalized (することを, 行使を) — another instance of the extreme nominalization that defines legal Japanese.

### ～の定めるところにより

This phrase means "in accordance with what [X] prescribes" or "as provided for by [X]." It is used to cross-reference other laws, regulations, articles, or bylaws.

> 法律の定めるところにより、裁判所に訴えを提起することができる。
> "One may file a suit in court as provided for by law."

> 政令の定めるところにより、届出をしなければならない。
> "One must submit notification as provided for by government ordinance."

### Formation

| Cross-reference source | Full expression |
|---|---|
| 法律 (law) | 法律の定めるところにより |
| 政令 (government ordinance) | 政令の定めるところにより |
| 条例 (local ordinance) | 条例の定めるところにより |
| 別に定める規則 (separately prescribed rules) | 別に定める規則の定めるところにより |

### ～に基づき / ～に基づいて

This means "based on" or "pursuant to" and is used to cite the legal basis for an action or provision.

> 憲法に基づき、この法律を制定する。
> "This law is enacted pursuant to the Constitution."

> 前条の規定に基づき、以下のとおり定める。
> "The following is prescribed based on the provisions of the preceding article."

### Sentence length and structure

Legal sentences in Japanese are long because they front-load all conditions, limitations, and cross-references before reaching the main verb. Consider this pattern:

> [Subject]は、[condition 1]の場合において、[condition 2]に該当するときは、[cross-reference]の定めるところにより、[action]ものとする。

A real example:

> 事業者は、その事業活動に伴い消費者の生命、身体又は財産に対し重大な危害を及ぼすおそれがあることを知ったときは、直ちに、当該危害の発生又は拡大の防止のために必要な措置を講じなければならない。
> "When a business operator becomes aware that there is a risk of serious harm to the life, body, or property of consumers in connection with its business activities, it must immediately take measures necessary to prevent the occurrence or spread of such harm."

This single sentence contains:
- A subject marked by は (事業者は)
- A nominalized condition (…おそれがあることを知ったときは)
- A purpose clause (…防止のために)
- A nominalized object (必要な措置を)
- A legal obligation verb (講じなければならない)

Understanding this architecture — conditions first, obligation last — is the key to reading any Japanese statute.

## 12.2 Constitutional Language

The Constitution of Japan (日本国憲法) was drafted in 1946 and adopted in 1947. Its language reflects the formal legal style of the early postwar period, with some influence from the English-language draft prepared during the Allied Occupation. Constitutional Japanese uses a set of fixed expressions that recur throughout the document.

### ～権利を有する

This means "to possess the right to" or simply "to have the right to." It is the standard constitutional formula for declaring a right.

> すべて国民は、法の下に平等であって、人種、信条、性別、社会的身分又は門地により、政治的、経済的又は社会的関係において、差別されない。
> "All citizens are equal under the law and shall not be discriminated against in political, economic, or social relations because of race, creed, sex, social status, or family origin."

> 何人も、裁判所において裁判を受ける権利を奪はれない。
> "No person shall be deprived of the right to receive a trial in court."

> 国民は、すべての基本的人権の享有を妨げられない。
> "The people shall not be prevented from enjoying all fundamental human rights."

### ～を保障する

This means "to guarantee" and is used when the Constitution declares that a right or freedom is protected.

> 集会、結社及び言論、出版その他一切の表現の自由は、これを保障する。
> "Freedom of assembly, association, speech, press, and all other forms of expression is guaranteed."

> 学問の自由は、これを保障する。
> "Academic freedom is guaranteed."

Note the structure: [right or freedom]は、これを保障する. The pronoun これ refers back to the subject, creating a formal emphasis. This is a constitutional formula — it does not appear in ordinary writing.

### ～を侵してはならない

This means "shall not be infringed" or "shall not be violated." It establishes an absolute prohibition.

> 思想及び良心の自由は、これを侵してはならない。
> "Freedom of thought and conscience shall not be violated."

> 通信の秘密は、これを侵してはならない。
> "The secrecy of communications shall not be violated."

### ～を負ふ / ～の義務を負ふ

This establishes a duty or obligation. The historical kana spelling ふ (modern う) is preserved in the original constitutional text.

> すべて国民は、勤労の権利を有し、義務を負ふ。
> "All citizens shall have the right and obligation to work."

> 国民は、法律の定めるところにより、納税の義務を負ふ。
> "Citizens shall have the obligation to pay taxes as provided for by law."

### Additional constitutional expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Example context |
|---|---|---|
| ～に反する | contrary to, in violation of | この憲法に反する法律は無効である |
| ～を受ける権利 | the right to receive | 裁判を受ける権利 |
| ～してはならない | must not, shall not | 検閲をしてはならない |
| ～の自由を有する | to possess the freedom of | 居住・移転の自由を有する |
| ～に基づく | based on, pursuant to | 国民の厳粛な信託に基づく |
| 公共の福祉 | the public welfare | 公共の福祉に反しない限り |

### The structure of a constitutional article

A typical constitutional article follows this pattern:

1. **Subject** — 国民は / 何人も / すべて国民は
2. **Scope or condition** — (optional) ～の場合において / ～に反しない限り
3. **Right, freedom, or obligation** — ～の権利を有する / ～の自由を有する / ～の義務を負ふ
4. **Guarantee or prohibition** — ～を保障する / ～を侵してはならない

Understanding this template lets you parse even the most complex constitutional provisions.

## 12.3 Contract Language Expanded

Japanese contracts (契約書) use their own set of conventions. The parties are referred to as 甲 (こう, "Party A") and 乙 (おつ, "Party B"), sometimes with 丙 (へい, "Party C") for three-party agreements. The language is dense, formal, and highly formulaic.

### 甲 and 乙

> 甲及び乙は、以下のとおり業務委託契約を締結する。
> "Party A and Party B hereby enter into the following outsourcing agreement."

> 甲は乙に対し、本業務の対価として、月額金五十万円を支払うものとする。
> "Party A shall pay Party B a monthly sum of five hundred thousand yen as compensation for the services."

Note that amounts in contracts are often written in formal kanji numerals (金五十万円) rather than Arabic numerals, to prevent alteration.

### ～に違反した場合

This phrase establishes the consequences of a breach. It means "in the event of a violation of" and is the standard way to introduce penalty or termination clauses.

> 乙が本契約に違反した場合、甲は催告なしに本契約を解除することができる。
> "In the event that Party B violates this agreement, Party A may terminate this agreement without prior notice."

> 甲又は乙が本契約の条項に違反した場合、相手方に対し、損害賠償の責を負うものとする。
> "In the event that Party A or Party B violates the provisions of this agreement, such party shall bear liability for damages to the other party."

### ～の責に帰すべき事由

This is one of the most characteristic — and most difficult — legal phrases in Japanese contract law. It means "causes attributable to the responsibility of [X]" or more naturally, "reasons for which [X] is responsible."

> 甲の責に帰すべき事由により本契約が解除された場合、甲は乙に対し違約金を支払うものとする。
> "In the event that this agreement is terminated due to causes attributable to Party A, Party A shall pay a penalty to Party B."

> 天災その他乙の責に帰すことのできない事由により履行が不可能となったときは、乙はその責を免れるものとする。
> "When performance becomes impossible due to natural disasters or other causes not attributable to Party B, Party B shall be relieved of liability."

### Formation for ～の責に帰すべき事由

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 甲の責に帰すべき事由 | causes attributable to Party A |
| 乙の責に帰すことのできない事由 | causes not attributable to Party B |
| 双方の責に帰すべき事由 | causes attributable to both parties |
| いずれの当事者の責にも帰すことのできない事由 | causes not attributable to either party |

### Additional contract expressions

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 本契約の有効期間 | the effective period of this agreement |
| 契約期間満了日 | the date of expiration of the contract term |
| 書面による承諾 | written consent |
| 事前の書面による同意 | prior written consent |
| 善良なる管理者の注意義務 | duty of care of a good manager (fiduciary duty) |
| 秘密保持義務 | confidentiality obligation |
| 反社会的勢力 | anti-social forces (organized crime) |
| 損害賠償 | compensation for damages |
| 遅延損害金 | late damages / penalty interest |
| 管轄裁判所 | court of jurisdiction |
| 合意管轄 | agreed jurisdiction |
| 協議の上解決する | resolve through consultation |
| 信義誠実の原則 | principle of good faith |

### Contract structure

A standard Japanese contract follows this order:

1. **表題** (title) — 業務委託契約書, 売買契約書, etc.
2. **前文** (preamble) — identifies 甲 and 乙, states the purpose
3. **目的条項** (purpose clause) — Article 1
4. **業務内容** (scope of services/goods) — Article 2
5. **対価・支払条件** (compensation and payment terms) — Article 3
6. **契約期間** (contract term) — Article 4
7. **秘密保持** (confidentiality) — Article 5 or later
8. **解除** (termination) — middle articles
9. **損害賠償** (damages) — late articles
10. **反社会的勢力の排除** (exclusion of anti-social forces) — near end
11. **管轄裁判所** (jurisdiction) — final article
12. **後文** (closing) — signatures, seals, dates

## 12.4 Reading Practice

### Reading 1 — Constitutional Article (Adapted from Articles 25-27)

The following passage is modeled on actual provisions of the Constitution of Japan. Read it carefully, then study the annotations below.

---

> 第二十五条　すべて国民は、健康で文化的な最低限度の生活を営む権利を有する。
>
> ② 国は、すべての生活部面について、社会福祉、社会保障及び公衆衛生の向上及び増進に努めなければならない。
>
> 第二十六条　すべて国民は、法律の定めるところにより、その能力に応じて、ひとしく教育を受ける権利を有する。
>
> ② すべて国民は、法律の定めるところにより、その保護する子女に普通教育を受けさせる義務を負ふ。義務教育は、これを無償とする。
>
> 第二十七条　すべて国民は、勤労の権利を有し、義務を負ふ。
>
> ② 賃金、就業時間、休息その他の勤労条件に関する基準は、法律でこれを定める。
>
> ③ 児童は、これを酷使してはならない。

**Translation:**

> Article 25. All citizens shall have the right to maintain the minimum standards of wholesome and cultured living.
>
> (2) In all spheres of life, the State shall endeavor to promote and extend social welfare, social security, and public health.
>
> Article 26. All citizens shall have the right to receive an equal education corresponding to their ability, as provided for by law.
>
> (2) All citizens shall be obligated to have the boys and girls under their protection receive ordinary education as provided for by law. Such compulsory education shall be free.
>
> Article 27. All citizens shall have the right and the obligation to work.
>
> (2) Standards for wages, working hours, rest, and other working conditions shall be fixed by law.
>
> (3) Children shall not be exploited.

### Structural Annotations

**第二十五条 — Article 25**

- すべて国民は — "All citizens" (subject)
- 健康で文化的な最低限度の生活を営む — "to maintain the minimum standards of wholesome and cultured living" (long modifier for 権利)
  - 健康で — "wholesome and" (な-adjective in て-form, linking)
  - 文化的な — "cultured" (な-adjective modifying 最低限度)
  - 最低限度の — "minimum level of" (modifying 生活)
  - 生活を営む — "to maintain a living" (verb phrase modifying 権利)
- 権利を有する — "to possess the right" (constitutional formula)

**第二十五条②**

- 国は — "the State" (subject)
- すべての生活部面について — "in all spheres of life" (scope marker)
  - 生活部面 (せいかつぶめん) — "spheres of life"
  - について — "concerning, in regard to"
- 社会福祉、社会保障及び公衆衛生の — "of social welfare, social security, and public health"
  - 及び — formal "and" (used in legal/constitutional texts instead of と)
- 向上及び増進に — "toward improvement and promotion"
- 努めなければならない — "must endeavor" (obligation)

**第二十六条②**

- その保護する子女に — "to the boys and girls under their protection"
  - 保護する — "to protect" (relative clause modifying 子女)
  - 子女 (しじょ) — "sons and daughters, children" (formal/legal term)
- 普通教育を受けさせる — "to have [them] receive ordinary education" (causative)
- 義務を負ふ — "to bear the obligation" (constitutional formula, historical kana)
- これを無償とする — "this shall be free" (stipulative formula)
  - 無償 (むしょう) — "free of charge, gratuitous"

**第二十七条③**

- 児童は、これを酷使してはならない — "Children shall not be exploited"
  - 児童 (じどう) — "children" (legal term)
  - 酷使 (こくし) — "exploitation, harsh treatment"
  - ～してはならない — "shall not, must not" (prohibition formula)

### Reading 2 — Contract Clause (Business Outsourcing Agreement)

The following passage is modeled on a standard Japanese business outsourcing contract (業務委託契約書). It includes typical clauses on termination, damages, and confidentiality.

---

> （解除）
> 第十二条　甲又は乙は、相手方が次の各号のいずれかに該当した場合、何らの催告を要せず、直ちに本契約の全部又は一部を解除することができる。
>
> 一　本契約の条項に違反し、相当の期間を定めて催告したにもかかわらず、当該違反が是正されないとき
>
> 二　支払いの停止又は破産手続開始、民事再生手続開始、会社更生手続開始若しくは特別清算開始の申立てがあったとき
>
> 三　自己の振出し又は裏書きに係る手形若しくは小切手が不渡りとなったとき
>
> 四　差押え、仮差押え、仮処分、強制執行又は競売の申立てがあったとき
>
> 五　租税公課を滞納し、その保全差押えを受けたとき
>
> 六　解散、合併又は事業の全部若しくは重要な一部の譲渡の決議をしたとき
>
> （損害賠償）
> 第十三条　甲又は乙が本契約に違反し、相手方に損害を与えた場合、当該違反当事者は相手方に対し、その被った損害（逸失利益を含む。）を賠償する責を負うものとする。ただし、当該損害が違反当事者の責に帰すことのできない事由に基づく場合はこの限りでない。
>
> （秘密保持）
> 第十四条　甲及び乙は、本契約の履行に際し知り得た相手方の技術上又は営業上の秘密（以下「秘密情報」という。）を、本契約の有効期間中のみならず本契約終了後三年間、第三者に開示又は漏洩してはならず、かつ、本契約の履行の目的以外に使用してはならないものとする。ただし、次の各号のいずれかに該当する情報はこの限りでない。
>
> 一　開示を受けた時点で既に公知であった情報
>
> 二　開示を受けた後、自己の責に帰すべき事由によらず公知となった情報
>
> 三　開示を受ける前から適法に保有していた情報
>
> 四　正当な権限を有する第三者から秘密保持義務を課せられることなく取得した情報

**Translation:**

> (Termination)
> Article 12. Party A or Party B may terminate all or part of this agreement immediately without any prior notice if the other party falls under any of the following items:
>
> (i) When the other party has violated the provisions of this agreement and the violation has not been remedied despite a demand for remedy within a reasonable period;
>
> (ii) When the other party has suspended payments or filed for bankruptcy proceedings, civil rehabilitation proceedings, corporate reorganization proceedings, or special liquidation;
>
> (iii) When bills or checks drawn or endorsed by the other party are dishonored;
>
> (iv) When seizure, provisional seizure, provisional disposition, compulsory execution, or auction has been filed against the other party;
>
> (v) When the other party has defaulted on taxes and public charges and received a preservative seizure;
>
> (vi) When the other party has resolved to dissolve, merge, or transfer all or a material part of its business.
>
> (Damages)
> Article 13. In the event that Party A or Party B violates this agreement and causes damage to the other party, the violating party shall be liable to compensate the other party for the damages suffered (including lost profits); provided, however, that this shall not apply in the event that such damages are attributable to causes for which the violating party is not responsible.
>
> (Confidentiality)
> Article 14. Party A and Party B shall not disclose or divulge to any third party any technical or trade secrets of the other party learned in the course of performing this agreement (hereinafter referred to as "Confidential Information"), and shall not use such information for purposes other than the performance of this agreement, not only during the effective period of this agreement but also for a period of three years after the termination of this agreement; provided, however, that the following information shall be excluded:
>
> (i) Information that was already publicly known at the time of disclosure;
>
> (ii) Information that became publicly known after disclosure through no fault of the receiving party;
>
> (iii) Information that was lawfully possessed before disclosure;
>
> (iv) Information obtained from a third party with legitimate authority without any obligation of confidentiality.

### Structural Annotations — Article 12

- 相手方が次の各号のいずれかに該当した場合 — "if the other party falls under any of the following items"
  - 相手方 (あいてがた) — "the other party" (legal term)
  - 各号 (かくごう) — "each item" (referring to numbered sub-provisions)
  - いずれかに該当する — "falls under any of" (legal formula for listing conditions)
- 何らの催告を要せず — "without requiring any prior notice"
  - 何ら — "any, whatsoever" (formal)
  - 催告 (さいこく) — "demand, notice" (legal term)
  - 要せず — "without requiring" (classical negative of 要する)
- 直ちに — "immediately"

### Structural Annotations — Article 13

- その被った損害（逸失利益を含む。） — "the damages suffered (including lost profits)"
  - 逸失利益 (いっしつりえき) — "lost profits" (legal term)
  - を含む — "including" (parenthetical clarification)
- ただし…この限りでない — "provided, however, that this shall not apply"
  - ただし — "however, provided that" (proviso marker)
  - この限りでない — "shall not be limited to this" = "this shall not apply" (legal formula for exceptions)

### Structural Annotations — Article 14

- 知り得た — "came to know, learned" (potential past = "was able to learn" → "learned")
- 以下「秘密情報」という — "hereinafter referred to as 'Confidential Information'"
  - This is the standard definitional formula in Japanese contracts
- 有効期間中のみならず…終了後三年間 — "not only during the effective period but also for three years after termination"
  - のみならず — "not only" (formal equivalent of だけでなく)
- 開示又は漏洩してはならず、かつ…使用してはならない — "shall not disclose or divulge, and moreover shall not use"
  - かつ — "and moreover, and also" (formal conjunction)
- 自己の責に帰すべき事由によらず — "not due to causes attributable to oneself"

## 12.6 Additional Legal Patterns and Expressions

Beyond the core patterns covered in 12.1, legal Japanese employs many additional formulaic expressions. This section covers the most important ones that you will encounter across different types of legal documents.

### ～場合において / ～場合には / ～場合は

These are conditional markers in legal text. ～場合において is the most formal and is used in statutes. ～場合には and ～場合は appear in contracts and regulations.

> 前項の場合において、必要があると認めるときは、委員会は調査を行うことができる。
> "In the case of the preceding paragraph, when it is recognized that there is a need, the committee may conduct an investigation."

> 契約期間中に甲が死亡した場合には、本契約は当然に終了する。
> "In the event that Party A dies during the contract period, this agreement shall terminate automatically."

> 天災地変その他不可抗力により履行が遅延した場合は、遅延損害金を請求しないものとする。
> "In the event that performance is delayed due to natural disasters or other force majeure, penalty interest shall not be claimed."

### ～ただし書 / ただし…この限りでない

The ただし clause is the standard way to create an exception to a general rule. The formula is: [general rule]. ただし、[exception condition]はこの限りでない.

> 本規約は全会員に適用される。ただし、名誉会員についてはこの限りでない。
> "These bylaws apply to all members. However, this shall not apply to honorary members."

> すべての届出は書面によるものとする。ただし、緊急の場合はこの限りでない。
> "All notifications shall be in writing. However, this shall not apply in cases of emergency."

The phrase この限りでない literally means "is not within this limit" — that is, "the preceding rule does not apply to this case." It is one of the most frequently occurring formulas in Japanese law.

### ～とみなす / ～と推定する

These two expressions create legal fictions and presumptions:

- **みなす** (to deem) — creates an irrebuttable presumption. Once something is "deemed" to be the case, it cannot be challenged.
- **推定する** (to presume) — creates a rebuttable presumption. The presumption holds unless evidence to the contrary is presented.

> 届出が到達した時点で承諾したものとみなす。
> "It shall be deemed that acceptance was given at the time the notification arrived."

> 配達証明付郵便により発送した場合は、発送の日から三日後に到達したものと推定する。
> "In the event of dispatch by certified mail, it shall be presumed to have arrived three days after the date of dispatch."

### ～しなければならない vs ～することができる vs ～するものとする

These three expressions create different levels of legal obligation:

| Expression | Force | English equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| ～しなければならない | Mandatory obligation | "must," "shall" (obligatory) |
| ～するものとする | Stipulative norm | "shall" (establishing a rule) |
| ～することができる | Permission/right | "may," "can" |
| ～してはならない | Prohibition | "must not," "shall not" |
| ～することを要しない | Exemption from obligation | "need not" |

> 届出は、事由の発生した日から十四日以内にしなければならない。
> "Notification must be made within fourteen days from the date the cause arose."

> 届出は、書面により行うものとする。
> "Notification shall be made in writing."

> 届出人は、届出に際し、添付書類の提出を求めることができる。
> "[The authority] may require the submission of attached documents at the time of notification."

> 届出人は、届出の内容を虚偽のものとしてはならない。
> "The person filing notification must not make the content of the notification false."

> 届出人が海外に居住する場合は、代理人による届出をもって足り、本人の出頭を要しない。
> "When the person filing notification resides overseas, notification by proxy shall suffice, and personal appearance shall not be required."

### ～に該当する

This means "to fall under, to correspond to, to constitute." It is used when listing conditions or categories.

> 次の各号のいずれかに該当する者は、会員となることができない。
> "A person who falls under any of the following items may not become a member."

> 当該行為が刑法第二百四条に該当する場合は、刑事告発を行うものとする。
> "When the act in question constitutes an offense under Article 204 of the Penal Code, a criminal complaint shall be filed."

### ～若しくは / ～又は / ～及び / ～並びに

Legal Japanese uses a strict hierarchy for "or" and "and":

**"Or" (disjunction):**
- 又は (または) — connects larger units
- 若しくは (もしくは) — connects smaller units within a 又は group

**"And" (conjunction):**
- 及び (および) — connects smaller units
- 並びに (ならびに) — connects larger units that contain 及び groups

> 懲役又は罰金に処する。
> "Shall be punished by imprisonment or a fine." (simple "or")

> 懲役若しくは禁錮又は罰金に処する。
> "Shall be punished by imprisonment or confinement, or a fine." (nested "or": 懲役/禁錮 are one group, 罰金 is another)

> 会長及び副会長
> "The chairperson and vice-chairperson" (simple "and")

> 会長及び副会長並びに理事及び監事
> "The chairperson and vice-chairperson, as well as directors and auditors" (nested "and")

This system is unique to legal Japanese and is strictly observed in statutes. Misusing 若しくは and 又は, or 及び and 並びに, changes the meaning of a provision.

## 12.7 Additional Reading Practice — Statute Excerpt

The following passage is modeled on a provision from the Consumer Contract Act (消費者契約法). It demonstrates how statutes use the patterns covered in this chapter in combination.

---

> （消費者の取消権）
> 第四条　消費者は、事業者が消費者契約の締結について勧誘をするに際し、当該消費者に対して次の各号に掲げる行為をしたことにより当該消費者が当該各号に定める誤認をし、それによって当該消費者契約の申込み又はその承諾の意思表示をしたときは、これを取り消すことができる。
>
> 一　重要事項について事実と異なることを告げること。当該告げられた内容が事実であるとの誤認
>
> 二　物品、権利、役務その他の当該消費者契約の目的となるものに関し、将来におけるその価額、将来において当該消費者が受け取るべき金額その他の将来における変動が不確実な事項につき断定的判断を提供すること。当該提供された断定的判断の内容が確実であるとの誤認

**Translation:**

> (Consumer's Right of Rescission)
> Article 4. When a business operator, in soliciting the conclusion of a consumer contract, has committed any of the acts listed in the following items against the consumer, causing the consumer to make the misapprehension defined in the respective items, and the consumer has thereby made a manifestation of intention to apply for or accept the consumer contract, the consumer may rescind such manifestation.
>
> (i) Stating something contrary to fact regarding an important matter. Misapprehension that the stated content is fact.
>
> (ii) Providing a conclusive judgment regarding matters whose future fluctuation is uncertain, such as the future value of goods, rights, services, or other objects of the consumer contract, or future amounts to be received by the consumer. Misapprehension that the content of the provided conclusive judgment is certain.

### Structural Analysis

This is a single sentence of extraordinary complexity. Let us break it down layer by layer.

**Main structure:**

消費者は…[long chain of conditions]…ときは、これを取り消すことができる。

The main verb is 取り消すことができる ("may rescind"). Everything between 消費者は and ときは is a chain of conditions that must all be met for the right to arise.

**Condition chain:**

1. 事業者が…勧誘をするに際し — "when the business operator solicits" — に際し = "on the occasion of"
2. 当該消費者に対して次の各号に掲げる行為をしたことにより — "due to having committed the acts listed in the following items against the consumer"
3. 当該消費者が当該各号に定める誤認をし — "the consumer made the misapprehension defined in the respective items"
4. それによって…意思表示をしたときは — "and thereby made a manifestation of intention"

The four conditions must all be satisfied: the business operator solicited, committed a listed act, the consumer was misled, and the consumer acted on that misapprehension. Only then may the consumer rescind.

**Key vocabulary:**

| Term | Reading | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 取消権 | とりけしけん | right of rescission |
| 勧誘 | かんゆう | solicitation |
| 誤認 | ごにん | misapprehension, mistake |
| 意思表示 | いしひょうじ | manifestation of intention (legal act) |
| 断定的判断 | だんていてきはんだん | conclusive/definitive judgment |
| 価額 | かがく | value, price |

> (a) 議決権は、一株につき一個とする。

Pattern: _______________

> (b) 本条の規定は、法令に基づく開示義務の履行を妨げない。

Pattern: _______________

> (c) 入会の申込みは、所定の書式による場合に限る。

Pattern: _______________

> (d) 会員が六ヶ月以上会費を滞納した場合は、退会したものとみなす。

Pattern: _______________

> (e) 甲の責に帰すべき事由により納品が遅延した場合、甲は遅延損害金を支払わなければならない。

Pattern: _______________

### Answers

(a) ものとする — stipulative norm establishing that each share carries one vote.

(b) を妨げない — permissive clause clarifying that the provision does not preclude compliance with statutory disclosure obligations.

Translate the following contract clause into English:

> 本契約の有効期間は、本契約締結の日から一年間とする。ただし、期間満了日の三十日前までに甲又は乙から書面による解約の申入れがない場合は、本契約は同一条件でさらに一年間自動的に更新されるものとし、以後も同様とする。

The effective period of this agreement shall be one year from the date of conclusion of this agreement. ### Exercise 3 — Structural parsing

Parse the following statutory sentence by identifying: (a) the subject, (b) all conditions, (c) the main verb.

## 12.9 Legal Document Types

Understanding the hierarchy of Japanese legal documents helps you interpret any legal text you encounter. Each type of document has its own authority, drafting conventions, and typical language.

### Hierarchy of legal instruments

| Document | Reading | Authority | Drafter |
|---|---|---|---|
| 憲法 | けんぽう | Supreme law | The people (via referendum) |
| 法律 | ほうりつ | Statute (act of parliament) | National Diet |
| 政令 | せいれい | Government ordinance (cabinet order) | Cabinet |
| 省令 | しょうれい | Ministerial ordinance | Individual ministries |
| 条例 | じょうれい | Local ordinance | Local assemblies |
| 規則 | きそく | Rules, regulations | Administrative agencies, organizations |
| 通達 | つうたつ | Circular, directive | Ministries to subordinate agencies |
| ガイドライン | ガイドライン | Guideline | Various |

Each lower-level document must conform to the higher-level ones. A 政令 cannot contradict a 法律, and a 法律 cannot contradict the 憲法.

### Numbering conventions

Legal documents use a consistent numbering system:

| Level | Term | Reading | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chapter | 章 | しょう | 第一章 総則 |
| Section | 節 | せつ | 第一節 通則 |
| Article | 条 | じょう | 第一条 |
| Paragraph | 項 | こう | ② (second paragraph) |
| Item | 号 | ごう | 一、二、三 |
| Sub-item | イ、ロ、ハ | イ、ロ、ハ | Katakana iroha order |

> 民法第七百九条
> "Article 709 of the Civil Code"

> 労働基準法第三十六条第一項
> "Article 36, Paragraph 1 of the Labor Standards Act"

### Cross-referencing formulas

Legal documents constantly refer to other provisions. The standard formulas are:

> 前条の規定にかかわらず — "notwithstanding the provisions of the preceding article"

> 第五条第二項に規定する場合を除き — "except in the cases prescribed in Article 5, Paragraph 2"

> 第三条から第八条までの規定は、～に準用する — "The provisions of Articles 3 through 8 shall apply mutatis mutandis to ~"

The term 準用する (じゅんようする) means "to apply mutatis mutandis" — that is, to apply the same provisions to a different situation with necessary modifications. This is one of the most characteristic concepts in Japanese legal drafting.

### Reading practice — Civil Code article

> 第七百九条　故意又は過失によって他人の権利又は法律上保護される利益を侵害した者は、これによって生じた損害を賠償する責任を負う。

**Translation:** "A person who has infringed upon the rights of others, or legally protected interests of others, intentionally or negligently, shall bear responsibility to compensate for any resulting damages."

**Structural breakdown:**
- 故意又は過失によって — "intentionally or negligently" — 故意 (こい) = intent, 過失 (かしつ) = negligence
- 他人の権利又は法律上保護される利益を — "the rights of others or legally protected interests of others"
- 侵害した者は — "a person who has infringed" — 者 (もの) = "a person (legal subject)"
- これによって生じた損害を — "damages arising therefrom"
- 賠償する責任を負う — "shall bear responsibility to compensate"

This single article — Civil Code Article 709 — is the foundation of Japan's entire tort law system. Every law student in Japan memorizes it. Its structure is a perfect example of how legal Japanese front-loads conditions and delays the main predicate.

## 12.10 Comparative Legal Expressions

Legal Japanese contains many expressions that function as near-synonyms but carry different legal weight. Understanding these distinctions prevents misreading.

### Obligation strength

| Expression | Strength | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ～しなければならない | Strong obligation (must) | 届出をしなければならない |
| ～するものとする | Normative stipulation (shall) | 届出は書面によるものとする |
| ～するよう努めなければならない | Best-effort obligation (shall endeavor) | 環境の保全に努めなければならない |
| ～するよう努めるものとする | Soft best-effort (shall endeavor — softer) | 情報の公開に努めるものとする |
| ～することが望ましい | Desirable (it is desirable that) | 第三者の意見を求めることが望ましい |

The progression from strong to weak obligation is critical in legal interpretation. A court enforces しなければならない provisions strictly, while 望ましい provisions are merely advisory.

> 事業者は、消費者に対し、商品の安全性に関する情報を提供するよう努めなければならない。
> "Business operators shall endeavor to provide consumers with information regarding the safety of products."

This is an "effort obligation" (努力義務, どりょくぎむ) — the operator must try, but failure to provide the information is not automatically a violation.

### Permission and prohibition

| Expression | Function |
|---|---|
| ～することができる | Permission ("may") |
| ～してはならない | Prohibition ("must not") |
| ～を妨げない | Non-preclusion ("does not preclude") |
| ～することを要しない | Exemption ("need not") |
| ～することを得ない | Cannot, is unable to (formal) |

> 本人の同意を得ることが困難な場合は、個人情報を第三者に提供することを要しない。
> "When it is difficult to obtain the consent of the individual, providing personal information to a third party shall not be required."

### Time expressions in law

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 直ちに | immediately (strongest urgency) |
| 遅滞なく | without delay (allows reasonable time) |
| 速やかに | promptly (between the above two) |

These three expressions are not interchangeable. Court precedent has established that 直ちに means "right away with no room for delay," 遅滞なく means "as soon as reasonably possible," and 速やかに falls between them.

> 事故が発生したときは、直ちに消防署に通報しなければならない。
> "When an accident occurs, [the responsible party] must immediately notify the fire department."

> 届出事項に変更が生じたときは、遅滞なく届け出なければならない。
> "When a change occurs in the matters of notification, [the party] must report without delay."

> 審査の結果は、速やかに申請者に通知するものとする。
> "The results of the review shall be promptly communicated to the applicant."
