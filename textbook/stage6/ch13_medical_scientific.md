# Chapter 13 — Medical and Scientific Japanese

Japanese medical and scientific writing occupies a unique space in the language. It draws heavily on Sino-Japanese vocabulary (漢語), uses a distinctive impersonal style, and follows conventions borrowed from — but not identical to — Western academic writing. A medical case report, a pharmaceutical insert, a research paper abstract, and a popular science article each have their own register, but they share a core vocabulary and a set of grammatical patterns that this chapter will teach you.

The difficulty of medical and scientific Japanese lies not in the grammar, which tends to be straightforward, but in the density of technical vocabulary and the precision with which terms must be understood. A single character difference — 良性 versus 悪性, 急性 versus 慢性 — changes a diagnosis completely. This chapter gives you the vocabulary to navigate these distinctions and the reading practice to apply them.

## 13.2 Scientific Writing Style

Japanese scientific writing follows conventions that are distinct from both everyday Japanese and literary Japanese. The style is impersonal, precise, and heavily nominalized. The following patterns appear in virtually every research paper, grant proposal, and scientific report.

### ～と仮定する

This means "to hypothesize that" or "to assume that." It is used to state the working assumption of a study.

> 温度の上昇が酵素活性に影響を与えると仮定する。
> "We hypothesize that the rise in temperature affects enzyme activity."

> この反応が一次反応であると仮定した場合、反応速度は基質濃度に比例する。
> "Assuming this reaction is a first-order reaction, the reaction rate is proportional to substrate concentration."

### ～を対象とした

This means "targeting" or "with [X] as the subject/sample." It is the standard way to describe the study population or the focus of research.

> 65歳以上の高齢者を対象とした大規模調査を実施した。
> "A large-scale survey targeting elderly individuals aged 65 and over was conducted."

> 本研究は、日本語を母語とする大学生200名を対象とした。
> "This study targeted 200 university students who are native speakers of Japanese."

### ～を測定した結果

This means "as a result of measuring [X]." It introduces findings.

> 血中コレステロール値を測定した結果、対照群と比較して有意な低下が認められた。
> "As a result of measuring blood cholesterol levels, a significant decrease was observed compared to the control group."

> 反応速度を測定した結果、触媒の添加により約30%の増加が確認された。
> "As a result of measuring the reaction rate, an increase of approximately 30% was confirmed through the addition of a catalyst."

### 統計的に有意

This means "statistically significant." It is a fixed phrase that appears in the results section of almost every quantitative study.

> 実験群と対照群の間に統計的に有意な差が認められた（p < 0.05）。
> "A statistically significant difference was observed between the experimental group and the control group (p < 0.05)."

> 両群間の差は統計的に有意ではなかった。
> "The difference between the two groups was not statistically significant."

### Additional scientific writing patterns

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ～が示唆された | it was suggested that | 遺伝的要因の関与が示唆された |
| ～と考えられる | it is considered that | 環境要因が主な原因であると考えられる |
| ～が明らかになった | it became clear that | 新たなメカニズムが明らかになった |
| ～との相関が見られた | a correlation with ~ was observed | 睡眠時間と記憶力との相関が見られた |
| ～に寄与する | to contribute to | 運動が健康維持に寄与する |
| ～を踏まえて | based on, taking into account | 先行研究の結果を踏まえて |
| ～における | in, at (formal location marker) | 臨床試験における副作用の発生率 |
| ～に関する | concerning, regarding | 免疫応答に関する研究 |

### The passive voice in scientific Japanese

Scientific Japanese uses the passive voice extensively — far more than conversational or even literary Japanese. This serves the same function as the English scientific passive: it removes the researcher as the agent and focuses attention on the phenomenon.

> 試料は37℃で24時間培養された。
> "The samples were incubated at 37°C for 24 hours."

> 患者には書面によるインフォームドコンセントが取得された。
> "Written informed consent was obtained from the patients."

> 本実験は二重盲検法により実施された。
> "This experiment was conducted using a double-blind method."

Note that in scientific Japanese, the passive does not carry the adversarial or suffering nuance (迷惑の受身) that it can carry in everyday Japanese. It is purely structural.

### Hedging and qualification

Japanese scientific writing hedges extensively, using expressions that soften claims:

> ～可能性がある — "there is a possibility that"
> ～と推測される — "it is speculated that"
> ～と思われる — "it appears that" (literary/scientific)
> ～であろう — "it is likely that" (written conjecture)
> ～ないとは限らない — "it is not necessarily the case that ~ not" (double negative hedge)

> 本結果は、食事制限が血圧低下に寄与する可能性があることを示唆している。
> "These results suggest the possibility that dietary restriction may contribute to lowering blood pressure."

## 13.3 Reading Practice

### Reading 1 — Medical Article Abstract

The following passage is modeled on a typical Japanese medical journal abstract. It follows the standard IMRAD structure (Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion) compressed into a single paragraph.

---

> 【目的】本研究は、2型糖尿病患者における新規GLP-1受容体作動薬Xの有効性及び安全性を、従来のDPP-4阻害薬と比較検討することを目的とした。
>
> 【方法】当院内分泌内科外来に通院中の2型糖尿病患者120名（男性68名、女性52名、平均年齢58.3±9.7歳）を対象とし、無作為に2群に分けた。A群（60名）にはGLP-1受容体作動薬Xを週1回皮下投与し、B群（60名）にはDPP-4阻害薬Yを1日1回経口投与した。主要評価項目はHbA1c値の変化量とし、副次評価項目として体重変化、空腹時血糖値、及び有害事象の発現率を評価した。観察期間は24週間とした。
>
> 【結果】24週時点において、A群のHbA1c値は投与前7.8±0.9%から6.5±0.7%へと有意に低下し（p < 0.001）、B群の7.7±0.8%から7.0±0.8%への低下と比較して、A群の方がより顕著な改善を示した（群間差 −0.6%、95%信頼区間 −0.9〜−0.3、p < 0.001）。体重については、A群で平均3.2kg減少したのに対し、B群では0.4kgの減少にとどまった。有害事象としては、A群で悪心（15.0%）及び下痢（8.3%）が認められたが、いずれも軽度であり、投与中止に至った症例はなかった。
>
> 【考察】GLP-1受容体作動薬Xは、DPP-4阻害薬Yと比較して、2型糖尿病患者の血糖コントロール及び体重管理においてより優れた効果を示した。副作用は消化器系に限定され、臨床的に管理可能な範囲であった。今後、長期的な心血管系イベントへの影響について更なる検討が必要である。

**Full Translation:**

> [Objective] The purpose of this study was to compare and evaluate the efficacy and safety of a novel GLP-1 receptor agonist X versus a conventional DPP-4 inhibitor in patients with type 2 diabetes.
>
> [Methods] A total of 120 patients with type 2 diabetes (68 males, 52 females, mean age 58.3 ± 9.7 years) attending the endocrinology outpatient clinic of our hospital were enrolled and randomly divided into two groups. Group A (60 patients) received subcutaneous administration of GLP-1 receptor agonist X once weekly, and Group B (60 patients) received oral administration of DPP-4 inhibitor Y once daily. The primary endpoint was the change in HbA1c level, and secondary endpoints included changes in body weight, fasting blood glucose levels, and the incidence of adverse events. The observation period was 24 weeks.
>
> [Results] At week 24, the HbA1c level in Group A decreased significantly from 7.8 ± 0.9% to 6.5 ± 0.7% before administration (p < 0.001), showing more pronounced improvement compared to the decrease from 7.7 ± 0.8% to 7.0 ± 0.8% in Group B (between-group difference −0.6%, 95% confidence interval −0.9 to −0.3, p < 0.001). Regarding body weight, Group A showed a mean decrease of 3.2 kg, while Group B showed a decrease of only 0.4 kg. As adverse events, nausea (15.0%) and diarrhea (8.3%) were observed in Group A, but all were mild, and there were no cases leading to discontinuation of treatment.
>
> [Discussion] GLP-1 receptor agonist X demonstrated superior efficacy in glycemic control and weight management in patients with type 2 diabetes compared to DPP-4 inhibitor Y. Side effects were limited to the gastrointestinal system and were within a clinically manageable range. Further investigation of the long-term effects on cardiovascular events is needed in the future.

### Structural Annotations

**Title section (目的)**

- ～を目的とした — "had as its purpose" — standard formula for stating a study's aim
- 有効性及び安全性を…比較検討すること — the entire nominalized phrase is the object of 目的とした
- 従来の — "conventional, traditional" — implies the comparator is an existing standard treatment

**Methods section (方法)**

- 当院…外来に通院中の — "attending the outpatient clinic of our hospital" — long modifier preceding 患者
- を対象とし — "targeting, enrolling" — standard enrollment description
- 無作為に2群に分けた — "randomly divided into two groups"
- 主要評価項目は…とし — "the primary endpoint was designated as" — note とする used to define/stipulate
- 副次評価項目として — "as secondary endpoints" — として marks the capacity/role

**Results section (結果)**

- 有意に低下し — "decreased significantly" — 有意に = "significantly" (statistically)
- より顕著な改善を示した — "showed more pronounced improvement"
- 群間差 — "between-group difference"
- ～に対し — "in contrast to, whereas" — introduces comparison
- ～にとどまった — "was limited to, was only" — indicates a small or disappointing result
- ～に至った症例はなかった — "there were no cases that reached (the point of)" — no cases severe enough to cause discontinuation

**Discussion section (考察)**

- より優れた効果を示した — "demonstrated superior efficacy"
- ～に限定され — "was limited to" (passive)
- 臨床的に管理可能な範囲 — "clinically manageable range"
- 更なる検討が必要である — "further investigation is needed" — standard closing formula

### Reading 2 — Popular Science Article

The following passage is modeled on a Japanese popular science magazine article. The register is less formal than the medical abstract but still uses technical vocabulary. This style bridges the gap between specialist and general audiences.

---

> 　腸内細菌と脳の関係――いわゆる「腸脳相関」――が、近年の研究で急速に解明されつつある。私たちの腸には約1,000種、100兆個もの細菌が棲んでおり、その総重量は1.5キログラムから2キログラムに達する。この膨大な微生物群は「腸内フローラ」と呼ばれ、消化・吸収の補助にとどまらず、免疫系の調節、ビタミンの合成、さらには脳機能にまで影響を及ぼしていることが分かってきた。
>
> 　腸と脳は、迷走神経を介して直接つながっている。腸内細菌が産生する短鎖脂肪酸やセロトニン前駆体などの代謝産物は、この神経経路を通じて脳に信号を送ることができる。実際、腸内細菌を持たない無菌マウスを用いた実験では、通常のマウスと比較してストレス反応が過剰になることが報告されている。興味深いことに、特定の乳酸菌を投与すると、この過剰なストレス反応が正常化することも確認された。
>
> 　ヒトを対象とした研究も進んでいる。ある大規模疫学調査では、うつ病患者の腸内細菌叢が健常者と有意に異なることが明らかになった。具体的には、酪酸産生菌の一種であるフィーカリバクテリウム属の菌が、うつ病患者において顕著に減少していた。酪酸は腸管上皮細胞のエネルギー源であるだけでなく、抗炎症作用を持つことが知られており、その減少が腸管バリア機能の低下、ひいては全身性の軽度炎症を引き起こしている可能性が指摘されている。
>
> 　こうした知見は、精神疾患の新たな治療戦略としての「サイコバイオティクス」――精神的健康に良い影響を与える生菌や食品成分――という概念の登場につながった。まだ臨床応用には多くの課題が残るものの、腸内環境を整えることが心の健康につながるという考え方は、もはや民間療法の域を超え、科学的根拠に裏打ちされた医学的知見となりつつある。

**Full Translation:**

> The relationship between gut bacteria and the brain — the so-called "gut-brain axis" — is being rapidly elucidated by recent research. Approximately 1,000 species and 100 trillion individual bacteria inhabit our intestines, with a total weight reaching 1.5 to 2 kilograms. This enormous community of microorganisms is called the "gut flora," and it has become clear that its influence extends beyond merely assisting with digestion and absorption to regulating the immune system, synthesizing vitamins, and even affecting brain function.
>
> The gut and the brain are directly connected via the vagus nerve. Metabolic products such as short-chain fatty acids and serotonin precursors produced by gut bacteria can send signals to the brain through this neural pathway. In fact, experiments using germ-free mice — mice that possess no gut bacteria — have reported that stress responses become excessive compared to normal mice. Interestingly, it has also been confirmed that administering specific lactic acid bacteria normalizes this excessive stress response.
>
> Research targeting humans is also progressing. One large-scale epidemiological survey revealed that the gut microbiota of patients with depression differs significantly from that of healthy individuals. Specifically, bacteria of the genus Faecalibacterium, a type of butyrate-producing bacterium, were notably decreased in patients with depression. Butyrate is known not only as an energy source for intestinal epithelial cells but also as having anti-inflammatory properties, and it has been pointed out that its decrease may cause a decline in intestinal barrier function and, consequently, systemic low-grade inflammation.
>
> These findings have led to the emergence of the concept of "psychobiotics" — live bacteria and food components that have a positive effect on mental health — as a new treatment strategy for mental illness. Although many challenges remain before clinical application, the idea that maintaining a healthy gut environment leads to mental health is no longer in the realm of folk remedies but is becoming a medical finding backed by scientific evidence.

### Structural Annotations

**Paragraph 1**

- いわゆる「腸脳相関」 — "the so-called 'gut-brain axis'" — いわゆる introduces a named concept
- 解明されつつある — "is being elucidated" — ～つつある indicates an ongoing process
- ～にとどまらず — "not limited to, going beyond" — formal equivalent of だけでなく
- ～にまで影響を及ぼしている — "extends its influence even to" — にまで emphasizes the surprising reach
- ～ことが分かってきた — "it has become understood that" — ～てきた indicates a gradual process of discovery

**Paragraph 2**

- ～を介して — "via, through" — formal connector for pathways and intermediaries
- ～を用いた実験 — "experiments using" — を用いる is the formal equivalent of を使う
- ～ことが報告されている — "it has been reported that" — passive + いる for established findings
- 興味深いことに — "interestingly" — discourse marker for surprising findings

**Paragraph 3**

- ～ことが明らかになった — "it became clear that" — standard phrasing for confirmed findings
- ～だけでなく…も — "not only ~ but also" — parallel structure
- ひいては — "and by extension, consequently" — formal discourse connector
- ～可能性が指摘されている — "the possibility has been pointed out" — hedged claim

**Paragraph 4**

- ～という概念の登場につながった — "led to the emergence of the concept of"
- ～ものの — "although" — concessive (from Chapter 1 of this stage)
- もはや…域を超え — "has now transcended the realm of" — もはや = "no longer, by now"
- ～に裏打ちされた — "backed by, underpinned by" — 裏打ち = "lining, backing"

## 13.5 Pharmaceutical Package Inserts

The 添付文書 (package insert) is a regulated document that accompanies every prescription drug in Japan. It follows a strict format established by the Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices Agency (PMDA). Being able to read a package insert is a practical skill — patients, caregivers, and researchers all encounter these documents.

### Standard sections of a package insert

| Section | Reading | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 警告 | けいこく | Warning (most serious cautions) |
| 禁忌 | きんき | Contraindications (who must NOT take this drug) |
| 組成・性状 | そせい・せいじょう | Composition and properties |
| 効能・効果 | こうのう・こうか | Indications (approved uses) |
| 用法・用量 | ようほう・ようりょう | Dosage and administration |
| 使用上の注意 | しようじょうのちゅうい | Precautions for use |
| 副作用 | ふくさよう | Adverse reactions |
| 相互作用 | そうごさよう | Drug interactions |
| 薬物動態 | やくぶつどうたい | Pharmacokinetics |
| 臨床成績 | りんしょうせいせき | Clinical results |
| 貯法・有効期間 | ちょほう・ゆうこうきかん | Storage and shelf life |

### Key package insert expressions

> 本剤の成分に対し過敏症の既往歴のある患者には投与しないこと。
> "This drug shall not be administered to patients with a history of hypersensitivity to any component of this product."

The imperative ～しないこと is characteristic of package inserts. It functions as a direction — "do not do X" — without the softer hedging of academic writing.

> 妊婦又は妊娠している可能性のある女性には、治療上の有益性が危険性を上回ると判断される場合にのみ投与すること。
> "This drug shall be administered to pregnant women or women who may be pregnant only when the therapeutic benefit is judged to outweigh the risk."

> 本剤投与中は、授乳を避けさせること。
> "Breastfeeding should be avoided during administration of this drug."

### Reading a dosage section

> 【用法・用量】
> 通常、成人にはアムロジピンとして2.5〜5mgを1日1回経口投与する。なお、症状に応じ適宜増減するが、効果不十分な場合には1日1回10mgまで増量することができる。

**Translation:**

> [Dosage and Administration]
> Usually, for adults, 2.5 to 5 mg of amlodipine is orally administered once daily. The dosage may be adjusted as appropriate according to symptoms, but in cases of insufficient efficacy, the dosage may be increased to up to 10 mg once daily.

**Key phrases:**

- 通常、成人には — "usually, for adults" — standard opening
- ～として — "as" (indicating the active ingredient)
- 1日1回 — "once daily"
- 適宜増減する — "adjust as appropriate" — 適宜 (てきぎ) = "as appropriate"
- 効果不十分な場合には — "in cases of insufficient efficacy"
- ～まで増量することができる — "may be increased up to"

### Reading an adverse reactions section

> 【副作用】
> 承認時までの臨床試験において、総症例892例中109例（12.2%）に副作用が認められた。主な副作用は、ほてり22例（2.5%）、めまい15例（1.7%）、頭痛12例（1.3%）、動悸10例（1.1%）であった。（承認時）
>
> 重大な副作用
> 1. 肝機能障害、黄疸（頻度不明）：AST、ALT、γ-GTPの上昇等を伴う肝機能障害や黄疸があらわれることがある。
> 2. 無顆粒球症、白血球減少、血小板減少（頻度不明）：無顆粒球症、白血球減少、血小板減少があらわれることがあるので、検査を行うなど観察を十分に行い、異常が認められた場合には投与を中止し、適切な処置を行うこと。

**Translation:**

> [Adverse Reactions]
> In clinical trials up to the time of approval, adverse reactions were observed in 109 of 892 total cases (12.2%). The main adverse reactions were flushing in 22 cases (2.5%), dizziness in 15 cases (1.7%), headache in 12 cases (1.3%), and palpitations in 10 cases (1.1%). (At time of approval)
>
> Serious Adverse Reactions
> 1. Hepatic dysfunction, jaundice (frequency unknown): Hepatic dysfunction or jaundice accompanied by elevations in AST, ALT, gamma-GTP, etc. may occur.
> 2. Agranulocytosis, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia (frequency unknown): Since agranulocytosis, leukopenia, and thrombocytopenia may occur, sufficient observation including laboratory tests should be performed, and if abnormalities are observed, administration should be discontinued and appropriate measures taken.

## 13.6 Scientific Paper Methodology Patterns

Scientific methodology sections use a distinctive set of patterns that describe experimental procedures in precise, reproducible terms. These patterns are highly formulaic.

### Describing subjects and materials

> 実験には、Wistar系雄性ラット（8週齢、体重200〜250g）を使用した。
> "Wistar male rats (8 weeks old, body weight 200-250 g) were used for the experiment."

> 試薬はすべて和光純薬工業株式会社より購入し、さらなる精製を行わずに使用した。
> "All reagents were purchased from Wako Pure Chemical Industries and used without further purification."

### Describing procedures

> 試料を遠心分離機（3,000 rpm、10分間、4℃）にかけ、上清を回収した。
> "The samples were centrifuged (3,000 rpm, 10 minutes, 4°C) and the supernatant was collected."

> PCR反応は、95℃で30秒の変性、60℃で30秒のアニーリング、72℃で1分の伸長反応を1サイクルとし、35サイクル行った。
> "The PCR reaction consisted of 35 cycles, with each cycle comprising denaturation at 95°C for 30 seconds, annealing at 60°C for 30 seconds, and extension at 72°C for 1 minute."

### Statistical analysis descriptions

> 統計解析にはSPSS ver. 27を用い、対応のないt検定により群間比較を行った。有意水準はp < 0.05とした。
> "SPSS ver. 27 was used for statistical analysis, and between-group comparisons were performed using the unpaired t-test. The significance level was set at p < 0.05."

> データは平均値±標準偏差で表した。多重比較にはTukey法を用いた。
> "Data were expressed as mean ± standard deviation. The Tukey method was used for multiple comparisons."

## 13.8 Comparison: Medical vs. Popular Science Register

The two reading passages in this chapter illustrate a key distinction in scientific Japanese: the formal research register versus the popular explanation register. Understanding these differences helps you calibrate your reading expectations.

### Register comparison table

| Feature | Research paper | Popular science |
|---|---|---|
| Sentence length | Long, multi-clause | Moderate |
| Technical terms | Used without explanation | Introduced and defined |
| Numbers | Precise (p-values, CI, ±SD) | Approximate (約, ～程度) |
| Hedging | Extensive, formulaic | Present but less rigid |
| Voice | Passive dominant | Mixed active/passive |
| Self-reference | Avoided (impersonal) | Occasional 私たち |
| Reader assumed to know | Methodology, statistics | Basic science literacy |
| Paragraph structure | IMRAD sections | Narrative flow |
| Citations | (Author Year) format | Informal or absent |
| Purpose | Report findings | Explain and engage |

### Examples of the same information in both registers

**Research register:**

> 腸内細菌叢の組成と宿主の免疫応答との間に有意な相関が認められた（r = 0.68, p < 0.001）。
> "A significant correlation was observed between the composition of the gut microbiota and the host's immune response (r = 0.68, p < 0.001)."

**Popular science register:**

> 腸内細菌のバランスと体の免疫力には、密接な関係があることが分かってきた。
> "It has become clear that there is a close relationship between the balance of gut bacteria and the body's immune strength."

The information is essentially the same, but the research version provides precise statistical evidence while the popular version uses accessible language and emphasizes the discovery process (分かってきた).

**Research register:**

> 被験者に乳酸菌含有カプセル（1日あたり1×10⁹ CFU）を12週間経口投与した。
> "Capsules containing lactic acid bacteria (1 × 10⁹ CFU per day) were orally administered to subjects for 12 weeks."

**Popular science register:**

> 実験では、参加者に乳酸菌のサプリメントを約3ヶ月間飲んでもらった。
> "In the experiment, participants were asked to take a lactic acid bacteria supplement for about 3 months."

Note how the popular version uses ～てもらった (a benefactive construction suggesting cooperation) rather than the cold passive 投与された, and rounds "12 weeks" to "about 3 months."

## 13.9 Reading a Clinical Trial Summary

The following short passage represents a clinical trial result as it might appear in a medical review article. This bridges the formal abstract and the popular science article in register.

---

> 　関節リウマチ治療における生物学的製剤の有効性に関しては、多数の無作為化比較試験が実施されている。代表的な研究であるSAKURA試験（2019年）では、メトトレキサート単剤療法で効果不十分であった中等度から重度の関節リウマチ患者268名を対象とし、新規IL-6受容体阻害薬Z（162mg、2週間隔皮下投与）の追加投与群とプラセボ追加群を24週間にわたって比較した。
>
> 　主要評価項目であるACR20改善率は、Z追加群で67.9%、プラセボ群で32.1%であり、Z追加群が有意に優れていた（p < 0.001）。副次評価項目であるDAS28-ESRの変化量についても、Z追加群で有意な改善が確認された。重篤な有害事象の発現率は両群間で有意差を認めなかった。本試験の結果は、メトトレキサート効果不十分例に対するIL-6受容体阻害薬の追加投与が有効かつ安全であることを支持するものであった。

**Translation:**

> Regarding the efficacy of biological agents in the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis, numerous randomized controlled trials have been conducted. In the representative SAKURA trial (2019), 268 patients with moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis who had an inadequate response to methotrexate monotherapy were enrolled, and a group receiving add-on therapy with the novel IL-6 receptor inhibitor Z (162 mg, subcutaneous administration every two weeks) was compared with a placebo add-on group over 24 weeks.
>
> The ACR20 improvement rate, the primary endpoint, was 67.9% in the Z add-on group and 32.1% in the placebo group, with the Z add-on group being significantly superior (p < 0.001). A significant improvement was also confirmed in the Z add-on group for the change in DAS28-ESR, a secondary endpoint. No significant difference was observed between the two groups in the incidence of serious adverse events. The results of this trial supported the conclusion that add-on therapy with an IL-6 receptor inhibitor is effective and safe for patients with inadequate response to methotrexate.

**Key vocabulary:**

| Term | Reading | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 関節リウマチ | かんせつリウマチ | rheumatoid arthritis |
| 生物学的製剤 | せいぶつがくてきせいざい | biological agent / biologic |
| 無作為化比較試験 | むさくいかひかくしけん | randomized controlled trial (RCT) |
| メトトレキサート | メトトレキサート | methotrexate |
| 単剤療法 | たんざいりょうほう | monotherapy |
| 効果不十分 | こうかふじゅうぶん | inadequate response |

## 13.10 Numbers and Units in Scientific Writing

Scientific texts in Japanese use a mix of Arabic numerals and kanji-based units. Knowing the conventions prevents misreading.

### Common units and abbreviations

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ○○mg/日 | milligrams per day |
| ○○mL/分 | milliliters per minute |
| ○○例 | number of cases/patients |
| ○○名 | number of persons |
| ○○倍 | times, fold |
| ○○%増加 | X% increase |
| ○○未満 | less than X |
| ○○以上 | X or above |
| ○○以下 | X or below |
| ○○を超える | exceeding X |
| ○○に達する | reaching X |
| 約○○ | approximately X |

### How to read ranges

> 体重200〜250g — "body weight 200 to 250 grams" (〜 is read as から or a pause)

> 18歳〜29歳 — "ages 18 to 29"

> p < 0.05 — "p is less than 0.05" — read as ピーちいさい（小さい）ゼロてんゼロゴ

> 95%信頼区間 — "95% confidence interval"

### Precise vs. approximate

Scientific writing distinguishes carefully between precise and approximate figures:

| Expression | Precision level |
|---|---|
| 正確に10mL | exactly 10 mL |
| 約10mL | approximately 10 mL |
| およそ10mL | roughly 10 mL |
| 10mL程度 | around 10 mL |
| 10mL前後 | in the vicinity of 10 mL |

In research papers, 約 is the standard approximation marker. In popular science, およそ and 程度 are more common.

### The telegram style of clinical notes

Clinical notes in Japanese use a distinctive telegram-like style:
- Subjects are omitted (the patient is always implied)
- Verbs are sometimes omitted: 食欲低下あり = "there is decreased appetite"
- あり/なし pattern for presence/absence: 呼吸困難なし = "no dyspnea"
- Adjective + 著明 for "markedly [adjective]": 倦怠感著明 = "marked fatigue"

This style is unique to medical records and would be ungrammatical in any other context.

| 治験 | ちけん | Clinical trial | — (medical only) |

These overlaps mean that vocabulary learned in one domain accelerates learning in another. The reader who knows 因果関係 from scientific papers will recognize it immediately in a legal document, and vice versa.
